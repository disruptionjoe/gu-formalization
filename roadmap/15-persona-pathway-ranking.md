## Scoring Method

Each pathway is scored 0-5 by each persona for **combined novelty/profundity potential**:

- 0 = no meaningful insight expected
- 1 = background or provenance only
- 2 = useful but unlikely to move the conceptual frontier
- 3 = worthwhile, with moderate insight potential
- 4 = strong candidate for novel/profound insight
- 5 = highest-upside frontier for that persona

The total is out of 75. The ranking measures upside, not immediate ease. A path can rank high while still needing scaffolding before it is safe to publish as a lead claim.

## Impact / Difficulty Lens

The raw ranking asks "where is the deepest insight?" It does not by itself answer "what can we finish quickly?" or "which high-upside tests have a reasonable chance of executing?"

For that, use a second lens:

- **Impact average:** persona total divided by 15. This approximates novelty/profundity upside on a 0-5 scale.
- **Difficulty to first bounded pass:** difficulty of finishing the first meaningful artifact, not the full research program.
  - 1 = hours; mostly extraction, formatting, or classification
  - 2 = one clear agent/contributor pass
  - 3 = several passes or one careful specialist-readable scoping artifact
  - 4 = specialist-heavy construction/scoping, meaningful but hard
  - 5 = long research program before a useful result appears
- **Difficulty to potential insight:** difficulty of getting from the first artifact to a real novel/profound insight, not just a clean note.
  - 1 = the first pass itself can produce the insight or close the path
  - 2 = one follow-up pass likely produces a decision
  - 3 = a small sequence of artifacts can produce a credible insight
  - 4 = likely needs specialist collaboration or a substantial construction
  - 5 = long-horizon research before the real payoff appears
- **First-pass leverage:** impact divided by difficulty to first bounded pass. Higher means the path is cheap to start well.
- **Insight leverage:** impact divided by difficulty to potential insight. Higher means the path has a realistic chance of paying off, not just starting cleanly.
- **Execution confidence:** chance that an agent or early contributor can finish a meaningful first artifact without needing a full research collaborator.

## Corrected Priority Interpretation

The raw totals put the specification protocol and forgetful-image thesis at the top because nearly every persona sees them as structurally important. But they are **framing pathways**, not construction pathways.

For actual research movement, the ranking should be interpreted in three tiers.

### Tier 1 - Highest Profound Upside With Concrete Mathematical Contact

1. **Type II_1 / non-embeddable spectral Standard Model**
   - Why: strongest combination of novelty, profundity, and existing mathematical machinery.
   - First test: finite Connes control checklist plus Type II_1 extension requirements.
   - Risk: failure to preserve KO-dimension, order-one, chirality, gauge extraction, or anomaly control.

2. **Observer-pairing anomaly enrichment**
   - Why: genuinely unexplored and conceptually deep.
   - First test: toy enriched bordism category with observer/reference-frame data.
   - Risk: enriched data collapses back to ordinary Freed-Hopkins input.

3. **Distler-Garibaldi stress-case functor**
   - Why: it pressure-tests whether the forgetful-image thesis handles the hardest outlier.
   - First test: try to express single-E_8 representation extraction as loss of richer compactification/enhancement data.
   - Risk: analogy breaks, revealing that the synthesis only works for anomaly-like no-gos.

### Tier 2 - Highest Gradient-Escape / Heterodox Upside

4. **Nielsen-Ninomiya protocol analogy**
   - Why: most operational heterodox bridge; assumptions already resemble system-model assumptions.
   - First test: map locality, translation invariance, Hermiticity, staticity, and reversibility to protocol assumptions.
   - Risk: analogy remains metaphorical rather than theorem-preserving.

5. **Holographic QEC / chiral MTC substrate**
   - Why: strong bridge between QFT, topology, and error-correcting substrate language.
   - First test: specify what chirality-like invariant lives in the logical algebra and what its smooth shadow is.
   - Risk: becomes AdS/CFT adjacency without Standard Model chirality contact.

6. **Higher / motivic / tmf / prismatic substrate**
   - Why: algebraic topology and higher geometry both see high-depth possibility.
   - First test: name one generalized cohomology-valued invariant and one forgetful map to ordinary smooth-bundle data.
   - Risk: too abstract to generate a falsifiable SM-facing test soon.

### Tier 3 - Scaffolding That Makes Tier 1 and 2 Non-Cranky

7. **Six-axis specification protocol**
   - Why: the best discipline mechanism.
   - First test: every candidate must fill substrate, observer, pairing, causal order, emergence, coordination loop.
   - Note: this should be required for all public open problems.

8. **No-go / forgetful-image evidence base**
   - Why: the deepest conceptual frame, but credibility depends on specific theorem-by-theorem maps.
   - First test: no-go assumption/evasion matrix plus candidate richer data and forgetful operation for each theorem.

9. **Media claim mining**
   - Why: useful for provenance, terminology, contributor onboarding, and finding overlooked source-native hooks.
   - First test: timestamped claim ledger from the transcript-rich sources.
   - Note: this is scaffolding, not proof.

## Main Recommendation

If the goal is **most likely to yield novel and profound insight**, the best sequence is:

1. **Build the no-go / forgetful-image evidence base** enough to make the frame credible.
2. **Run the Type II_1 spectral Standard Model control checklist** as the main high-upside construction path.
3. **Run the Distler-Garibaldi stress test** early, because it can falsify the overbroad version of the synthesis.
4. **Run the Nielsen-Ninomiya protocol analogy pilot** as the heterodox gradient-escape path.
5. **Scope observer-pairing Freed-Hopkins** as a profound but carefully labeled open extension.

The sharpest public framing is:

> The repo is not trying to prove GU from media claims. It is using GU-class problems to identify whether the standard no-go theorems are shadows of richer substrate-level invariants, then testing the most mathematically serious candidate paths one at a time.

## Bottom Line

The 15-persona ranking says:

- **Lead research bet:** Type II_1 / non-embeddable spectral Standard Model.
- **Deepest conceptual frame:** no-goes as forgetful images of substrate invariants.
- **Hardest falsification stress test:** Distler-Garibaldi as lossy-functor outlier.
- **Best heterodox escape hatch:** Nielsen-Ninomiya as protocol/impossibility analogy.
- **Most profound but fragile extension:** observer-pairing anomaly enrichment.
- **Most important discipline layer:** six-axis specification protocol.

Do not choose between rigor and heterodoxy. The Hegelian move is to make rigor the container that lets heterodoxy become testable.
