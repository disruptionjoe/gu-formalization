# 5-Persona Dialectic — internal origin artifact Publication Path Lock

**Status.** Local-only draft. internal origin artifact deliverable. Generated 2026-05-31.
**Scope.** Applies the persona seed from internal origin artifact (Strategic research PM thesis, Math-physics author with publication experience thesis, Reviewer antithesis, Reputation guardian antithesis, Honest-verdict gatekeeper synthesis) to the three-option decision packet.
**Convention.** `[speculation]` tags applied to venue-fit and reviewer-vector estimates that are not directly evidenced.

---

## P1 — Strategic Research PM (thesis)

**Frame.** Which option maximizes impact while controlling risk?

**(a) Leverage.** Three convergent v3 results across three independent dispatched workers landing on the same C_MPR architecture is the strongest possible structural signal Joe has so far. The convergence itself is the load-bearing finding, not any single result. The publication path should make the convergence maximally visible to readers.

**(b) Construction.** Three feasible structures:
- Option I packages the convergence as one paper's central claim.
- Option II packages the convergence as a coordinated co-release where the convergence is named in each abstract.
- Option III ships the three results independently without the convergence being load-bearing in any single paper.

**(c) Failure.** Option I's failure mode is reviewer overload: a single 30-50 page paper carrying three theorems plus a frame may be rejected for scope. Option II's failure mode is coordination delay: one paper's slow revision drags the cluster. Option III's failure mode is invisibility: the convergence is the headline finding, and three independent papers may bury it.

**(d) Named obstructions.** Phase 2 editor's "12-18 page correspondence" recommendation reflected the v1 single-paper plan; v3 results overshoot that envelope. CMP reviewers want a single load-bearing theorem, not a multi-theorem package [speculation: based on Phase 2 editor's P9 antithesis read, not directly evidenced for the v3-packaged paper(s)].

**(e) Verdict.** **Lean Option II (tight companion set).** It packages the convergence visibly (each paper's abstract names the other two as companions), it scopes each paper to a reviewer-tractable single contribution, it diversifies venue risk, and it maximizes reputation cluster effect. Option I is the high-variance "swing for the fences" play; Option III is the low-variance "diversified portfolio" play; Option II is the highest expected value across the realistic reviewer-acceptance space.

## P2 — Math-Physics Author with Publication Experience (thesis)

**Frame.** Which option is realistic for a non-academic author with no institutional backing?

**(a) Leverage.** Three concrete results with explicit theorems (signed-readout monotonicity criterion + BvN obstruction generalized + 9-tuple frame with checklist) is more than most non-academic authors land in a year. The publication pipeline should not waste this volume.

**(b) Construction.** Non-academic authors face higher reviewer skepticism in math-physics venues. Strategies that mitigate the skepticism include: (i) targeting venues with explicit cross-disciplinary tracks, (ii) co-authoring with a recognized specialist, (iii) coordinating multiple papers to demonstrate sustained research program, (iv) honest scope-statements that pre-empt reviewer skepticism.

**(c) Failure.** Option I demands a single tour-de-force paper carrying three theorems; reviewer skepticism at a top venue (CMP) toward a non-institutional author is highest here [speculation: based on general math-physics venue norms, not directly evidenced for Joe's specific submission profile in 2026]. Option II demands sustained coordination across three submissions; non-academic authors have weaker co-author networks to draw on. Option III demands per-venue style adaptation across three different communities; this is real authoring labor.

**(d) Named obstructions.** Phase 2 editor's Q3 (authorship) named co-authoring with a Lüscher-school physicist as a reviewer-trust amplifier. Under Option II, co-authoring opportunities cluster on the internal origin artifact v3 paper (the most physics-leaning of the three); the other two can ship solo. Under Option III, three independent co-author searches required.

**(e) Verdict.** **Lean Option II.** It distributes the non-academic-author risk across three papers (one paper's rejection does not block the others), it creates natural co-author opportunities on the internal origin artifact v3 anchor paper, and it lets Joe ship the internal origin artifact v3 frame paper solo to arXiv as a methodology contribution where institutional backing matters least. Option I concentrates non-academic risk on a single CMP submission; Option III spreads coordination burden too widely.

## P3 — Reviewer (antithesis)

**Frame.** Under each option, what's the most likely reviewer rejection vector?

**(a) Leverage.** All three options stand on a real corpus (three v3 theorems with explicit scope statements + Phase 1 15-persona dialectic + Phase 2 editor pass). The reviewer cannot reject for "no rigor pipeline applied."

**(b) Construction of rejection vectors per option.**

**Option I rejection vectors:**
- "Three theorems in one paper; pick one and resubmit." (Most likely vector at CMP / LMP.)
- "The 9-tuple frame is methodological and does not belong with the iff theorem and the BvN obstruction." (Scope coherence pushback.)
- "30-50 pages is too long for a single contribution." (Length pushback.)
- "The factorization architecture is presented as discovered, but it is engineered to fit the three results." (Post-hoc framing pushback.)
- "C_MPR is named in three v3 sources before being defined here; this is a research-program-internal artifact, not a publishable category." (Convergence-as-evidence pushback.)

**Option II rejection vectors (per paper):**
- internal origin artifact v3 paper: "The signed-readout monotonicity criterion is correct but obvious from PCP-blindness literature." [speculation; the criterion is in fact novel per v3 sources but reviewers may not immediately see this.] "Anomaly sub-corollary depends on a substrate the paper does not formally construct."
- internal origin artifact v3 paper: "BvN generalization is interesting but is the central claim of the paper actually established, or just suggested?" "C_MPR is constructed but stronger claims (reflectivity, free adjoint, fibration) are flagged OPEN; without those, the construction is weaker than the obstruction theorem it accompanies."
- internal origin artifact v3 paper: "Universal completeness is open; the paper publishes a frame + checklist + worked corpus, not a theorem. What is the publishable contribution?"

**Option III rejection vectors (per paper):**
- Same as Option II rejection vectors per paper, plus:
- Cross-paper convergence (C_MPR architecture across all three) is invisible to the venue reviewers; each paper must independently justify the architectural framing.
- internal origin artifact v3 frame paper in a venue without a worked-instance companion is harder to publish (Phase 2 editor's "no new TCS theorem" P10 antithesis applies).

**(c) Failure of antithesis pressure.** The strongest antithesis pressure under Option II is on the internal origin artifact v3 paper's OPEN claims (reflectivity / free adjoint / fibration); under Option III the same paper has the same pressure plus venue isolation. Option I's antithesis pressure is on scope, length, and post-hoc framing — the hardest to mitigate by writing.

**(d) Named obstructions.** internal origin artifact v3 paper, even in Option II isolation, must explicitly acknowledge the v1 / v2 / v2.1 reframing history (per Phase 1 dialectic synthesis) to pre-empt the "engineered to fit" criticism. internal origin artifact v3 paper must lead with the negative theorem (BvN generalized) as load-bearing, with the C_MPR construction framed as the corrected positive companion (per v3 reframe note §3 recommended title-shape).

**(e) Verdict.** **Lean Option II.** The per-paper rejection vectors under Option II are bounded and each paper has a credible path to acceptance via revision; Option I's scope and length rejection vectors are harder to mitigate without substantively restructuring the paper; Option III duplicates Option II's per-paper risk without the cross-citation insurance.

## P4 — Reputation Guardian (antithesis)

**Frame.** Which option carries the highest "Weinstein-adjacent speculation" framing risk?

**(a) Leverage.** The v3 results are theorem-grade or frame-grade; they do not depend on speculative GU framing. Joe's reputation case rests on the technical content, not on the broader research program. The publication path should preserve this independence.

**(b) Construction of risk profiles per option.**

**Option I risk profile.**
- One paper carries three theorems + frame; if the paper is framed as a "research program contribution," it begins to look like a position paper with theorems attached, not a theorem paper with frame attached.
- The "research program" framing is what attracts the Weinstein-adjacent speculation framing.
- A single 30-50 page paper invites the reviewer to ask "what is this paper really claiming?" and the answer "C_MPR architecture + value-lattice-distributivity wall" sounds program-level, not theorem-level.

**Option II risk profile.**
- Three coordinated papers each carry one bounded theorem; the architectural convergence is named in companion footers but is not the load-bearing claim of any single paper.
- Each paper individually reads as a theorem paper, not a position paper.
- The companion-set framing creates a "research program" signal that is internal verification (three independent dispatched workers converged), not speculative framing.

**Option III risk profile.**
- Three independent papers without cross-citation may each read as theorem papers without research-program framing.
- BUT three papers from a non-academic author in three different communities without coordinated framing may surface the "what is this person's broader research program?" question by inference; the answer (GU) is then back-channel rather than front-channel.
- Highest risk that the "hidden GU affiliation later surfaces" (Phase 2 editor T12) framing happens by inference rather than by Joe's choice.

**(c) Failure of antithesis pressure.** Option II's coordinated GU-affiliation disclosure (Q4 from Phase 2 editor pass) must be consistent across all three papers. Option III's three independent disclosure decisions create the highest risk of inconsistency surfacing as a reputational liability.

**(d) Named obstructions.** Phase 2 editor's Option B (minimal mention, "structured multi-persona dialectical review" framing) is the recommended default disclosure. Under Option II, this needs to govern all three papers; under Option III, three independent decisions must each land on Option B.

**(e) Verdict.** **Lean Option II with consistent Option-B disclosure.** The companion-set framing is the strongest defense against speculation framing because it makes the research program visible and rigor-pipeline-derived rather than back-channel-discovered. Option I concentrates the speculation-framing risk on a single paper; Option III creates the highest risk of inconsistent disclosure.

## P5 — Honest-Verdict Gatekeeper (synthesis)

**Frame.** Name the option with explicit trade-offs Joe accepts.

**(a) Leverage.** P1 (Strategic PM), P2 (Math-physics author), P3 (Reviewer), P4 (Reputation guardian) all lean Option II. The convergence is not coincidence: Option II is the option that best matches the v3 evidence shape (three convergent theorems anchored on one architecture), the venue ceiling (LMP / CMP / arXiv each accessible per-paper), the reviewer-acceptance space (bounded per-paper contributions), and the reputation strategy (visible research program via companion-set framing, not back-channel inference).

**(b) Construction of the explicit trade-offs Joe accepts under Option II.**

If Joe locks Option II, Joe accepts:

1. **Author coordination burden.** Three coordinated papers require shared definitions (especially C_MPR), shared notation (especially the 9-tuple `(T_X, C_X, U, ρ, C_U, ft, ex, rr, arch)`), shared abstracts naming the companion papers, and submission timing coordination. ~120-180 min of agent coordination overhead beyond per-paper drafting.

2. **Reputation cluster timing risk.** Three coordinated submissions need to land within a credible window (arXiv co-release within 1-2 weeks; journal submissions within ~4-8 weeks). If internal origin artifact v3 paper's LMP submission slips, the cluster effect weakens.

3. **Consistent disclosure obligation.** Phase 2 editor Q4 (GU-affiliation visibility) must be answered once and applied consistently across all three papers. Joe must commit to one disclosure level (Option B recommended) for the cluster.

4. **One paper does not carry the full architectural story.** Readers picking up internal origin artifact v3 paper alone get the iff theorem but the BvN wall (internal origin artifact v3) and the 9-tuple frame (internal origin artifact v3) are footnote-referenced, not load-bearing. The architectural unity is visible only to readers of all three.

5. **Per-paper revision burden × 3.** Each paper has its own revision cycle; three revision cycles vs one (Option I) or three decoupled (Option III).

6. **Higher total agent-time** (~1560-1980 min vs ~600-900 min for Option I), but parallelizable as 3 sub-agent workers (real-time ~600-780 min) if dispatched in parallel.

**(c) Failure of the verdict.** The verdict could fail if:
- internal origin artifact C_MPR convergence synthesis (sibling, pending) returns with a substantively different canonical definition than the v3 sources used; in that case, the companion-set framing's coordination overhead would compound. **Mitigation:** internal origin artifact packet is upstream of internal origin artifact landing; if internal origin artifact returns with a different canonical definition, internal origin artifact Phase 3 dispatch should pause for re-alignment before drafting any companion-set papers.
- The chat-agent validation brief (out-of-scope for internal origin artifact) surfaces a sleight-of-hand in the C_MPR architecture; in that case, any option that depends on C_MPR as load-bearing collapses. **Mitigation:** internal origin artifact names this as a Joe-decision remaining after lock (cross-validation against chat-agent brief outcome).
- Joe judges that the cluster-effect reputation strategy is too institutionally-coded for his non-academic positioning; in that case, Option III (independent papers across communities) becomes the alternative. **Mitigation:** P4 reputation guardian analysis above explicitly addresses this; Option II is the recommendation when cluster-effect is desired, Option III is the recommendation when independence is prioritized.

**(d) Named obstructions.** The chat-agent validation brief outcome (sibling, out-of-scope for internal origin artifact) is the load-bearing external check; the internal origin artifact C_MPR convergence synthesis landing is the load-bearing internal check. Both should resolve before Phase 3 dispatch under any option.

**(e) Verdict (synthesis).** **Option II (tight companion set of 3 papers) is the recommendation.** Joe accepts the six explicit trade-offs named in (b) above. If Joe judges any of those trade-offs unacceptable, the alternative under each:

- If trade-off #1 (coordination burden) is unacceptable: **fall back to Option I** (single integrated paper) and accept higher reviewer-overload risk in exchange for zero coordination burden.
- If trade-off #2 (cluster timing risk) is unacceptable: **fall back to Option III** (independent papers) and accept fragmentation risk in exchange for decoupled timing.
- If trade-off #3 (consistent disclosure) is unacceptable: **fall back to Option III** and accept independent disclosure decisions (and their inconsistency risk).
- If trade-off #4 (no single paper carries the full architectural story) is unacceptable: **fall back to Option I** and accept the reviewer-overload risk in exchange for a single-paper architectural story.
- If trade-off #5 (3 revision cycles) is unacceptable: **fall back to Option I** and accept the single-paper revision burden concentration.
- If trade-off #6 (higher total agent-time) is unacceptable: **fall back to Option I** if Joe prefers serial single-paper drafting, OR **fall back to Option III** if Joe prefers to ship one paper and decide on the others later.

The recommendation stands as Option II with the named fallback paths under each trade-off.

## 6. Dialectic synthesis (Hegelian shape)

**Thesis (P1, P2).** Option II maximizes impact-controlled-by-risk for both the strategic and authorial perspectives.

**Antithesis (P3, P4).** Option II's per-paper reviewer rejection vectors are bounded; Option II's consistent disclosure protects against speculation framing better than Option I (concentration risk) or Option III (inconsistency risk).

**Synthesis (P5).** Option II is the recommendation. Joe accepts six explicit trade-offs (coordination burden, cluster timing risk, consistent disclosure obligation, no-single-paper-tells-the-whole-story, ×3 revision burden, higher total agent-time but parallelizable). Six named fallback paths if any specific trade-off is unacceptable.

The dialectic does not collapse the decision into the recommendation; it surfaces the trade-offs explicitly so Joe locks Option II knowing what he accepts, or chooses Option I or Option III knowing why.
