# Example 03 — RG / Universality-Class Emergence (L5 Axis-Drop)

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| RG universality emergence | Smooth principal bundle (00d default) | Finite Turing (BPP/BQP) | Cartesian / smooth | Total-order Lorentzian | Universality class / RG fixed point | RG flow as coordination dynamics | Identify SM chirality / 3-generation structure as the value of an RG-relevant operator at a specific fixed point; if chirality is forced to be irrelevant / marginal under RG flow, the candidate is dead. |

## Leg 1 — Substrate class

- **Class label:** (a) Smooth principal bundle on a smooth manifold (00d default).
- **Specification:** Standard smooth principal `G`-bundle over `M^4`. The substrate is conventional; the candidate's heterodoxy is in L5 (and the coupled L6), not L1. The bet is that emergence-class is the load-bearing axis even when substrate is conventional.
- **Literature anchor:** Standard fiber-bundle GR/gauge-theory framework; Atiyah-Bott.
- **Class-assumption signature broken:** Preserves Witten 1981 / Distler-Garibaldi substrate assumption.

## Leg 2 — Observer class

- **Class label:** (a) Finite Turing observer (BPP / BQP).
- **Specification:** Standard finite-resource observer. The observer reads off RG-relevant operators at a fixed point via standard correlation-function measurements at the appropriate scale.
- **Literature anchor:** Standard.
- **Class-assumption signature broken:** Preserves observer-class assumption.

## Leg 3 — Pairing

- **Class label:** (a) Cartesian / smooth.
- **Specification:** Standard smooth coupling. The observer measures correlation functions; standard QFT/statistical-mechanics measurement channel.
- **Literature anchor:** Standard QFT.
- **Class-assumption signature broken:** Preserves pairing assumption.

## Leg 4 — Causal-order class

- **Class label:** (a) Total-order Lorentzian.
- **Specification:** Standard smooth Lorentzian background `(M^4, g)`. The candidate is single-axis-heterodox on L5 + coupled L6; L4 is unchanged.
- **Literature anchor:** Standard.
- **Class-assumption signature broken:** Preserves causal-order assumption.

## Leg 5 — Emergence class

- **Class label:** (b) Universality class / RG fixed point.
- **Specification:** The "substrate" relevant to SM physics is not a specific Lagrangian or bundle structure but the universality class / RG fixed point that the microscopic dynamics flow to. SM chirality, 3-generation structure, and gauge content are encoded as values of RG-relevant or marginal operators at the IR (or UV) fixed point. Different microscopic Lagrangians sharing the same fixed point produce the same SM-class observables. The "substrate" is the equivalence class under RG flow, not a specific microscopic structure.
- **Literature anchor:** Wilson, "The renormalization group: critical phenomena and the Kondo problem" (Rev. Mod. Phys. 1975); Kadanoff block-spin; Polchinski exact RG; the modern Wilsonian effective-field-theory framework.
- **Class-assumption signature broken:** Witten 1981, Freed-Hopkins, Nielsen-Ninomiya, and Distler-Garibaldi all assume specific-object substrate (a particular Lagrangian, bundle, or representation). RG-universality reframes the question: the no-go theorems compute obstructions to a specific microscopic realization, but SM-class observables depend only on the fixed point. The obstruction may exist for all microscopic representatives while the fixed point still hosts the SM content as an RG-relevant operator.

## Leg 6 — Coordination-loop class

- **Class label:** (g) Stochastic gradient / RG flow as coordination dynamics.
- **Specification:** The substrate-observer coupling is mediated by RG flow itself: the observer's effective theory at scale `μ` is the result of integrating out modes above `μ`, and this is a coordination loop in the sense that the observed effective dynamics back-react on the relevant operator content. L6 is coupled to L5; you cannot have universality-class emergence without RG flow as the coordination dynamics that defines the equivalence class.
- **Literature anchor:** Wilson-Polchinski exact RG; Wegner-Houghton equations.
- **Class-assumption signature broken:** Breaks "no loop" assumption of standard no-go theorems. RG flow as coordination dynamics is implicit in modern QFT but is not part of the formal frame Witten / Freed-Hopkins / Distler-Garibaldi work within.

## Chirality bridge claim

The substrate-level invariant carrying chirality is a **specific RG-relevant or marginal operator at a fixed point** whose presence forces the IR effective theory to contain SM chirality and 3-generation structure. The forgetful operation is the standard reduction of "fixed point with RG-relevant operator content" to "specific microscopic Lagrangian L on a specific bundle B," which is the level at which Witten 1981 / Freed-Hopkins / Distler-Garibaldi compute obstructions. The substrate-level claim is that the obstruction theorems compute null images on the microscopic representative even when the fixed-point operator content is non-null, because they cannot see RG-equivalence-class data — they work at a specific representative. The chirality-bearing data is the RG-relevant-operator content, not any microscopic Lagrangian.

## One first falsification test

**Test:** Identify a candidate RG-relevant or marginal operator at a candidate fixed point such that:

1. The operator's RG dimension is relevant or marginal (i.e., it does not get scaled away in IR flow).
2. The operator's presence forces SM-class chirality / 3-generation / gauge content in the IR effective theory.
3. The operator is RG-equivalence-class data — it depends on the fixed point, not on any specific microscopic representative.
4. Standard no-go theorems applied to specific microscopic Lagrangians in the universality class compute null obstructions consistent with the operator being invisible to specific-representative analysis.

**Failure modes that kill the candidate:**

- Chirality is forced to be an *irrelevant* operator that flows away under RG; SM chirality would then be a tuning, not an emergent universality-class feature.
- Every operator that could carry SM-chirality content turns out to be specific-representative data (cannot be defined on the equivalence class); the RG-universality reframe collapses to specific-substrate.
- No fixed point compatible with the candidate operator content is known; the candidate is non-constructive.

**Runner:** RG / Wilsonian-EFT specialist, with cross-check from a phenomenologist familiar with SM-fixed-point candidates. Agent pass can identify the candidate operators in existing literature and check failure mode 1; failure modes 2 and 3 require specialist judgment about what counts as equivalence-class data.

**Why this test is load-bearing:** RG-universality emergence is only interesting if SM chirality is RG-relevant or marginal. If chirality is irrelevant under RG (the most likely outcome given that chirality is a discrete property and RG flow typically does not preserve discrete properties without explicit symmetry protection), the candidate cannot host the SM content as fixed-point data. The candidate then collapses to "you still need a specific microscopic theory," and the no-go theorems retain their bite at that level.
