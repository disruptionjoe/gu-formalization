## Corrected Theorem Spine

### Definition 1: Evidence Monoid

Let `E` be the free commutative monoid generated by local contribution events.
Equip `E` with the information order:

```text
e <= e' iff e' = e + d for some d in E
```

This says that new evidence has arrived.

### Definition 2: Signed Readout

Let `G` be an ordered abelian group with positive cone `G_+`.

Let each generator `x` have weight:

```text
w(x) in G
```

Define the scalar readout:

```text
R(sum_i x_i) = sum_i w(x_i)
```

### Theorem: Signed Readout Monotonicity Criterion

`R : E -> G` is monotone iff every generator has nonnegative weight:

```text
R monotone iff w(x) in G_+ for every generator x
```

### Proof Sketch

If every generator is nonnegative, then adding evidence adds an element of
`G_+`, so the output cannot decrease.

If some generator has negative weight, take `e = 0` and `e' = x`. Then
`e <= e'`, but `R(e')` is not greater than or equal to `R(e)`. Monotonicity
fails.

This is the theorem spine. Anomaly becomes an application, not the iff
condition.

## Why The Anomaly-Iff Fails

### 1. No Anomaly Is Not Sufficient

An anomaly-free observable can still be non-monotone if its scalar readout has
negative generators.

Examples:

- Non-abelian vector charges with traceless generators.
- U(1)-style charges when the modeled physical state space includes both
  positive and negative charge sectors.
- Any signed conserved quantity whose readout is a difference, not a count.

So:

```text
anomaly-free -> CALM-monotone
```

is false unless the theorem adds the stronger condition that all allowed
generators are nonnegative in the chosen output order.

### 2. Anomaly Is Not Necessary

Signed cancellation can occur without an anomaly. Signed measures, phase-
sensitive amplitudes, positive-minus-negative variation, and other readouts can
all be non-monotone without being anomaly observables.

So:

```text
non-monotone -> anomalous
```

is also false.

### 3. Anomaly Does Not Block Monotone Provenance

Even an anomaly/index-bearing observable can have a monotone provenance
representation:

```text
E -> E_+ x E_- -> G
```

The first layer accumulates positive and negative evidence monotonically. The
final readout subtracts:

```text
read(P, N) = P - N
```

The provenance layer can be CALM-like. The scalar readout is not.

## Wolfram / Local-Rule Guardrail

CALM is a special case of local-rule systems:

```text

```

broader class:

```text

```

Therefore failure of CALM monotonicity does not imply failure of local-rule
renderability. It only says the global scalar cannot be shortcut as a monotone
query.
