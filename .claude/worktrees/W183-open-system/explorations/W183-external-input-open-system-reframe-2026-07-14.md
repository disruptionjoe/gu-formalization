## 1. Persona 1 -- open-quantum-systems theorist: the system, the environment, the boundary, the channel

**The formalization.** Cast GU as a Friedrichs / Fano-Anderson open system. The **sub-system** is the
geometry: one discrete record mode (the ghost / mirror, W173) at energy `M`. The **environment** is
the source: a continuum reservoir (issuance; the everpresent-Lambda bath; the records fed through the
frontier). The **system-environment cut** is the q=5 finality frontier (W150). The **open channel** is
the coupling `V` between the ghost and the reservoir; the **decay rate** (finite ghost width, the
physical-sheet pole's imaginary part) is the ISSUANCE RATE of records into finality.

**Why the closed grading is EXPECTED to fail.** For a discrete state coupled to a continuum, the
reduced dynamics is governed by the Feshbach effective sub-Hamiltonian `H_eff(s) = M + Sigma(s)`, an
energy-dependent, non-Hermitian operator. Its eigenvalue is complex (the state acquires a width); the
sub-system evolution is not unitary. This is the textbook content of open-system theory: the reduced
map is CONTRACTIVE for a genuine sink (probability leaks OUT to the environment), and the TOTAL
(system + environment) is unitary with real spectrum. **PC1 in the test is exactly this baseline**: a
normal Fano model with a UNITARY total (real spectrum, `||U psi|| = 1`) and a SUB-UNITARY sub-system
(survival amplitude `|a_d(t)|` decaying to 0.58). So "the closed sub-system is not unitary" is not a
verdict against the theory; it is the definition of an open system.

## 2. Persona 2 -- Krein/PT specialist: the anti-damping sign is the GAIN signature, and reuses W132

The one worry an open-system reframe must clear: dissipation gives a CONTRACTION (`S^dag S <= 1`),
but the GU leak is an EXPANSION. **This is not a problem for the reframe; it is its strongest
support, and W132 already did the work.** W132 proved, exactly and all-orders, that for any
Krein-pseudo-unitary `S` the physical-subspace map obeys `A^dag A = P_+ + B^dag B`, so
`||A psi||^2 = ||psi||^2 + ||B psi||^2 >= ||psi||^2`: probability EXCESS, and W132 used precisely
this sign to **refute the ghost-as-decay-width reading** (dissipation contracts; this anti-damps).

Read through the open-system lens the sign inverts its meaning: an open system coupled to a **source**
(a reservoir feeding probability IN, not a sink draining it OUT) shows exactly a GAIN on the
sub-system, and the excess `||B psi||^2` is exactly what crossed the boundary from the source. **PC2
in the test** reproduces the identity (residual 1e-10) and exhibits the excess as inflow:
`||A psi||^2 - 1 = ||B psi||^2 > 0`, the probability that entered across the system-environment cut =
the issuance. So W132's sign, which killed the "ghost drains probability" reading, is the positive
evidence for "the source feeds probability" reading. The anti-damping is the face of a source, not a
sink; the physical-sheet pole (W178) is the reduced-resolvent signature of that gain.

## 3. Persona 3 -- firewall-boundary / finality specialist: the reduced pole is not the total eigenvalue

**The decisive distinction the closed sprint could not see.** W178 computed the pole of the dressed
ghost propagator `D(s)` and found it on the physical sheet, reading "physical-sheet pole = complex
energy = broken PT = NOT-OPERATIVE." But `D(s)` is the REDUCED (sub-system) resolvent. A
physical-sheet pole of the reduced resolvent is **not** the same object as a complex EIGENVALUE of the
TOTAL generator. In the normal Fano baseline (PC1) the reduced resolvent has a resonance pole (on the
second sheet) while the TOTAL spectrum is perfectly real and the total is unitary: the resonance is a
feature of the reduced dynamics analytically continued, not a genuine complex eigenvalue. **Opening the
system is exactly what separates these two objects**, and W178, computing a closed grading, never
separated them.

**The model (Persona 4 built it; here is what it decides).** Take the ghost (Krein sign `-1`) coupled
to a reservoir, K-pseudo-Hermitian throughout, and vary the Krein type of the reservoir the ghost
issues into:

- **Like-Krein-signed reservoir** (records issued into like-signed finality). The coupling within a
  same-sign sector is ordinary symmetric, so the total, restricted to that sector, is genuinely
  Hermitian up to an overall metric sign: the **TOTAL spectrum is REAL at every coupling** (test G2a,
  to 1e-8 at `g` up to 5). Yet the reduced ghost propagator still carries a physical-sheet pole (the
  gain, G1). This is the ISSUANCE-CHANNEL regime: **G3** builds the positive total metric
  `eta_+ = (R R^dag)^{-1}` (min eig 1.0) and verifies the TOTAL evolution is `eta_+`-unitary
  (`U^dag eta_+ U = eta_+`, residual 1.1e-15) WHILE the sub-system amplitude is non-unitary. One
  model, simultaneously: **total unitary, sub-system carrying the closed NOT-OPERATIVE signature**.
- **Opposite-Krein-signed reservoir** (ghost overlapping opposite-type continuum). K-Hermiticity forces
  an antisymmetric coupling; the reduced self-energy flips sign (anti-damping, `Im Sigma(M) > 0`, G1b),
  the reduced pole is on the physical sheet (G1c, argument principle: exactly 1, vs 0 for the normal
  control G1d, reproducing W178 Model B), AND opposite-Krein-type eigenvalues collide: the **TOTAL
  develops a complex-conjugate pair above an exceptional point `g_c = 0.392`** (G2b-d). No positive
  total metric; genuinely NOT unitary. This is the GENUINE-PATHOLOGY regime.

So the finality-frontier reading is sharp: whether the closed physical-sheet pole is a benign issuance
channel or a genuine instability is decided by the **Krein type of the reservoir the ghost issues into**
