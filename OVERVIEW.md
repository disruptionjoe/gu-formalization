# Five-Minute Research Map

This repository is a first-principles research map, not a proof of Geometric Unity.

## Central Hypothesis

The working hypothesis is that several chirality/anomaly no-go theorem families are class-relative impossibility results. They may compute what richer substrate-level invariants look like after projection into ordinary smooth, local, or classical observer-frame categories.

The hypothesis is not established. The repo is designed so a clean negative result is still a success.

## What Is Canonical Here?

The canon is deliberately modest:

- The public posture is research-map-first, not proof-first.
- No-go results must be handled by their exact assumptions and known class exits.
- Candidate substrate programs must be specified before they are defended.
- Type II1 spectral Standard Model work must preserve the finite Connes control case.

See `CANON.md` and `canon/`.

## What Is Active Research?

The most important active theorem lane is the signed-readout boundary:

> monotone provenance can coexist with non-monotone, signed, phase-sensitive, or cancellation-bearing readout.

This reframes the CALM/Ginsparg-Wilson bridge as a boundary theorem rather than a direct equivalence. See `active-research/`.

## What Is Exploratory?

C_MPR, the 9-tuple, PCP-blindness, BvN/Classical-Value-Lattice Wall, stochastic, causal-set, RG/universality, computational-irreducibility, and higher-categorical branches are available under `explorations/`. They are valuable, but they are not canon.
