The remaining math does not look like one missing calculation. It looks like a
terrain classification problem. The live walls appear to sit on at least eight
different mathematical terrains:

| terrain | guiding question | current GU relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Smooth local terrain | Does an ordinary PDE/symbol/action calculation close? | VZ principal/subprincipal checks, GR recovery, IG variation. |
| Noncompact/end terrain | What data lives at infinity or on a boundary correction? | `Y^14 -> K3`, APS, eta, spectral flow, generation count. |

## 4. Lens Steelmen

### 4.1 Fourier / Phase Topography

**Orthodox steelman.** Fourier and phase methods identify spectral terrain only
after a named operator, state, trace, or group action exists.

**Heterodox steelman.** [speculation] Some GU invariants may be phase-coded:
locally invisible in smooth 4D, but visible as eigenphase, spectral flow, or
phase-gap data.

**Topographic output.** Search for the operator whose spectrum matters before
choosing the transform. Candidate surfaces: `Pi_RS * E_+ * Pi_RS`, VZ mixed
covector families, source branch orbits, and Type II_1 spectral projections.

### 4.2 Hidden-Subgroup / Period Topography

**Orthodox steelman.** A hidden subgroup exists only if there is a source-defined
action and oracle whose level sets are genuine proof-equivalence classes.

**Heterodox steelman.** [speculation] The missing period may be in branch
provenance, section choice, or source-to-shadow quotient, not spacetime.

**Topographic output.** Map branch space as orbits, stabilizers, quotients, and
forbidden downstream labels. This is primarily a QFT branch-row and selector lens.

### 4.3 Heavy-Tail / Power-Law Topography

**Orthodox steelman.** Heavy tails matter only if a named residual or spectral
measure lacks finite variance or Gaussian decay in a stable, invariant way.

**Heterodox steelman.** [speculation] The math may fail under Gaussian smoothing
because source-to-shadow projection produces stable-law, resonance, or Levy-like
tails.

**Topographic output.** For each residual, record whether it is Gaussian-like,
exponential, power-law, intermittent, resonance-like, or undefined. Do this
before deciding whether Fourier or wavelet methods are appropriate.

### 4.4 Chaos-Shadow Topography

**Orthodox steelman.** Chaos diagnostics are stability and information-loss
tests, not physical proofs.

**Heterodox steelman.** [speculation] Two smooth 4D shadows may be identical
while differing in source-level OTOC, Lyapunov, or operator-growth signatures.

**Topographic output.** Add a dynamical-shadow axis to source-to-shadow maps:
what operator information is preserved, scrambled, decohered, or erased?

### 4.5 Multifractal / Wavelet Topography

**Orthodox steelman.** Wavelets and multifractals locate scale-dependent
regularity, singular support, and localized residual structure.

**Heterodox steelman.** [speculation] The hidden math may be sparse or
multifractal rather than smooth-small.

**Topographic output.** For VZ, theta residuals, DG shadow loss, and anomaly
density, ask whether the object is scale-stable, scale-dependent, or
coarse-graining artifact.

### 4.6 QEC / Syndrome Topography

**Orthodox steelman.** A code interpretation requires carrier, checks, syndrome,
logical algebra, and recovery.

**Heterodox steelman.** [speculation] A 4D smooth failure may be a decoder
failure: the projection discarded syndrome data needed for the invariant.

**Topographic output.** For each wall, classify whether missing data looks like:
constraint violation, gauge quotient, lost syndrome, unrecoverable erasure, or
target import.

### 4.7 Tensor / Holographic Topography

**Orthodox steelman.** Tensor/holographic language is useful only when it names a
reconstruction map, code subspace, cut, and logical operator.

**Heterodox steelman.** [speculation] The source-to-shadow relation may be

**Topographic output.** Determine whether each live wall asks for local
calculation, boundary reconstruction, bulk logical algebra, or recoverability
across a cut.

### 4.8 ZK Circuit Topography

**Orthodox steelman.** ZK exposes finite witness shape: public inputs, private

**Heterodox steelman.** [speculation] If the terrain is too large to inspect

**Topographic output.** Use circuit shape as a diagnostic: if no finite witness

### 4.9 FHE / Public-Private Topography

## 6. Hegelian Analysis

### Thesis

The orthodox thesis is that the remaining GU walls are ordinary mathematical
obligations: write the operator, action, symbol, index problem, selector,
state-preparation map, or provenance relation. Until those objects are typed,
topographic lenses can only diagnose.

### Antithesis

The heterodox antithesis is that the objects may not live in the ordinary smooth
4D/Gaussian/L2 terrain the current formulation keeps falling back to. The
missing math may live in end data, spectral measures, power-law tails, singular
sets, scale filtrations, operator growth, code subspaces, descent obstructions,
transport losses, or observer-internal truth.

### Incompatibility

If the antithesis is used too early, it becomes analogy inflation. If the thesis
is used too narrowly, it forces every wall into smooth local analysis and may
miss the terrain where the actual obstruction lives.

### Aufhebung

The synthesis is a two-step discipline:

```text
first: terrain classification
then: proof object or certificate
```

This preserves the orthodox demand for exact objects while preserving the
heterodox insight that the exact object may be in the wrong terrain. The shared
premise negated is that the next move must immediately be either a standard
smooth proof or a quantum/crypto certificate. The relocated object is the
terrain map: before solving a wall, classify the mathematical landscape where
the wall lives.
