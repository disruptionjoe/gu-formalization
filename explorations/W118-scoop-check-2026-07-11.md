## Adjacent works and deltas

1. **Breuer (1995), "The Impossibility of Accurate State Self-Measurements," Phil. Sci. 62(2):197-214.**
   Diagonal-flavored proof that no internal observer can distinguish all states of a system
   containing it (classical or quantum, deterministic or stochastic). This is prior art for the
   *observer reading of Part I-a only*. It does NOT subsume the theorem: no grading involution, no
   invariant-valuation no-go (I-b), no arena/value partition (II), no symmetry-breaking conclusion.
2. **Szangolies (2018), "Epistemic Horizons and the Foundations of Quantum Mechanics," arXiv:1805.10668** (also arXiv:2007.14909).
   Applies Lawvere's fixed-point theorem to measurement/observers to derive quantum "epistemic
   horizons." Closest Lawvere-plus-observer prior art. No involution on the value object, no
   invariant-valuation leg, no partition.
3. **Lawrence (2026), "The No Barber Principle: Towards Formalised Selection in the Inaccessible Game," arXiv:2604.21945.**
   Uses Lawvere diagonalisation (via Russell's paradox) to constrain *selection* in a
   physics-adjacent information-theoretic system; argues FinProb's cartesian copying makes
   Lawvere-style constructions expressible. Adjacent on Lawvere-plus-selection; no fixpoint-free
   involution, no invariant-valuation no-go, no arena/value partition. Recent (Apr 2026) and
   thematically close enough that a citation is prudent.
4. **Equivariant-obstruction framing of Lemma C.** Lemma C ("a nonempty domain cannot map into an
   empty fixed-set") is exactly the trivial base case of the classical equivariant statement: an
   alpha-invariant valuation p : A -> B is a Z/2-equivariant map from A (trivial action) into B
   (free action), and equivariance from a trivially-acted domain forces the image into
   B^{Z/2} = emptyset. The classical statement is standard in equivariant obstruction theory
   (Borsuk-Ulam-adjacent), but no paper was found stating it as a valuations/physics no-go. The
   paper should own this framing explicitly rather than let a topologist point it out.

## Not-adjacent (checked and dismissed)

- Kochen-Specker / "value no-go" hidden-variable theorems (arXiv 1707.07368): "valuation" there is
  a hidden-variable assignment obstructed by contextuality, not by a grading involution; different
  machinery, different claim. The paper's existing scope statement covers this.
- Brukner-style "no-go for observer-independent facts" (Wigner's-friend family): Bell-inequality
  machinery, not fixed-point machinery.
- Frumin-Massas, Kuang, and the Lawvere survey (arXiv 2503.13536): expository unifications of
  diagonal arguments; none adds the involution/invariance/partition legs.
- Categorical cybernetics / open games / dialectica: Lawvere-style arguments present, but graded
  by payoff/lens structure, not by an involutive admissibility grading; no invariant-valuation
  no-go found.
- Ben-Ya'acov arXiv:2007.13036: conceptual Godel-analogy essay, no formal machinery.
