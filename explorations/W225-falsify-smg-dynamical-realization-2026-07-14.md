## 1. Persona 1 -- SMG / lattice-chiral-fermion specialist: EXISTENCE is guaranteed, REALIZATION is not

The decisive existence result is Wang-Wen (Phys. Rev. Research 2, 023356 (2020), arXiv:1809.11171):
because the `16` of `Spin(10)` is anomaly-free including the mod-16 cobordism anomaly (`16 = 0
mod 16`), it **admits a symmetric gapped boundary for the mirror sector** on a trivial 5d symmetric
invertible topological order. Concretely, the SM / SO(10) `16n` chiral fermion theory can be defined
non-perturbatively on a 3+1D lattice of bosons/qubits precisely because the mirror can be gapped
symmetrically. So the symmetric gapped phase is **guaranteed to EXIST**. **F1 cannot fire for the
`16`.** This is the same load-bearing datum as W224: the `nu_R` completion is exactly what makes the
mod-16 anomaly vanish, and it is what makes the symmetric gapped phase exist. Teeth: remove `nu_R`
(the `15`), and the mod-16 anomaly is `15 != 0`, the symmetric gapped boundary does NOT exist, and
F1 fires (verdict FALSIFIED). The test's CONTROL 1a exhibits this.

But **existence of a phase is not the same as the strong-coupling dynamics flowing to it.** The
existence proof is a cobordism / boundary statement; it constructs an interaction that gaps the mirror
symmetrically, but it does not certify that GU's OWN interaction lands in that basin rather than a
competing one. This is the entire gap between necessary and sufficient, and it is where the original
Eichten-Preskill program died: the anomaly-free mirror was there, the symmetric gapped phase existed
in principle, but the strong-coupling dynamics they wrote flowed to a symmetry-breaking phase (the
mirror stayed light or the gauge symmetry broke). The realization is a dynamical question, and for the
EXACT `16` in 3+1D it is not settled.

## 2. Persona 2 -- condensed-matter / deconfined-criticality specialist: the transition and the intermediate-phase risk

The modern understanding of the SMG transition (You-BenTov-Xu / Wang-You, Phys. Rev. X 8, 011026
(2018), arXiv:1705.09313) is that it is a **single continuous transition beyond the Landau paradigm**,
reminiscent of deconfined criticality, with emergent non-Abelian gauge fields coupled to Dirac
fermions and critical Higgs bosons. The key risk this framework identifies is an **intermediate
gapless phase**: if an extended deconfined phase intervenes, one gets a gapless "Bose semimetal"
between the massless Dirac phase and the symmetric gapped phase, which would obstruct a clean flow to
the gap. The reassuring fact is empirical: across several microscopic models and numerical methods,
the transition has been observed to be a **single direct continuous transition with no intermediate
gapless phase**. So the DQC-intermediate-gapless obstruction is **not generically forced**.

However -- and this is the honest cut -- the models where this is cleanly established are the
**smaller anomaly-free ones**: 8 Dirac fermions in 2+1D, the Fidkowski-Kitaev Z/8 chain in 1+1D, the
3-4-5-0 model in 1+1D, and Catterall's 3+1D SU(2)xSU(2) bifundamental reduced staggered fermions
(arXiv:2111.01001), where the fermions demonstrably gap via a four-fermion condensate WITHOUT a
symmetry-breaking bilinear. For the **exact SO(10) `16` in 3+1D**, the SMG review literature records
that "whether 16 Weyl fermions can be gapped to produce a featureless state through a single
continuous transition remains to be seen, with numerics on one microscopic model appearing to give an
intervening symmetry-breaking phase." So the **Eichten-Preskill failure mode is LIVE** for the exact
`16`: it is neither excluded nor confirmed. This is the crux of the OPEN verdict.

## 3. Persona 3 -- higher-symmetry / non-invertible-anomaly specialist: the beyond-anomaly obstructions do NOT fire for the standard 16

This is where a beyond-anomaly obstruction could have killed GU even with all 't Hooft anomalies zero.

- **The `w2 w3` mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly.** This is a mod-2 nonperturbative GLOBAL anomaly

  trivially and featurelessly disordered without either ground-state degeneracy (topological order) or
  symmetry breaking. If GU's `16` carried it, the "symmetric gapped phase" would be forced to be
  either gapless or topologically ordered, sharpening the falsification. **But the STANDARD `Spin(10)`
  GUT with the `16` does NOT have the `w2 w3` anomaly.** Only a **modified** SO(10) with an extra
  discrete torsion / Wess-Zumino-Witten class carries it. GU's carrier is the plain `Spin(10)` `16`

  test's CONTROL 3 gives the detector teeth: it FIRES on the modified SO(10)+WZW content and is ABSENT
  on the standard `16`.

- **The categorical / non-invertible higher symmetry.** Gauging the `Z_2` flip between Georgi-Glashow
  and flipped-`u(5)` produces a potential non-invertible global symmetry (a split fusion rule of a 2d
  topological surface operator). If this were an unavoidable feature of GU's UV, it could obstruct a
  trivial gap. **But the un-Higgsed `Spin(10)` at UV RETRACTS this categorical symmetry** (Wang
  2111.10369). GU's UV is exactly the un-Higgsed `Spin(10)`, so the categorical symmetry is retracted
  and does not obstruct.

- **The forced deconfined-quantum-criticality gapless region.** There IS a gapless 4d deconfined
  quantum critical region -- but it sits **between the gauged GUT vacua** (Georgi-Glashow vs
  Pati-Salam), as a phase-diagram feature of the DYNAMICALLY GAUGED theory tuned between two
  symmetry-breaking orders. It is **not** a forced obstruction to the GLOBAL-symmetric **mirror-gap**
  problem, which is what SMG requires (the mirror is gapped as a global-symmetry-preserving boundary,
  not by tuning between gauged vacua). So the DQC gaplessness is a real and beautiful phenomenon of the
  gauged GUT landscape, but it does not fire as F2 for the mirror gap.

**Net for Persona 3: F2 does NOT trigger for GU's standard `Spin(10)` `16`.** No higher-group,
