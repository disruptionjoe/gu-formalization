# Pre-registration: generation-mechanism collision

## Objective

Test whether three author-stated accounts of the generation count are layers
of one natural GU construction or mutually unintegrated mechanisms:

1. extension of the inhomogeneous gauge group by supercharges fixes three;
2. the exact Clifford decomposition
   \(\Omega^0\otimes S\oplus\Omega^1\otimes S
   \cong S\oplus\operatorname{im}\Gamma\oplus\ker\Gamma\), with the
   third-generation piece assigned to \(\ker\Gamma\); and
3. the rolled \(0\to1\to13\to14\) complex leads to three generations.

The first pass must try the strongest construction and the strongest
obstruction independently. It may not identify repeated uses of the word
"three" without an explicit typed map.

## Corrected source-object slot

B.5/I6 is frozen for this run as an **unnormalized \(K_S\)-linear
conditional/post-selection-shaped pairing**. Its natural per-column and
trace normalizers vanish identically. The run must not demand or claim a
normalized weak value, and must not transfer Steinberg's experimental
literature as evidence for a GU observable.

## Frozen tests

A successful bridge must supply, without an unpriced choice:

- explicit maps among all three mechanisms;
- compatibility with the manuscript field content and the verified
  \(\Gamma\) contraction;
- a physical-generation readout rather than an internal multiplicity;
- reconciliation with the three equal 64-dimensional Krein weight copies;
- the external \(S_3\) label or a proof that those copies are not the three
  physical generations;
- B.5 Gate 1 (commutant reach), Gate 2 (habitat nondegeneracy), and class
  stability; and
- provenance separation among manuscript content, podcast labeling,
  repo reconstruction, and repo-originated predictions.

The following do not count as a bridge: choosing an \(SU(2)_+\) axis,
selecting one quotient or subrepresentation by hand, importing a target
generation label, or citing the unreleased two-connection complex without a
defined differential.

## Predeclared outcomes

- **GEN-BRIDGE:** one natural diagram supplies all required maps and gates.
- **GEN-INCOMPATIBLE:** an exact representation, character, dimension, or
  naturality obstruction proves the mechanisms cannot all have the claimed
  physical reading.
- **GEN-UNDERDEFINED:** a consistent scaffold exists, but the physical
  readout depends on a missing source-action/shiab/middle-map datum. The
  result must name the smallest such datum.

A result may be `GEN-INCOMPATIBLE` for a particular dictionary while leaving
the three algebraic objects individually valid. No branch may inflate that
into a global GU verdict.

## Team and cross-examination contract

The first wave has three independent read-only branches:

1. constructive GU geometer/representation/BV steelman;
2. hostile representation/category/physics/provenance referee; and
3. closed-operator/Krein/boundary-domain audit of the independent continuum
   pencil front.

The orchestrator will collide the first two reports, return each strongest
claim to the other for one bounded response, and adjudicate from typed
receipts rather than majority vote. A fresh verifier in the second wave must
attack the provisional generation outcome. The other second-wave branches
are the exact spectral-pairing involution and the four-metric enumeration.

Local heavy computation remains serialized. No claim, canon, scientific
status, or public posture moves from this exploration-tier run.
