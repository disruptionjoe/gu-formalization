## 2. Persona 2 (Cartan-split specialist) -- why the stabilizer is compact, and the dimension jump

The load-bearing new computation. W203's nulldim-1 used the FULL non-compact gauge group `so(9,5)`.
But the good-stable STABILIZER is smaller: a deformation that preserves the positive-definite total
metric `eta_+` must be COMPACT, and preserving BOTH `eta` (the Krein form) and `eta_+` (positive
definite) is exactly the maximal compact subgroup `SO(9) x SO(5)` -- the Cartan `k` part. The test
verifies the split directly: the compact `so(9) (+) so(5)` generators preserve both `eta` and `eta_+`
(residual 0, `STAB1a/b`), while the non-compact boosts preserve `eta` but BREAK `eta_+` (`STAB1c`). So
the non-compact boosts are NOT in the stabilizer; the stabilizer is the maximal compact.

Now count invariant symmetric forms under that compact stabilizer (`STAB2`): the space is EXACTLY
**2-DIMENSIONAL**, spanned by the two block forms `{P_9, P_5}` (the form on the 9-block and the form on
the 5-block separately, each an `SO(9)`- resp. `SO(5)`-Schur invariant; the `9 (x) 5` off-block carries
no invariant). Both `eta = P_9 - P_5` (indefinite) and `eta_+ = P_9 + P_5` (positive definite) live in
this space (`STAB3a/b/c`). **The grading sign -- the relative sign of the two blocks -- is a FREE
coordinate** (`STAB4`). So conditioning on good-stable does NOT pin the grading; it ENLARGES the
invariant space from 1 (full non-compact) to 2 (compact stabilizer). `dim = 2 > 1` =>
