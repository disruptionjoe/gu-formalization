# Candidate Form C: enumeration-exhaustiveness / hidden universal

The class-no-go council audit (2026-07-10) confirmed the two known pathology forms and found no third
among the audited walls. But our concurrent KO-ladder work surfaced a third form the audit could NOT
have caught by construction -- because it is not about the wrong object (Form A) or a bad diagnostic
(Form B), but about the LOGICAL FORM of the closure itself.

## The form

> **Form C -- enumeration-exhaustiveness / hidden universal.** A wall is asserted as a UNIVERSAL
> ("*no* obstruction can X" / "*every* candidate is Y"), but the proof covers only an ENUMERATED set
> of cases. Completeness-by-type -- that the enumeration exhausts the possibilities -- is an unproven
> premise. An un-enumerated case of a different type could break the universal.

## Why it is distinct (and more dangerous than A or B)

- Form A (homonymy) fails the Layer-0 semantic check: a key TERM denotes a different object.
- Form B (diagnostic artifact) fails a statistical check: a screen rejects genuine target-class
  examples.
- **Form C passes BOTH.** Every enumerated case is the right object (no homonymy) and every closure is
  a genuine proof (no artifact). The pathology is that the UNIVERSAL QUANTIFIER outruns the proof: the
  theorem is correct for its list and mis-stated as general. It survives Layer-0 and the diagnostic
  check precisely because each listed rung is honest -- only the "for all" is unearned.

## The check (if adopted)

For a wall phrased as a universal: (1) Is the proof a genuine argument over ALL objects of the
relevant type, or an enumeration of instances? (2) If an enumeration: is completeness-by-type PROVEN
(a classifying argument -- "every obstruction of this sector factors through structure S") or ASSUMED?
(3) Name one un-enumerated candidate of a DIFFERENT type and check whether it escapes. If it can, the
universal is Form-C-flagged; downgrade the claim from "general theorem" to "theorem over the
enumeration," and the completeness premise becomes the load-bearing open sub-task.

## The receipt (and the live test)

`canon/ko-degree-obstruction-ladder-RESULTS.md`: "located, not forced" was upgraded from empirical to
5-of-7-rungs structural, BUT the universal reading ("no obstruction can carry odd torsion") rides the
pin-spin completeness premise ("every GU generation-sector obstruction IS a KO/Spin/Pin invariant").
That premise is exactly a Form-C hidden universal: the enumerated obstructions are all 2-primary, but
a `tmf`-type (3-primary torsion) obstruction of a non-KO/Spin/Pin type would escape. **The
exhaustiveness swing running now (tmf/string + triality-Z/3) is Form C's own live test:** if it CLOSES
the premise (a classifying argument that no non-KO/Spin/Pin obstruction can act on the sector), Form C
was a phantom for this result and located-not-forced becomes general; if it finds the escape, Form C
just proved itself on the program's most robust finding.

## Disposition (HELD)

Do NOT ratify Form C into the Layer-0/no-go protocol, and do NOT cascade it into the template /
no-go-map / white paper, until the exhaustiveness swing lands. Rationale: a pathology form earns its
slot only if it has teeth on a real case; the swing is that case, in flight. If the swing shows the
premise is genuinely open (or finds an escape), Form C is earned and this proposal becomes the
ratification basis -- a precondition-companion to Layer-0, run on any wall phrased as a universal. If
the swing closes the premise cleanly, Form C stays a documented-but-unratified observation. Either
way: proposal-tier now; no protocol, canon, or verdict movement.
