# Mirror-sector candidate does not freeze

## Bounded question

Do the completed weld probes make the charged vectorlike mirror-sector
candidate admissible under the Prediction Package Standing Rule?

They do not. The candidate still lacks a derivable physical observable with a
typed event class and kill threshold. This is a packet-admission disposition,
not a change to any mathematical result or physical verdict.

## Probe readout

### W-A: condensate versus the curvature-locked 54

`P-54-WELD` closes the direct/native identification by representation type:
the native distortion is valued in a `10`, while the conventional breaking
order parameter is a `54`. A traceless quadratic composite can be written, but
the current source action does not select it and no nonzero/stabilizer,
normalization, or real-form/observer reduction has been proved. The proposed
gapping condensate therefore is not yet a source-selected GU-native order
parameter.

### W-B: sign bit versus payload orientation

The orientation sign is not a mass-texture knob. The mirror-sector table's M4
result gives the identical mass multiset in both orientations. Separately, Q2
leaves `sigma` free and internally unreadable; it does not prove that `sigma`
is the M4 sign bit. Even under that proposed identification, spectral silence
would supply no mirror mass window, charge pattern, or other discriminator.

## Decisive blocker

The candidate's proposed collider observable requires the K-negative 96 modes
to be physical, producible asymptotic states. The same dependency chain also
treats those modes as negative-norm ghosts removed by the Turok-Bateman
projector/Born rule. Until one typed physical-state rule resolves that fork,
"pair-producible mirror generation" and "projected-out ghost sector" cannot be
used together as a prediction.

Two further gaps remain subordinate to that blocker:

- the absolute scale `mu` and hence any collider mass window remain
  dynamics-gated;
- the Standard Model hypercharge embedding that gives the states their stated
  charge labels remains imported rather than GU-derived.

Consequently the mirror candidate is **BLOCKED**, not frozen and not numbered
as a prediction package. It must not reuse the already-occupied PP3 identifier.

## Exact reopen conditions

Reopen packet admission only after owner-native evidence supplies all of:

1. a source action selecting the gapping order parameter (or its typed
   quadratic composite), with normalization, nonzero/stabilizer, and reduction
   data;
2. a physical-state/Born-rule decision proving that the K-negative modes are
   producible asymptotic states, or a decision retiring the collider observable;
3. a native compact Standard Model embedding compatible with the projector;
4. an observable event class or mass window with a predeclared falsification
   threshold independent of post-data tuning.

PP1, PP2, and PP3 remain frozen exactly as committed. B.5 remains parked at
`B5-MIDDLE-SOURCE-GAP`. No claim, canon, verdict, public posture, or external
action changes.
