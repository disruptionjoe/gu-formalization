# Path 5, Branch B: the filtration <-> section map

**One-line result.** The map path 4 named as the single highest-leverage missing object --
`{F_tau}` (an observer's record/information FILTRATION) `<->` `Sect(Met(X^4))` (a SOURCE ACTION =
a section of the metric-on-metrics) -- is **not a mystery correspondence: it is precisely the
CONNES RADON-NIKODYM COCYCLE bijection**, wrapped in one geometric identification (Branch A's)
and one observerse certification. Stated at the right level it is a **genuine isomorphism** --
but at the **SELECTION-object level (state / weight / cocycle), not the raw filtration level**,
which is exactly where the rate-independence finding says the identification must live. The
honest ceiling: **characterizable now, toy-constructible now, unconditionally constructible
GU-side only after two named sibling dependencies are discharged.** No canon / verdict / status
movement.

Construction fork (`GEOMETER-VS-PHYSICS-OBJECTS.md`): the admissibility algebra `M` is program-
native **Krein/indefinite** (KEEP-AND-GRADE ghost clearance; the (9,5) signature forces the
indefinite form). The Connes/Tomita-Takesaki apparatus I use to *prove* the map is the standard
**definite-metric** theory, applied to a **type-I stand-in**. The Krein lift is flagged as the
load-bearing gap (Branch A), not silently assumed. I state which construction is in play at each
load-bearing step.

## Persona team (inline, sequential, one context)

### (1) Specialist -- information geometry / filtered probability / section-functor: the construction

**Step 1 -- what is `{F_tau}` precisely?** A filtration is an increasing family of information
structures `F_s subseteq F_t` (`s <= t`) indexed by a parameter `tau` -- an observer's
*accumulating record*. In a commutative (classical) setting this is an increasing family of
sigma-algebras. In the modular (Branch A / Tomita-Takesaki) setting the noncommutative analogue
is the **modular filtration**: a von Neumann algebra `M` (the region's admissibility algebra)
carries, for each faithful normal weight `phi`, a **modular automorphism flow**
`sigma^phi_tau in Aut(M)` (Tomita-Takesaki), and `tau` -- modular time -- is the record parameter.
The observer's accumulating record is the family generated by the flow up to `tau`. **Crucial
discipline (rate-independence):** the *rate* at which `tau` advances is NOT allowed to be the
carrier of physical content (path 4; `rate-independence-negative-finding-2026-06-22.md`: rates
are absorbed, do not enter any structural theorem). So the object that must map to a section is
NOT the flow-as-dynamics but its **rate-invariant content**.

**Step 2 -- what is `Sect(Met(X^4))` precisely?** `Y^14 = Met(X^4)` is the DeWitt/gimmel
metric-on-metrics; a **source action** is a choice of **section** of this bundle -- a point in the
proven **shape-dimension-1 family plus 2 scales** (the non-uniqueness proven not forced;
`CONJECTURE` sec 1). So `Sect(Met(X^4))` is, at the level of residual freedom, a **3-real-
coordinate** family: 1 shape coordinate + 2 scale coordinates. (The constant/vacuum section and
its DeWitt second fundamental form are the objects computed in
`tests/threads/B_constant_section_background_curvature.py`.)

**Step 3 -- construct the map.** The bridge between "a rate-invariant modular datum" and "a
section" is a **standard theorem**, not a new object:

> **Connes' Radon-Nikodym cocycle theorem (1973).** Fix a faithful normal *reference* weight
> `phi_0` on `M`. The map
> `phi  |-->  (D phi : D phi_0)_tau`  (the Connes cocycle: a family of unitaries `u_tau in M`
> obeying the cocycle identity `u_{s+t} = u_s , sigma^{phi_0}_s(u_t)`)
> is a **bijection** from faithful normal weights `phi` on `M` onto `sigma^{phi_0}`-cocycles.

The cocycle `(D phi : D phi_0)_tau` is the **rate-invariant content** of the observer's modular
filtration relative to the reference: it records **which state/weight** `phi` the observer's
record encodes, *not* how fast `tau` runs. So the composite map is:

```
  {F_tau}            modular          Connes RN            Branch-A
 (observer   --->  filtration's  --->  (fix phi_0)  --->  identification  --->  Sect(Met(X^4))
  record)          weight phi        cocycle u_tau        weight <-> section    (source action)
                                     = selection object
```

- **`{F_tau}` -> `phi`:** a modular filtration is generated by its faithful normal weight `phi`
  (Tomita-Takesaki). [needs: the record IS a modular filtration -- sibling dep B.]
- **`phi` <-> `u_tau` (cocycle):** Connes RN **bijection**. [standard theorem; definite metric.]
- **`phi` <-> section:** the load-bearing geometric identification -- the region's faithful
  normal weights are parametrized by sections of `Met(X^4)`. This is **Branch A's** modular-
  conjugation identification (the Krein `C`-operator IS the modular conjugation `J`); a section
  chooses the state, the state chooses the weight, the weight generates the flow whose fixed
  surface is the firewall (Bisognano-Wichmann). [needs: Krein modular theory -- sibling dep A;
  and a **dimension match** weight-slice <-> (shape-dim-1 + 2 scales).]

**Step 4 -- is it an isomorphism?** At the **selection-object level: YES** -- the middle link
(Connes RN) is a genuine bijection, and the outer links are bijections *iff* their stated
conditions hold. At the **raw filtration level: NO** -- it is **many-to-one**, and the fiber is
exactly the **reparametrization of `tau` (the rate)**. That is not a defect; it is the precise
form of the rate-independence caveat: **observer-states `<->` source-actions is an isomorphism;
observer-dynamics -> source-action is a rate-quotient.** The identification "observer = source
action" is therefore precise **at the selection level** and only there.

**The structure `{F_tau}` must carry** (to match a section, i.e. to sit in the domain of this
map): (a) a **faithful normal weight / KMS state** generating it -- it must be a *modular*
filtration, not an arbitrary increasing family; (b) a **fixed reference weight** `phi_0` (the
cocycle is relative); (c) **`tau` = modular time**, so that the rate-quotient lands in the
`sigma^{phi_0}`-cocycle group. A filtration lacking a generating KMS/modular structure has **no
image** under the map -- that is the well-definedness condition, and it is a real filter (most
"information filtrations" are not modular).

### (3) Intra-team adversary (PRESENTS, does not veto)

- **"The correspondence secretly reintroduces the falsified rate reading."** The whole object is
  the *modular flow* `sigma^phi_tau`, and `tau` is a rate. If the section depended on `tau`, this
  would be the rate reading path 4 killed. **Rebuttal (decisive, and machine-checked):** the map
  factors through the **cocycle**, which is the rate-*invariant* content; W68 Part B shows the
  recovered selection object is invariant under `tau -> c tau` while the raw cocycle changes. The
  rate is the *quotiented fiber*, never the image. **Strength: neutralized -- this is exactly why
  the map is built at the selection level.** (If someone instead mapped `Sect` to the *generator*
  `log Delta_phi` including its overall scale, THAT would smuggle the rate back; the discipline is
  to map to the cocycle/weight, not the generator's scale.)
- **"The map is many-to-one, so it is not an identification."** Correct at the raw level, and this
  is admitted, not hidden: raw filtrations -> sections is many-to-one with rate fiber. **Rebuttal:**
  the *claim* is an iso at the selection level, and the many-to-one-ness is the precise content of
  "observer-dynamics is rate-free." **Strength: converts an objection into the statement.**
- **"`phi <-> section` is not well-defined -- you have not shown the weight space matches the
  section family."** **This lands.** The real region algebra is **type III_1** with an
  *infinite-dimensional* weight space (a Bloch-ball-of-a-factor is not finite); the source-action
  residual freedom is **3-dimensional** (shape-dim-1 + 2 scales). A bijection between an
  infinite-dim weight space and a 3-dim section family cannot be the *full* weight space -- it
  must be a **distinguished finite section slice** (e.g. the KMS states at fixed modular
  temperature compatible with the DeWitt geometry, or the weights fixed by the residual gauge).
  **Strength: strong -- this is the genuine GU-side open problem, and W68 encodes it as the
  dimension-match criterion (type-I_2 matches dim 3; type-I_3 does not).** The toy match (dim
  `2^2-1 = 3`) is a *stand-in*, not a derivation that the real slice matches.
- **"The Krein algebra may not even have a modular conjugation, so `phi` may not exist."**
  **Lands, and is Branch A's whole job.** Tomita-Takesaki is definite-metric; Krein modular theory
  is underdeveloped (`CONJECTURE` sec 7 sub-risk). If `M` has no `J` with `J^2=1, JMJ=M'`, the
  first link fails before Connes. **Strength: strong -- this is sibling dependency A, the critical
  path.**

### (4) Cross-checker -- a solvable case

**Finite type-I model (`M = M_n(C)`), fully computed in W68.** Here faithful normal weights are
full-rank positive matrices; the Connes cocycle is the finite RN derivative
`u_tau = rho^{i tau} rho_0^{-i tau}`. Checks (all exit-0):
- **Cocycle identity** `u_{s+t} = u_s sigma_s(u_t)` to 8e-16 -> the filtration-to-cocycle map is
  **well-defined into the cocycle group** (not a loose correspondence).
- **State recovery** from the cocycle to 4e-16 -> Connes RN is a **bijection** here, concretely.
- **Injectivity** (distinct states -> distinct cocycles) -> the selection object is **determined**.
- **Rate-independence** (`tau -> 3 tau` recovers the same state to 4e-16; raw cocycle changes) ->
  the section is the **rate-invariant** content; the rate reading is **not** reintroduced.
- **Section <-> state bijection** on the matched-dimension case: `Sect ~ R^3` (1 shape + 2 scales)
  <-> the **open Bloch ball** (faithful states of `M_2`), an explicit round-trip bijection via
  `v |-> v/(1+|v|)`. Dimension match `2^2-1 = 3`; the type-I_3 case (dim 8) has **no** such
  bijection -- the dimension obstruction is real and encoded.

**Cross-check verdict:** every link that is *claimed* to be a bijection **is** one in the solvable
model, to machine precision. What the solvable model **cannot** certify is that the *real* type-III
weight space has a 3-dim section slice matching `Met(X^4)`, and that a Krein `J` exists -- exactly
the two conditions the referee flagged. The model confirms the *mechanism*, not the *GU
instantiation*.

### (5) Synthesizer -- reachability, what holds, the gap, the sibling dependency

**What holds (constructible now, GU-side alone):**
1. The map is **identified**: it is the **Connes RN cocycle bijection** composed with Branch A's
   weight<->section identification. It is not a new object to invent; it is a known theorem to
   *instantiate*.
2. At the **selection-object level it is an isomorphism** (weights <-> cocycles is a bijection),
   machine-checked in a finite model.
3. It **respects rate-independence** by construction (factors through the rate-invariant cocycle;
   the tau-rate is the quotiented fiber) -- the falsified rate reading is provably not reintroduced.
4. The **well-definedness condition** on `{F_tau}` is pinned down: it must be a **modular
   filtration** (faithful normal weight / KMS, fixed reference, `tau` = modular time).
5. `Sect(Met(X^4))` is pinned down as the **3-coordinate** residual family (shape-dim-1 + 2 scales),
   and the **dimension-match** with a candidate weight-slice is isolated as the concrete criterion.

**The precise gap (why it is not unconditionally constructible GU-side):**
- **Gap A -- Krein modular theory (sibling: Branch A, critical path).** The first link needs the
  admissibility algebra `M` to admit a modular conjugation `J` (`J^2 = 1, JMJ = M'`) and a Connes
  RN cocycle calculus **in the indefinite/Krein metric** (`M` is program-native Krein, not
  positive-Hilbert). Standard Tomita-Takesaki is definite-metric; the Krein version is
  underdeveloped (`CONJECTURE` sec 7). Without it, `phi` and its cocycle are not even defined on
  the correct algebra. **Exactly what is needed: a Krein-space Tomita-Takesaki with (i) existence
  of `J` and `Delta` for the region's Krein admissibility algebra, and (ii) a Connes RN cocycle
  bijection valid for indefinite-metric weights.** This is Branch A's deliverable, not GU-side
  reconstructible here.
- **Gap B -- the record IS a modular filtration (sibling: observerse / temporal-issuance repo,
  cross-repo).** The `{F_tau} -> phi` link needs certification that the **physical observer
  record** is a modular filtration (`tau` = modular time, generated by a faithful normal weight),
  i.e. the typed relation `pi : U -> X^4` is **source-earned**. Path 4 already recorded that this
  cannot be manufactured from the public observerse bundle
  (`lab/archive/family-frontiers/14-...`) and needs the sibling observerse/temporal-issuance
  machinery. **Exactly what is needed: a source-earned map from the observerse record structure to
  a faithful normal weight on `M`, with the record's accumulation parameter identified as modular
  time.** Named, not executed (cross-repo).
- **Gap C -- the dimension/slice match (GU-side, open, not blocked).** Even granting A and B, the
  type-III weight space is infinite-dim while `Sect` is 3-dim; the iso must be onto a **finite
  distinguished slice** (candidate: KMS states at the DeWitt-fixed modular temperature, or the
  weights fixed by the residual source-action gauge). Isolating and matching that slice is a
  GU-side computation that **could be pushed further here** but is not yet done; W68 encodes it as
  the dimension-match criterion.

**Reachability:** **needs-new-math** for the *unconditional GU-side isomorphism* (Gap A is genuinely
new mathematics -- Krein modular theory; Gap B is cross-repo). **constructible-now** for (i) the
abstract skeleton, (ii) the toy isomorphism at the selection level, (iii) the precise conditional
theorem, (iv) the well-definedness structure `{F_tau}` must carry, and (v) the dimension-match
criterion. This is the honest predicted outcome: **characterizable and toy-constructible; not
fully constructible GU-side; needs Krein modular theory (A) and observerse certification (B).**

**Confidence grade:** **HIGH** that the map IS the Connes RN bijection at the selection level (it
is a named theorem, machine-checked in a finite model, and it dissolves the rate-independence
objection by construction). **HIGH** that the unconditional GU-side iso is blocked by Gaps A and B
as stated. **MEDIUM** that Gap C's slice-match is favourable (the dim-3 residual is suggestive but
the type-III slice is not identified). Overall: the *mechanism* is solid; the *GU instantiation* is
conditional on two named siblings plus one open GU-side slice computation.
