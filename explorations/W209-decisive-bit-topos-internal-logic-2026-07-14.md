## 4. Positive control PC3 -- the classifier machinery, and Boolean iff discrete

For sheaves over a finite poset (Alexandrov space), `Sub(1) = O(X)` is the frame of opens, a Heyting
algebra; `a -> b` is the largest open `u` with `u wedge a <= b`, and `not a = (a -> 0)`. A proposition
is **decided** iff its subobject is **complemented** (`a vee not a = 1` and `a wedge not a = 0`).

- The **hierarchical** observer site `g < r < i` has opens (up-sets) `{}, {i}, {r,i}, {g,r,i}` -- a
  4-element Heyting **chain**. The intermediate open `{i}` has `not{i} = 0`, so `{i} vee not{i} = {i}
  != top`: **NOT complemented**. The classifier is **NON-Boolean (intuitionistic)**.
- The **discrete** site (three observers, no refinement) has all `8` opens (the powerset), every one
  complemented: the classifier is **Boolean**.

The machinery therefore correctly separates a genuine hierarchy (intuitionistic) from an antichain
(Boolean). This is the lever the verdict turns on.
