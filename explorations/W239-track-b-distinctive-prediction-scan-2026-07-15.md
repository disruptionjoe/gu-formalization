## 1. Why the priority Yukawa route dies

W227 correctly prioritized a Standard Model mass or coupling relation because a dimensionless relation
among already measured parameters would be both cheap and hard to hide. H28 performed the relevant
representation-theory scan. Its exact result is a **selection rule, not a value rule**:

- the scalar Dirac Yukawa channel is unique and cross-chirality;
- the derived `Z/3` reduces nine generation-matrix entries to three;
- the surviving entries are `{y00, y12, y21}` on the transpose-bilinear fork, or three diagonal
  entries on the Krein-sesquilinear fork;
- all three surviving coefficients remain independent complex numbers;
- the sector-to-flavor identification is not supplied; and
- the mod-3 charges are Froggatt-Nielsen sterile on the allowed entries because every invariant entry
  has zero charge exponent.

Thus GU currently emits no mass ratio, no mixing angle, no Yukawa eigenvalue ratio, and no Jarlskog
invariant. The `1+2` support is also generic to a `Z/3` flavor selection rule. Textbook Clifford theory
accounts for the unique scalar channel. The exact negative conclusion is:

> Built GU structure constrains Yukawa support but not Yukawa magnitudes.

This is stronger and more useful than saying the mass calculation is merely unfinished. It says the
present symmetry data cannot produce the requested relation without additional dynamics or a larger
flavor structure.

## 2. The strongest quantitative conditional, and why it does not count

The H50-H52 chain is the sharpest numerical output in the repo. On the program-native `|II|^2`
construction, the exact geometric coefficient is

```text
c_L = 3/8,
rho_Lambda = c_L mu_DW^4,
m_2 = sqrt(m2_eff) mu_DW,        m2_eff in [5/6, 5/4].
```

If, and only if, H36 identifies that vacuum term with the observed dark-energy density, then eliminating
`mu_DW` gives

```text
lambda = (hbar c) c_L^(1/4) / (sqrt(m2_eff) rho_Lambda^(1/4)),
alpha = 1/3.
```

Using `rho_Lambda^(1/4) = 2.3 meV`, the output is

```text
alpha = 1/3,
lambda = 60.0 .. 73.6 micrometers.
```

The deterministic W239 test reproduces this band. H52's cited `alpha=1/3` 95 percent confidence
boundary is 47.6 micrometers, with an honest 46.0 to 51.2 micrometer envelope. Even the least excluding
edge is below the shortest predicted range. The conditional is excluded. Lee et al. independently
report no deviation and constrain gravitational-strength Yukawa interactions to ranges below 38.6
micrometers at 95 percent confidence. The 2026 short-range-gravity review was checked for a later rescue;
none was found.

This does **not** kill GU unconditionally, because the chain fails before the data comparison:

- H36 is an interpretive postulate, not a consequence of built GU dynamics;
- the observed dark-energy density is supplied as the scale-setting datum;
- the `alpha=1/3` Yukawa form is the standard linear massive-spin-2 trace factor, not uniquely GU; and
- quadratic higher-derivative gravity and its extra massive spin-2 mode predate GU, most classically in
  Stelle's 1977 theory.

The GU-specific part is the exact geometric coefficient `c_L=3/8` and the proposed cross-sector lock.
That is enough to make the conditional scientifically useful and falsifiable. It is not enough to make
it a native, independent GU prediction. The correct disposition is **KILLED_CONDITIONAL**, not
"prediction survived," and not an unconditional falsification of GU.

### What the divergence teaches

The personas converge on four families despite starting from different legs:

1. **Dimensionless Standard Model overconstraint.** This has the best impact-to-experimental-cost ratio
   because the data already exist. Its derivation difficulty is extreme because present GU flavor
   structure leaves coefficients free.
2. **A physical pole with native scale and residue.** Gravity, QFT, cosmology, and experiment all reach
   this family. It would be decisive, but it requires both the interacting positive `C` metric and a
   native scale. H36 shows the danger: a scale imported from the target can create a sharp but invalid
   "prediction."
3. **A complete mirror packet.** Collider, BRST, geometry, and hostile model selection converge here.
   It is highly distinctive if the mirror survives the quotient, but W237 adds a structural knot:
   channel S preserves chirality but does not compactify, while channel D compactifies but kills
   chirality. The physical-state, vacuum, and mass gaps make it an extreme-difficulty target.
4. **A correlated dark-energy and growth shape.** It can be future-testable, but genericity, fitted
   amplitudes, and completion dependence make it weaker than the first three.

The impact scores are high precisely because most proposals would transform GU from a reconstruction
framework into a constrained physical theory. Their uniformly high difficulty is not pessimistic
padding. It reflects the same missing objects seen from ten angles: the physical quotient and
interacting `C` metric, native normalizations, and coefficient-producing source dynamics.

The best practical priority remains a dimensionless flavor invariant, followed by a native physical
pole packet, then a complete mirror packet. The record-current proposals are useful diagnostics of the
source action but will count as novel predictions only if an ordinary fixed-source response cannot
reproduce them.

## 5. Construction forks that prevent false novelty

Three forks are load-bearing.

1. **Native DeWitt geometry versus standard quadratic gravity.** `c_L=3/8` belongs to the GU
   reconstruction. A fourth-order operator, a massive spin-2 pole, and the corresponding Yukawa form do
   not. The Track B object would have to be the native coefficient relation, with its scale derived
   independently.
2. **Native generation grading versus a standard flavor symmetry.** The derived `Z/3` has GU provenance.
   Its matrix support is nevertheless a standard finite-group selection rule. Novel provenance is not
   novel phenomenology.
3. **Native Krein mirror versus a standard vectorlike particle.** GU's mirror is initially a graded
   state-space object. Collider bounds apply only after it is shown to survive the physical quotient,
   acquire Standard Model charges and a mass, and have specified decay channels. CMS excludes some
   vectorlike T and B quarks up to about 1.5 TeV depending on branching fractions, but those bounds cannot
   yet be mapped honestly onto GU's unbuilt physical mirror.

## 6. What would reopen Track B

The next wave should not search a larger pile of qualitative similarities. It should derive one of
three exact objects:

1. **A dimensionless flavor invariant.** From the full native source action and physical quotient,
   derive a basis-invariant combination of Yukawa matrices, such as a singular-value ratio or the
   Jarlskog invariant, with no free flavor coefficients. Renormalization-group running and the energy
   at which the relation holds must be specified before comparison with data.
2. **A native scale relation.** Fix `kappa`, `Z_U`, or `mu_DW` from GU normalization and dynamics without
   importing the observed target. Then pre-declare the experimental observable before looking at the
   relevant data.
3. **A physical mirror observable.** Escape W237's compactification-versus-chirality obstruction with a
   native operator outside the null-pair bilinear route, then prove that the mirror excitations survive
   the physical quotient and derive their mass scale, charges, splittings, and branching fractions. Only
   then is a collider confrontation meaningful.

The first route remains the best Track B target because a dimensionless Standard Model relation avoids
the present scale-normalization trap and can be tested now. At present it is a burden, not a result.
