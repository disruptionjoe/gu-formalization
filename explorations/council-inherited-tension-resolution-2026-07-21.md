## Member (i) — Quantum-foundations / measurement theorist

**My assigned horn:** `sigma` is a superselection charge; superselection is the
einselection/preferred-basis mechanism (Zurek). Does supplying `sigma` partially
resolve the measurement problem's preferred-basis / einselection horn?

**The tempting upgrade, stated at its strongest.** A superselection rule forbids
coherent superpositions across sectors of distinct charge. `sigma` is a genuine
global superselection datum: the ghost-parity operator `P` commutes with the
dynamics (`[P,S]=0`, Turok-Bateman; CH-QM S4), and `sigma` fixes its orientation.
If pointer states lived in distinct `sigma`-sectors, their superpositions would be
forbidden — and that is one classical story for why macroscopic superpositions are
not seen. So on its face `sigma` "supplies the preferred decomposition for free."

**Why it fails — three independent kills.** The identification equivocates on the
word "superselection."

1. **Kinematic vs dynamical/emergent.** Einselection is *environment-induced*
   superselection — a **dynamical, emergent** basis selected by the
   system-environment interaction Hamiltonian via the predictability sieve. `sigma`
   is a **kinematic, construction-fixed, global** `Z/2`. One is produced by
   monitoring; the other is posited once. A fixed kinematic sector label does not
   do the dynamical work einselection does.
2. **Cardinality.** The measurement problem needs a superselection structure fine
   enough to separate *all* macroscopically distinct pointer configurations — an
   effectively continuous family of sectors. `sigma` is **one** `Z/2`
   (`CARDINALITY-1`, pin-bordism). A single binary sector split cannot index the
   pointer continuum. It separates *physical from ghost*, not *this pointer from
   that pointer*.
3. **No tensor split.** Einselection presupposes `H = H_S (x) H_E`. GU **imports**
   the arena/signature (parsimony knob; QD tension "assumed tensor split") — `sigma`
   supplies no factorization. Einselection cannot even get started natively.

**What `sigma` genuinely does here, and why it is not new.** `sigma` fixes the
**orientation of the physical Hilbert sector** (the CH-QM right-vs-wrong orientation;
wrong orientation fails *cohomologically*, a real kill test it survives). That is
exactly the **QM Krein-sign RESOLVE already inside the four-cluster** — not a new
dividend on the preferred-basis horn. The physical-sector rule is a graded quotient
`ker delta_2 / im d` then P-graded by `sigma`; that is a *unitarity/norm* structure
(which subspace is positive), **not** a *classicality/pointer* structure (which
basis is robust).

**My verdicts.**
- Preferred-basis / einselection — **COMPLETE-INHERIT.** `sigma` = one kinematic
  global `Z/2` sector-orientation; einselection needs an imported split + a
  dynamical pointer continuum. Reframe-only; no mechanism.
- Measurement / single outcome — **COMPLETE-INHERIT.** `sigma` fixes the sector,
  not *which* outcome; adjacent to QD's open single-outcome gap, both OPEN.
- Born rule (origin / non-circular) — **COMPLETE-INHERIT.** The L7 Born rule is
  source-gapped; `sigma`-superselection supplies no non-circular measure.
- Classical-quantum boundary — **COMPLETE-INHERIT.** Same source-gap; no derived
  cut; `sigma` gives no Heisenberg line.
- QD tensor split — **COMPLETE-INHERIT.** Split is imported; `sigma` is not it.

**Net (i):** the strongest measurement candidate does **not** upgrade. The word
"superselection" is shared; the mechanism is not.
