# Tension ledger — the open problems of the theories that have actually made confirmed predictions, and where GU sits

## What this document is, and the one filter that defines it

The **spine is the TENSIONS** — the specific, concrete open problems, limitations,
and "only-part-of-the-story" incompletenesses of the theories that have an
**actual confirmed-prediction track record**. Those, and only those, are:

- **Standard Model** (collider rates, g-2, precision electroweak — to many digits),
- **Quantum Mechanics (foundations)** (all of quantum experiment),
- **Quantum Field Theory** (the framework under the SM; QED to ~12 digits),
- **Quantum Darwinism / decoherence** (Zurek — einselection + redundant objectivity,
  now experimentally probed),
- **General Relativity** (perihelion, lensing, GW, frame-dragging, Shapiro delay),
- **LCDM** (CMB acoustic peaks, BAO, BBN abundances, growth of structure).

**Excluded from the spine** (per the charge): the no-confirmed-prediction
speculative frameworks — string / M-theory, LQG, E8, causal sets, NCG, asymptotic
safety, conformal gravity. These are candidate *resolutions*, not successful
theories whose tensions motivate the search. They may appear inside a cell as a
*proposed fix*; they are never a spine row.

## The type lens (Joe's) — what a tension actually IS: CONFLICT / BOUNDARY / BOTH

Every tension below is typed by **what kind of incompleteness it is**, in Joe's
taxonomy. This is the structuring lens — read the ledger through it:

- **CONFLICT** — the theory's story explains a lot but **CLASHES** with something it
  should also be telling: an internal contradiction, a clash with another successful
  theory, or a prediction that conflicts with observation. *(QFT's vacuum energy
  overshooting the observed CC by ~10^120; QM's unitary evolution vs a single
  definite outcome; QM and GR being mutually incompatible; a falsified-then-patched
  prediction.)*
- **BOUNDARY** — the theory works for everything **WITHIN** certain bounds, but
  **OUTSIDE** them it goes silent or breaks down; the **bookends where it stops being
  the story ARE the tension**. *(GR at singularities; the SM silent on gravity and
  dark matter; QM's classical–quantum cut; LCDM at small scales / high-z; QFT's
  non-perturbative regime.)*
- **BOTH** — many tensions are simultaneously an internal clash **and** a domain
  bookend; these are flagged BOTH. *(The cosmological-constant problem is a CONFLICT
  inside QFT AND a BOUNDARY where QFT and GR stop working together.)*

**One axis feeds directly into this typing: RENORMALIZABILITY** — and it is keyed
to **continuum** field-theoretic sectors ONLY (discrete candidate-resolutions like
causal sets and LQG are `N/A-DISCRETE` and are not spine theories anyway). A
sector's UV behaviour *is* a tension, and it maps onto the type:
- **RENORMALIZABLE** — predictive in the continuum (the SM's triumph; not itself a
  tension — though the CC-magnitude is where QFT nonetheless fails).
- **NON-RENORMALIZABLE** ⇒ a **BOUNDARY** tension — only an effective theory, breaks
  down at high energy (perturbative quantum GR; a core reason GR is "only part of
  the story").
- **RENORM-BUT-GHOSTS** ⇒ a **CONFLICT** — renormalizability bought at the cost of
  unitarity (conformal Weyl² gravity; **and GU's own fourth-order gravity sector**).
- **ASYMPTOTICALLY-SAFE** — non-perturbatively renormalizable via a UV fixed point (a
  candidate-resolution route, e.g. GU's E2 stability fork).

The dedicated check is the **Renormalizability section** below (keyed to the
continuum sectors); the main tension rows carry an inline `[Renorm: …]` tag where
UV behaviour *is* the tension.

## The containment lens, applied BOTH WAYS (the point of the GU column)

GU **houses** the SM, QM, and QFT (per-leg-recovery: QM at `R1_COND`, SM at `R1`
with 6 typed imports, GR-vacuum/COSMO/records at `R0_COND`). Housing is inheritance
in *both* directions: GU inherits the housed theory's **confirmed predictions** *and*
its **unsolved tensions**. So the honest question is never "does GU have fewer
tensions?" — by construction it carries nearly all of them. The honest question is:
**which tensions does GU RESOLVE that its housed theories could not?** That set is
small, and naming it exactly is the deliverable.

**Three GU verdicts per tension:**
- **RESOLVES** — GU genuinely renders it non-brute. Expected to be *only* the
  sign/orientation cluster (QM Krein sign, GR vacuum-cancellation sign, DE sign,
  arrow of time), which GU collapses to **one external bit `sigma`**.
- **INHERITS** — GU houses the theory that carries the tension, so GU carries the
  tension too, and offers no fix (hierarchy, flavor, the CC magnitude, the
  measurement problem, ...).
- **SILENT** — GU houses no model covering it, so it neither has nor solves it
  (dark-matter identity, H0, S8, the information paradox, ...).
