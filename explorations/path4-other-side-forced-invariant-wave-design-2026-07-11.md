# Path 4: what is on the other side of the source action -- forced family-invariant hunt (wave design)

**Status:** SETUP (deploying). The lateral move: instead of BUILDING the source action (which we proved is a
shape-dimension-1 family + 2 scales, so building = choosing = a model), characterize what EVERY member of that
family must imply. Anything forced across the whole family is a choice-INDEPENDENT consequence -- forced despite
the non-uniqueness. This targets exactly what our non-uniqueness result cannot touch, and it follows the Weyl
precedent (a wrong/unbuilt object whose forced consequence -- the gauge principle -- was the real contribution).

## 1. The target

A HEADLINE-grade result here is a **forced family-invariant**: a prediction, correlation, or structure that is
the same for every source action in the shape-dim-1 family (independent of the residual gravity-shape ratio
`beta/alpha` and the free scales `mu_DW`, `alpha`), AND is novel (not a re-derivation of known physics), AND is
discriminating (distinguishes the framework from LCDM / GR / a generic EFT), ideally testable. If one exists it
is a choice-independent prediction that survives even if the specific GU construction is wrong.

### Sub-questions (graded verdict on EACH, per candidate)
- **Q-forced:** is the candidate forced across the ENTIRE shape-dim-1 family (independent of the residual choices
  and the free scales), or does it depend on a residual choice?
- **Q-novel:** is it a genuinely NEW forced fact/correlation/structure, or a re-derivation of known physics (the
  "compression, not prediction" trap that H34 already flagged)?
- **Q-disc:** is it discriminating (distinguishes the framework from LCDM/GR/generic-EFT), and ideally testable?

## 2. Methodology refinement (Joe, this wave): the adversary PRESENTS, it does NOT DECIDE

In paths 2 and 3 the adversarial branch was terminal (it closed path 3 into a theorem). For a NOVELTY hunt that
is the wrong setting: a skeptic can dismiss almost anything as "already known physics" (everything connects to
known physics somehow), so an adversary-as-decider would kill genuine novelties by over-applying the dismissal.
Therefore in this wave:
- The adversary branch (E) builds the STRONGEST case against each candidate on all three axes (already-known /
  not-forced-across-family / not-discriminating) and returns ARGUMENTS tagged by strength.
- The adversary does NOT return a terminal verdict. The ORCHESTRATOR synthesis weighs the adversary's arguments
  against the constructive branches' claims and renders the {Q-forced, Q-novel, Q-disc} verdict per candidate.
- Rationale: novelty is a claim the constructive evidence can carry against a skeptical prior; the adversary
  sharpens and stress-tests it, it does not veto it. (Weight by evidence depth, not by who spoke last.)

## 3. The four candidate invariants (each a branch; constructive branches blind to each other and to E)

**Branch A -- C3: the EOS x strong-gravity correlation.** The dark-energy equation of state `w(z)` and
strong-field gravity BOTH flow from the same shared `theta` (the II-class). Is there a FORCED CORRELATION between
them -- a locus in (cosmological-EOS, strong-field-deviation) space that every family member must satisfy -- and
is it family-invariant and discriminating (no LCDM variant makes it)? This is the sharpest headline candidate: a
correlation is choice-independent precisely because it ties two observables to the one shared object, cancelling
the residual scales. Compute the correlation and test its family-invariance.

**Branch B -- B1: the O(M^0) intrinsic curvature we SUBTRACT.** The intrinsic curvature of `Met(X^4)` -- the
"constant section is not totally geodesic" term currently subtracted "as a convention" -- is it PHYSICAL, forced,
and equal to the dark-energy / cosmological-constant scale? Test: is keeping (not subtracting) this term forced
across the family, and if kept, what does it predict? "Do not subtract it away without checking" -- this is where
a novel forced DE contribution could live.

**Branch C -- B2: the observerse / issuance bridge.** Does the DeWitt curvature connect to observer-non-collapse
in a forced way -- is `f_0` (dark energy) an issuance/admissibility rate of an observerse-non-collapse condition,
and is there a HARD statement (an impossibility theorem, or a rate identity) that is family-invariant? The
highest-novelty, softest candidate -- "the paper only this program can write." Push for one sharp forced
statement, not a framework sketch.

**Branch D -- D4: the discrete 2-bit forced fact.** Independent of the residual freedom, what DISCRETE fact is
forced? The count in `{1,3}` (path 3), the carrier bit, the 4th-order/7-DOF property -- is there a discrete,
choice-independent, discriminating fact (a 2-bit prediction) that survives the whole family? Frame the strongest
discrete forced statement and test whether it discriminates (vs the H53 result that the 7-DOF property, while
forced, is not an accessible observable).

**Branch E -- the adversary (PROSECUTOR, not decider).** For EACH of the four candidates, build the strongest
case that it is (a) already-known physics, (b) not forced across the family (depends on a residual choice or a
free scale), or (c) not discriminating / not testable. Return arguments tagged by strength (decisive / strong /
weak). Do NOT render the verdict -- the orchestrator decides. Be genuinely adversarial (the "compression not
prediction" prior is real -- H34 found the honest register is accommodation), but present, do not veto.

## 4. Team composition (each branch = one worker running a 5-persona team INLINE)
Per the standing rule (personas always inline). Five agents total (A-E), parallel; A-D mutually blind and blind
to E; E is briefed on the candidate LIST (public thread items) but not on the constructive teams' reasoning.
5-persona template (specialize persona 1 per candidate): (1) the domain specialist (cosmology/EOS for A;
differential geometry / Met(X^4) for B; observerse/information theory for C; representation/topology for D;
the skeptic-of-novelty for E); (2) the referee (grades forced/family-invariant vs choice-dependent, novel vs
known); (3) the intra-team adversary (attacks the branch's own claim -- PRESENTS, the branch synthesizer weighs);
(4) the cross-checker (independent derivation / known-limit); (5) the synthesizer (states {Q-forced, Q-novel,
Q-disc}, confidence, the load-bearing assumption, which construction used, and -- for A-D -- the single strongest
forced-and-novel statement the branch can defend).

## 5. Wave protocol
1. Deploy A-E parallel. A-D return their strongest forced-and-novel candidate + graded {Q-forced, Q-novel,
   Q-disc} + a durable artifact (exploration + deterministic test where computed). E returns per-candidate
   prosecution arguments tagged by strength.
2. ORCHESTRATOR synthesis (inline, science-council lens, THE DECIDER): for each candidate, weigh the constructive
   evidence against E's arguments and render {Q-forced, Q-novel, Q-disc}. Do NOT let E's dismissal auto-veto;
   require E to have a DECISIVE argument to kill a candidate the constructive branch defended with evidence.
3. Cross-share + wave-2 design (deepen the surviving headline candidate; or, if all die to decisive arguments,
   the honest Lakatos answer).
4. Re-rank + capture the adversary-presents-not-decides method move.
