## 2. Axis A — who owns the state space? (the deepest fault line)

Mannheim and Bateman-Turok give **mutually exclusive ontologies** for the same fourth-order ghost:

- **Mannheim**: there is no ghost. The indefinite norm was a quantization error; the dynamics secretly
  defines a positive-definite inner product (the C operator), and once found, the theory is an ordinary
  unitary quantum theory. Positivity is **derived**.
- **Bateman-Turok**: the ghost is real and stays. The state space is genuinely indefinite; what changes is
  the **Born rule** (projectors + trace, positive iff ghost parity is a symmetry). Positivity is
  **relocated into the question asked**.

Notably, Bateman-Turok cite Bender-Mannheim only in passing and never adjudicate against PT quantization
(checked in the full text). So the two rescues have not been formally compared in the literature — the
identity-or-difference question is OPEN externally, which is exactly what today's swing route R1 computes
on the shared PU toy. First-principles expectation: they coincide on observables in the PT-unbroken,
diagonalizable regime (both reduce to "a discrete structure commuting with the dynamics splits the
hyperbolic pairs") and diverge at the degenerate/Jordan boundary, where the C operator fails to exist but
the kinematic parity survives.

**Where GU stands in this fight: nowhere — and that is the finding.** GU publicly specifies no state
space, no action, no quantization (the 10-claim UCSD set contains no positivity claim at all). The repo's
reconstruction shows GU's matter module IS a Krein space, but only because the internal group is
noncompact — a scope-exit from Distler-Garibaldi's Hilbert assumption, not a stated GU axiom. GU is the
only one of the three programs whose indefinite metric is **unacknowledged by its author**.

## 3. Axis B — same structure, different origin (why the rescue does not transfer for free)

The ghost's origin differs: Mannheim/Bateman-Turok get it from **four derivatives** (Ostrogradsky);
GU gets it from **noncompact gauge structure** (kinematic, no dynamics at all). The resulting
mathematics converges — hyperbolic (state, ghost) pairs under an indefinite form; Bateman-Turok's
(Omega, Upsilon) swap is structurally GU's (generation, mirror) swap — but the rescues are theorems
about **specific dynamics**. Bateman-Turok's positivity proof requires kappa to commute with the
S-matrix of their particular O(1,1) theory. GU has no S-matrix. Importing the rescue IS the unbuilt
source action; this is the canon file's open condition restated, and no external paper closes it.

## 5. Axis D — the anti-Einstein convergence (the genuine alignment)

All three programs reject the Einstein-Hilbert structure as fundamental, each from a different first
principle:

- **Weinstein (UCSD claim 2)**: Einstein's contraction (curvature -> Einstein tensor) is *disallowed*
  in the Ehresmannian world — it breaks gauge equivariance; hence distortion, the two-connection
  mechanism, and the shiab replacement.
- **Mannheim**: Einstein-Hilbert is not conformally invariant; exact conformal symmetry forces Weyl^2,
  whose field equations (Bach) need no contraction trick; Einstein gravity re-emerges only after
  symmetry breaking ('t Hooft connection noted in his abstract).
- **Bateman-Turok**: second-order gravity is the low-energy face of a four-derivative theory; their
  quantized toy is literally the conformally flat limit of quadratic gravity, spin-2 decoupled.

Three different symmetry diagnoses (gauge equivariance / scale invariance / UV completeness) of the
same alleged disease. This is the strongest sense in which the Mannheim episode "aligns with
Weinstein's theory": both refuse the contraction move, and both re-type the cosmological constant as
an artifact (Weinstein: epsilon_omega compose d_A replaces lambda g; Mannheim: zero-point readjustment
under symmetry breaking). The alignment is at the level of *what is rejected*; the replacements are
incompatible.

## 6. Axis E — the Higgs

Weinstein (claim 6): Higgs = geometric — Mexican hat is a negative-curvature signature of connection
perturbation; minimal coupling = Yukawa. Mannheim: Higgs = dynamical/composite — no fundamental scalar

scalar), incompatible mechanisms (geometry vs dynamics). Neither exhibits the matching calculation to
the SM's radiatively-stable effective description in the sources examined. Symmetric scorecard: both
are unfalsified-by-absence here, neither earns credit.
