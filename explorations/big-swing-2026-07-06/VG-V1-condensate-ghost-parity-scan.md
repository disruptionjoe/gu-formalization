## (a) The ghost parity P

**Relation to chirality: P ANTICOMMUTES with chi.** ‖{P, chi}‖ = 5.3e-14 while ‖[P, chi]‖ = 27.7.

K is purely off-diagonal in the chi-eigenbasis (both 96-dim halves K-null), so its sign is also
purely off-diagonal, i.e. P exchanges the chirality halves. Anticommutation, not commutation, is
"as appropriate" here.

**The canon pair-swap parity is the same operator.** Constructing the generation↔mirror swap
independently — the polar unitary part of K's cross-chirality block, the canonical

pair — gives ‖P_sign(K) − P_swap‖ = 4.3e-14. The singular values of the cross block are all
exactly 1.000. So the two constructions the route asked for coincide identically; there is one
ghost parity on this sector, and its P = +1 (physical) sector has min K-eig +1.000, its P = −1
(ghost/mirror) sector max K-eig −1.000, each 96-dimensional.

## The mechanism: a two-line lemma makes the scan exhaustive

**Intertwiner lemma.** If {P, chi} = 0, [M, P] = 0, and [M, chi] = 0, then chi maps the P = +1
sector isometrically (up to the K-sign) onto the P = −1 sector and conjugates M|₊ into M|₋, so
the two sectors are exactly isospectral. Verified numerically on all nine even chi-commuting
channels: max split 2.6e-14.

**Exhaustiveness over the native algebra.** Every route-enumerated native generator commutes with
chi (all family su(2)± generators are even Clifford elements; the scale direction is the identity
on W; chi commutes with itself) — measured [·, chi] ≈ 1e-14 across the board. And the compact
family generators commute with K itself (8.6e-14), hence with P = sign(K). The algebra they
generate therefore splits as E ⊕ chi·E with E in the commutant of both P and chi. So every
K-self-adjoint element of the native algebra is exhaustively one of:

- **P-even (the E part):** [M, P] = 0 holds, ghost-parity compatibility is free — but by the
  lemma the mass spectrum is EXACTLY parity-symmetric. Mirrors and generations gap together.
  m+ ≠ m− is not realizable.
- **P-odd (the chi·E part):** the deformation breaks [M, P] = 0 outright ([M,P]/‖M‖ = 2.0 for
  i·chi), i.e. Turok-Bateman positivity collapses through the condensate — kill condition 1's
  failure mode — or the direction is K-skew and not a mass channel at all (chi·family).

There is no third native branch. Mirror-selective gapping requires a mass direction that is
**P-even AND chi-non-commuting**, and the native algebra contains none: it lies entirely inside
the chi-commutant.

**The obstruction is native content, not Krein geometry.** The random P-even K-self-adjoint
controls (which are chi-non-commuting, [M,chi] ≈ 1.4) split the sectors at ~4e-3 while keeping
[M, P] = 0 to 7e-15. So ghost-parity-compatible, mirror-selective mass deformations DO exist on
this Krein module — they are just not in the algebra GU natively supplies on the triplet sector.
The splitting datum is an import, in exact parallel with canon's chirality verdict
(`swing-ghost-parity-no-chiral-selection.md`: consistency native, selection imported).

## Honest gaps

1. **"GU-native" = the route-enumerated generator set.** The exhaustiveness theorem covers the
   algebra generated by {scalar, fiber scale, su(2)+, su(2)−, pseudoscalar}. Other candidate
   native structures (full so(9,5) generators, Clifford-odd elements, base-position-dependent
   operators on an actual Y14) are outside this scan's scope. The lemma's reach is exactly as
   wide as the chi-commutant + P-parity structure of whatever else is proposed.
2. **Single signature (9,5).** Canon's anchors are signature-independent across (9,5)/(7,7)/(14,0),
   but this scan was not rerun in the other signatures.
3. **Kinematic mass deformation only.** M(Φ) = Φ is the instantaneous quadratic-form deformation;
   a Yukawa-bilinear or derivative-coupled condensate could induce a different M(Φ). And the
   dynamical half of T3' — [P_ghost, S] = 0 through a time-dependent breaking — remains
   uncomputable: no S exists. That gap is R1/R3 territory (running separately; not cited).
4. **The (iv) check is tautological given {P, chi} = 0** — flagged above with control; it should
   not be counted as an independent confirmation of canon.
5. **P-derived channels sampled, not exhausted:** even projections of native directions were
   scanned (two; both still chi-commuting, hence symmetric), but "P-derived" is not a closed
   class; the classification theorem applies to the native algebra proper.
