# Marble/Wood Local-Minimum And Open-Avenues Ledger

## 1. Verdict

Einstein's marble/wood complaint about general relativity is a useful warning for this
repo, but it should not be flattened into a simple left-side/right-side slogan. The more
useful split is:

```text
G_mu nu              principled geometric marble;
T_mu nu              phenomenological matter/stress-energy wood;
Lambda g_mu nu       geometric-looking patch or foreign insert on the marble side.
```

The cosmological-constant term is especially dangerous for GU-facing language. It lives on
the geometric side, but as a fixed constant it is not automatically principled marble. It
can be worse than ordinary wood if it is inserted into the marble side without a
source-derived dynamical justification.

The steelman GU move is not to announce that GU has already replaced the wood, not to
preserve the marble as unquestionable, and not to claim that GU has derived or cancelled
`Lambda`. The steelman is narrower and more heterodox:

```text
Maybe G_mu nu, T_mu nu, and the apparent dark-energy/Lambda sector are all observer
shadows of a deeper source geometry.
```

Current verdict:

```text
OPEN_AVENUES_PRESERVED
NO_ROUTE_SOLVED
NO_CLAIM_PROMOTION
SOURCE_GEOMETRY_IS_THE_STRONGEST_ORGANIZING_CONTRACT, NOT A PROOF
```

The marble/wood insight would become a local minimum if it made the repo do only one of
these:

- repair the matter side while silently treating the metric side as final;
- reject the metric side while failing to recover exact GR as a shadow;
- treat `Lambda g_mu nu` as clean marble merely because it is written geometrically;
- fit a dark-energy coefficient such as `xi_eff` to DESI/Rubin-style targets and call it
  source geometry;
- say "source geometry" as if naming the deeper source derived its shadows;
- discard metric quantization, compact controls, or ordinary GR/QFT checks because they
  look conventional;
- import ordinary QFT/GR, finite Connes data, Bell states, or K3 arithmetic and cite them
  as derived GU outputs.

The decision-grade posture is therefore:

```text
Keep all routes open as routes, not as claims.
Every route must carry allowed use, forbidden claim, proof debt, next computation,
and rollback/failure condition.
```

## 2. Route Taxonomy And Plain-English Meaning

| route id | short name | plain-English meaning | current status |
|---|---|---|---|
| `WOOD_REPAIR_WITHIN_SHADOW_GR` | fix the wood | Keep the GR-like metric shadow as the working left side and try to derive, constrain, or cleanly source the matter/stress-energy right side. | open/control |
| `MARBLE_INTERROGATION` | question the marble | Treat the metric/geometric left side as possibly premature: the metric may be a section shadow rather than the fundamental object. | open/specification |
| `LAMBDA_PATCH_DYNAMIC_SOURCE` | replace the patch | Treat `Lambda g_mu nu` as a suspicious marble-side patch term; keep open a dynamic source-geometric dark-energy replacement without claiming one is derived. | open/proof-debt |
| `SOURCE_DERIVE_BOTH` | derive both | Build the deeper source object and prove reductions to both GR marble and QFT/matter wood. | strongest contract/open |
| `METRIC_QUANTIZATION_DOWNSTREAM` | downstream metric quantization | Keep metric quantization as an effective comparison or shadow extraction task, not the definition of GU. | downstream comparison |
| `COMPACT_FINITE_CONTROLS` | honest finite controls | Use K3, finite Connes, Pati-Salam, and Bell fixtures as compact or finite controls with loss/provenance ledgers. | control-only unless bridged |
| `ORDINARY_GR_QFT_IMPORT_CONTROLS` | standard controls | Use ordinary GR and QFT imports as baselines, target checks, and anti-smuggling controls, not as GU derivations. | baseline/control |
| `CLAIM_GOVERNANCE_INTERFACE` | local-minimum guard | Keep the routes separated by interface contracts, provenance tags, rollback rules, and citation language. | process/open |

The key distinction is:

```text
route open != claim proved
control useful != physical derivation
hosted slot != selected shadow
comparison branch != primary GU
geometric-looking patch != source-derived dynamics
```

## 3. Route Ledger

### 3.1 `WOOD_REPAIR_WITHIN_SHADOW_GR`: Fix The Wood

**What it can do.** It can sharpen the right-hand side of an observer-facing GR shadow:
derive a source law, improve stress-energy provenance, classify matter/gauge/Higgs inputs,
and test whether `T_mu nu^shadow` is conserved as the image of a full source Noether or
Bianchi identity.

**What it cannot claim.** It cannot claim that GU has solved Einstein's complaint if the
left-hand metric equation is simply inherited from GR, if the stress tensor is imported
from ordinary QFT, or if unexplained source residuals are relabeled as matter.

| field | content |
|---|---|
| allowed use | Build a branch-specific source/stress-energy ledger for `R_GR`, `R_SM`, and `R_QFT`; classify ordinary matter terms as derived, hosted, imported, control, or failed. |
| forbidden claim | "GU fixes the wood" when `T_mu nu`, `A_F`, `G_SM`, Higgs dynamics, anomaly safety, or quantum states are inserted as external QFT/SM inputs. |
| proof debt | `ELProjectedGRShadowTheorem(branch)`; derived conservation of `T_mu nu^shadow`; `Phi_SG_MG` or equivalent source-selector certificate; anomaly and extra-mode accounting. |
| next experiment/computation | For one fixed branch, compute the full projected source equation and provenance-tag every right-hand-side term: source-derived, QFT import, finite-control import, residual, or control. |
| progress signal | A right-hand-side term survives with source provenance, conservation follows from the same branch, and replacement tests do not reproduce the result for arbitrary target matter. |
| rollback/failure condition | Any essential matter/stress-energy term is imported, target-fitted, nonconserved, anomalous, or moved from the left side to the right side only to save a GR solution. |

### 3.2 `MARBLE_INTERROGATION`: Question The Marble

**What it can do.** It can prevent the repo from assuming the 4D metric/geometric left side
is final. It makes `g = s^* g_Y` a shadow to be certified, asks whether the Einstein tensor
is an emergent projection, and forces exact GR recovery to be proved rather than inherited.

**What it cannot claim.** It cannot claim that GR recovery is optional. Questioning the
marble does not erase the empirical success of exact GR, black-hole metrics, weak-field
tests, PPN limits, gravitational radiation, or cosmology.

| field | content |
|---|---|
| allowed use | Treat the observer metric, Einstein tensor, connection, and macroscopic geometry as outputs of source fields and section variation. |
| forbidden claim | "The marble is premature, therefore exact GR recovery is unnecessary" or "weak-field compatibility is exact GR." |
| proof debt | `GRShadowRecoveryCertificate`; full `S_GU`; fixed IG variation branch; full `E_s,E_A,E_IG,E_Psi` tuple; exact Schwarzschild and Kerr witnesses; macroscopic/PPN limit. |
| next experiment/computation | Attempt `ELProjectedGRShadowTheorem(branch)` for exact Schwarzschild before Kerr, with no hidden matter relabeling and with the same branch later used for FLRW. |
| progress signal | The source EL tuple projects to Einstein's equation with fixed constants and exact vacuum witnesses, while the metric remains an extracted section shadow. |
| rollback/failure condition | Schwarzschild or Kerr fails the full branch-fixed vacuum EL tuple, or the pass requires an imported Einstein-Hilbert sector, target-fitted cross terms, or hidden stress-energy. |

### 3.3 `LAMBDA_PATCH_DYNAMIC_SOURCE`: Replace The Patch

**What it can do.** It can keep the special cosmological-constant/dark-energy problem
open as neither ordinary wood nor clean marble. The current repo may ask whether a
source-geometric term, such as a constrained or dynamical IG/theta contribution, can replace
bare `Lambda g_mu nu` or a phenomenological dark-energy fluid.

**What it cannot claim.** It cannot claim that GU derives, cancels, screens, or explains
`Lambda`. Current repo status is open: the theta/xi branches have not generated the needed
dynamic coefficient, free beta kills nonzero theta, and no branch has supplied a
source-derived dark-energy stress tensor or geometric correction.

| field | content |
|---|---|
| allowed use | Treat `Lambda g_mu nu` as a suspicious geometric-looking patch and test dynamic source-geometric replacements such as branch-fixed theta/IG contributions. |
| forbidden claim | "GU derives/cancels Lambda" or "GU explains dark energy" when the route uses a bare constant, fitted `xi_eff`, phenomenological fluid, or target-tuned potential. |
| proof debt | `LambdaDarkEnergyProvenanceCertificate`; branch-fixed scalar/tensor mode; generated `Z_theta` and `C_Rtheta`; canonical `xi_eff = C_Rtheta/Z_theta`; conservation and exact-GR compatibility; DESI/Rubin anti-fitting test. |
| next experiment/computation | In one written branch, compute the dark-energy candidate term from the same `S_GU` used for GR, then test whether the sign and magnitude survive with no DESI/Rubin target value, no bare `Lambda`, and no fitted `xi_eff`. |
| progress signal | A dynamic term is source-derived, conserved, compatible with the GR shadow certificate, and stable under target replacement or withheld-target tests. |
| rollback/failure condition | Roll back to phenomenological control if the branch inserts bare `Lambda`, chooses `xi_eff` after seeing the target, imports a dark-energy fluid, fits a potential to DESI/Rubin windows, or loses exact-GR/QFT consistency. |

### 3.4 `SOURCE_DERIVE_BOTH`: Derive Marble And Wood From Source

**What it can do.** It is the strongest GU avenue. It can unify the marble/wood split by
specifying a source object and reduction tuple:

```text
G_src -> (R_GR, R_QFT, R_SM, R_MEAS, R_RECORD)
```

Then GR geometry, QFT states, matter/gauge data, finite measurement records, and claim
certificates become shadows with provenance.

**What it cannot claim.** It cannot claim success until at least one branch supplies both a
true GR shadow certificate and a QFT/matter/measurement shadow certificate. A named source
geometry without reductions is an interface placeholder.

| field | content |
|---|---|
| allowed use | Use `I_GU` as the primary typed contract and require source-to-shadow certificates for GR, QFT, matter/gauge, measurement, and records. |
| forbidden claim | "Source geometry solves the marble/wood problem" before it derives both observer metric equations and observer-facing quantum/matter structures. |
| proof debt | Source-closed `D_GU` and `S_GU`; fixed variation space; `R_GR` projection; dark-energy provenance if a Lambda replacement is claimed; `QFTStateSpaceExtractionCertificate`; `QFTStateExtractionCertificate`; `ObservableAdmissibilityCertificate`; `Phi_SG_MG`; measurement channel. |
| next experiment/computation | Build `SOURCE_OBJECT_V0 + REDUCTION_PACKET_V0` for one branch: exact Schwarzschild attempt, Lambda/dark-energy provenance check if present, plus one two-point/quasifree QFT state extraction or finite `rho_AB` extraction. |
| progress signal | The same branch produces a computable GR EL projection and a positive state/observable structure without importing target GR/QFT/SM data. |
| rollback/failure condition | No stable source object can be written, reductions import their outputs, QFT state space is absent, or exact GR and QFT shadows require incompatible branches. |

### 3.5 `METRIC_QUANTIZATION_DOWNSTREAM`: Keep Metric Quantization As Comparison

**What it can do.** It can compare GU shadows with conventional quantum-gravity language:
linearized gravitons, effective metric fluctuations, semiclassical limits, path-integral
or algebraic QFT-on-background approximations, and known no-go hypothesis classes.

**What it cannot claim.** It cannot be the primary definition of GU, and it cannot be used
to say that avoiding metric quantization solves quantum recovery. If adopted as a branch,
it owes its own quantum consistency and recovery checks.

| field | content |
|---|---|
| allowed use | Downstream comparison after `R_GR` or `R_QFT` supplies an observer metric/QFT shadow; conventional QG no-go map and effective-theory benchmark. |
| forbidden claim | "Metric quantization is solved/irrelevant because GU starts elsewhere" or "not quantizing the metric recovers QFT." |
| proof debt | Effective metric fluctuation map; relation between source modes and spin-2/graviton degrees if claimed; unitarity/positivity; locality; anomaly and measurement compatibility. |
| next experiment/computation | After a branch-fixed `R_GR` exists, linearize the source-to-metric map and compare its effective fluctuation sector to ordinary spin-2 expectations and known QFT consistency gates. |
| progress signal | Metric quantization appears as a derived effective shadow or is cleanly shown not to be the right downstream approximation for that branch. |
| rollback/failure condition | The comparison branch becomes a hidden primary input, imports ordinary quantum gravity structure, or is cited as recovery without state space, observables, dynamics, and positivity. |

### 3.6 `COMPACT_FINITE_CONTROLS`: Use Compact/Finite Controls Honestly

**What it can do.** It can preserve useful finite and compact computations: K3 index
formulas, finite Connes checks, Pati-Salam branching, Higgs-slot hosting, Bell/CHSH
fixtures, and Type II_1 host/selector negative filters. These are valuable test harnesses.

**What it cannot claim.** It cannot claim physical GU evidence unless the source-to-control
transport map is proved. K3 is not a physical generation theorem without the same-operator
`Y^14 -> K3` bridge. Bell states are not GU measurements without `rho_AB` and admissible
observables. Finite Connes data are not selected if `A_F` is imported.

| field | content |
|---|---|
| allowed use | Compact arithmetic, finite fixtures, executable controls, branch sanity checks, and loss/provenance accounting. |
| forbidden claim | "K3 gives three generations," "finite Connes data are selected," "Pati-Salam labels are QFT states," or "Bell ansatz states are GU-derived." |
| proof debt | Physical `RS_GU^phys`; tau-discrete/weighted Fredholm bridge; right-`H` certificate; APS/end corrections; target-free `A_F` or gauge selector; GU state and measurement channel. |
| next experiment/computation | Derive `RS_GU^phys` from the actual source operator/action and choose either an H-unitary discrete bridge or APS bridge with corrections; separately keep CHSH `--require-gu` failing until real GU inputs exist. |
| progress signal | A compact or finite control receives a provenance-preserving bridge from source data and continues to pass replacement/anti-smuggling checks. |
| rollback/failure condition | The control imports target values, loses noncompact end data, lacks H-linearity, ignores APS corrections, works for arbitrary `n`, or uses ansatz/control states as GU evidence. |

### 3.7 `ORDINARY_GR_QFT_IMPORT_CONTROLS`: Treat Standard Imports As Controls

**What it can do.** It can use ordinary GR and QFT as calibration surfaces: exact
Schwarzschild/Kerr, FLRW, PPN, ordinary SM anomaly cancellation after a shadow is selected,
known QFT state/observable axioms, Born probabilities, spin-statistics, and locality.

**What it cannot claim.** It cannot treat imported GR or QFT machinery as GU derivation.
Standard formulas may be baselines, targets, and audits, but they do not close the source
reduction unless their provenance is GU-derived.

| field | content |
|---|---|
| allowed use | Use ordinary GR/QFT to define target checks, negative controls, imported baselines, and falsification surfaces. |
| forbidden claim | "GU recovers GR/QFT" when the proof merely assumes Einstein equations, a Hilbert space, a vacuum state, Born rule, ordinary SM matter, or anomaly-free content. |
| proof debt | Source-to-shadow replacement for every imported baseline: metric equation, state space, state, observables, dynamics, anomaly mode list, measurement rule. |
| next experiment/computation | Add provenance tags to any ordinary GR/QFT object used in a branch computation: `derived`, `imported_control`, `target_check`, `hosted`, or `failed`. |
| progress signal | Imported structures remain visibly labeled and are later replaced by source-derived certificates or used to falsify a branch. |
| rollback/failure condition | Baseline formulas silently become premises of a GU proof, or "compatibility with" ordinary GR/QFT drifts into "recovered from" source geometry. |

### 3.8 `CLAIM_GOVERNANCE_INTERFACE`: Local-Minimum Guard

**What it can do.** It can keep all open avenues live by forcing each claim to carry
dependencies, forbidden inputs, proof grade, citation language, route id, and rollback
condition. This is not physics evidence. It is the interface discipline that prevents
route collapse.

**What it cannot claim.** It cannot prove GR, QFT, matter selection, generation count, or
measurement. Governance is useful only when it changes a citation, blocks a forbidden
promotion, or names the next proof object.

| field | content |
|---|---|
| allowed use | Attach route ids and certificates to frontier-run outputs; block local-minimum collapse by requiring at least one opposing route to be considered. |
| forbidden claim | "The ledger/finality process proves the physics" or "all open routes are equally plausible." |
| proof debt | Machine-readable claim certificates; route-level audit; integration review that checks overclaiming, route coverage, and rollback language. |
| next experiment/computation | In the next five-lane run, require every lane prompt to declare whether it is repairing wood, questioning marble, deriving both, running a downstream QG comparison, using compact controls, or using standard controls. |
| progress signal | A future lane demotes, promotes conditionally, fails, or blocks a claim with exact citation language and a named missing object. |
| rollback/failure condition | The process becomes ceremony, routes are merged by prose, or audits pass documents that contain no decision, proof debt, or failure condition. |

## 4. Hegelian Synthesis

| route | steelman thesis | opposing antithesis | synthesis: what survives |
|---|---|---|---|
| `WOOD_REPAIR_WITHIN_SHADOW_GR` | The wood side is the visible defect, so derive better matter/source terms first. | Fixing only the right side leaves the geometric marble unexamined and may merely import QFT. | Keep wood repair as a controlled GR-shadow task, but require source provenance and conservation for every right-hand-side term. |
| `MARBLE_INTERROGATION` | The elegant metric left side may be a shadow, so treating it as fundamental may be the local minimum. | Rejecting the marble too early risks losing the best-tested part of physics. | Question the metric as primary, but make exact GR recovery harder, not optional. |
| `LAMBDA_PATCH_DYNAMIC_SOURCE` | `Lambda g_mu nu` looks geometric but may be a fixed patch smuggled into the marble side. | Treating every dark-energy term as suspicious can become target denial or fitted-theta speculation. | Keep Lambda/dark-energy as its own route: no bare constant or fitted `xi_eff` may count unless a source-derived dynamic term passes provenance and anti-fitting tests. |
| `SOURCE_DERIVE_BOTH` | A deeper source geometry could recover both sides as shadows and dissolve the split. | "Deeper source" can become a slogan that evades both GR and QFT obligations. | Promote source geometry only as a certificate stack: source object, reduction maps, provenance, recovery checks, and rollback. |
| `METRIC_QUANTIZATION_DOWNSTREAM` | GU need not start by quantizing the 4D metric. | Avoiding metric-first quantization does not recover quantum theory. | Keep metric quantization as an effective comparison after source-to-shadow maps exist. |
| `COMPACT_FINITE_CONTROLS` | Compact and finite models give exact arithmetic and executable gates. | They can smuggle target data or forget noncompact/source information. | Use them as controls until a lossless or corrected source-to-control transport certificate is proved. |
| `ORDINARY_GR_QFT_IMPORT_CONTROLS` | Standard GR/QFT are the empirical languages the shadows must match. | Importing them can fake recovery. | Use ordinary structures as labeled controls and target checks, never as untagged GU outputs. |
| `CLAIM_GOVERNANCE_INTERFACE` | Route/finality discipline prevents stale overclaiming. | Governance can become paperwork instead of mathematics. | Keep governance only where it changes a decision, blocks a forbidden citation, or specifies the next proof object. |

The synthesis is not "choose the source-geometry route and close the rest." The synthesis is:

```text
Treat source geometry as the central candidate contract, while keeping every other route
as either a repair lane, opposition lane, downstream comparison, control, or audit surface.
```

That is how the repo avoids a local minimum after the marble/wood insight.

## 5. Update To The Next Standard Five-Lane Run Logic

The next five-lane frontier run should not spawn five versions of the same intuition. It
should allocate lanes by route role.

Recommended run logic update:

| rule | update |
|---|---|
| Route declaration | Every lane prompt should name its route id from this ledger and say what it is allowed to promote, demote, block, or falsify. |
| Opposition requirement | At least one lane should pressure the marble side and at least one should pressure the wood/source side, unless a prior sequential result forces a narrower run. |
| Source-first burden | Any lane using the source-geometry route must emit a source-to-shadow certificate field, not only ontology. |
| Control honesty | K3, finite Connes, Pati-Salam, Bell, and ordinary GR/QFT lanes must declare `control_only`, `imported_control`, or `derived` status for each key datum. |
| Metric quantization placement | A metric-quantization lane is allowed only as downstream comparison or explicit branch, with QFT recovery obligations preserved. |
| Lambda/dark-energy placement | A dark-energy lane must distinguish bare `Lambda`, fitted `xi_eff`, phenomenological fluids, and source-derived dynamic terms, with DESI/Rubin target values withheld from the derivation. |
| Anti-smuggling audit | Every route that touches matter, gauge, generation count, or CHSH should list forbidden target inputs and replacement tests. |
| Failure as progress | A lane that cleanly rolls back a route is a successful frontier result. Do not force every lane toward positive recovery. |

Suggested five-lane template for the next run:

|---|---|---|
| one exact-source lane | `SOURCE_DERIVE_BOTH` plus `MARBLE_INTERROGATION` | Attempt one branch-fixed `ELProjectedGRShadowTheorem` for Schwarzschild. |

| one Lambda/dark-energy lane | `LAMBDA_PATCH_DYNAMIC_SOURCE` plus `SOURCE_DERIVE_BOTH` | Compute whether a branch-fixed dynamic source term can replace bare `Lambda` without fitted `xi_eff` or DESI/Rubin target insertion. |
| one compact/finite bridge lane | `COMPACT_FINITE_CONTROLS` | Derive `RS_GU^phys` or block K3 promotion with an exact missing object. |
| one selector or governance lane | `WOOD_REPAIR_WITHIN_SHADOW_GR` or `METRIC_QUANTIZATION_DOWNSTREAM` plus `CLAIM_GOVERNANCE_INTERFACE` | Attempt `Phi_SG_G`, compare route statuses, or update citation language without physics by slogan. |

## 7. Next Meaningful Proof/Computation Step

The next useful step is not another global essay and not another slogan about source
geometry. It is one narrow source-to-shadow packet:

```text
SOURCE_OBJECT_V0:
  choose one branch of I_GU,
  write the allowed fields, variation space, D_GU, S_GU, section map,
  source law, boundary/domain data, and forbidden target inputs.

REDUCTION_PACKET_V0:
  R_GR attempt:
    derive the branch-fixed full EL tuple and test exact Schwarzschild first.

  R_DE attempt:
    if the branch produces a Lambda/dark-energy term, classify it as bare Lambda,
    fitted xi_eff, phenomenological fluid, or source-derived dynamic contribution;
    keep DESI/Rubin target values out of the derivation and use them only as
    withheld validation or falsification checks.

  R_QFT attempt:
    derive one positive state-space/state object, preferably a two-point or
    quasifree state extraction, and reduce it to a finite observer state only
    if the provenance is GU-derived.
```

Binary outcomes should be accepted:

| outcome | decision |
|---|---|
| Both reductions are source-derived and compatible in one branch. | Promote only the exact certificates supplied; keep all other shadows open. |
| GR works but QFT state extraction fails. | Keep marble shadow branch-local; do not claim wood recovery. |
| QFT state extraction works but exact GR fails. | Keep quantum shadow branch-local; do not claim GR recovery. |
| A dynamic dark-energy term is source-derived and passes anti-fitting. | Promote only that Lambda/dark-energy certificate; do not claim broad Lambda cancellation. |
| The dark-energy term is bare `Lambda`, fitted `xi_eff`, or a phenomenological fluid. | Reclassify it as patch/control/import, not GU derivation. |
| Both fail cleanly. | Demote the branch and preserve other routes. |
| Either pass requires imported target data. | Reclassify the result as control/import, not derivation. |

This is the first computation that can actually move the marble/wood ledger: it tests
whether one source branch can begin to recover both sides without pretending either side is
already solved.
