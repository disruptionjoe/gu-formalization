## 1. Does GU's structure SUPPORT a two-time boundary formulation? -- YES, and it is natural not added

Three independent GU facts converge on **two-sided boundary data**, and none of
them was introduced to support the two-time story:

**(i) The ultrahyperbolic `(9,5)` arena has no clean Cauchy problem.** This is a
theorem-grade fact about the signature, not a GU-specific hope. Ultrahyperbolic
PDEs (more than one timelike direction) are the classical example of equations
that are **ill-posed as initial-value problems** -- initial data on a
hypersurface does not determine the solution with continuous dependence
(Hadamard). What such equations admit instead is **boundary data on a two-sided
boundary**. So the moment GU commits to `(9,5)`, "boundary conditions, not initial
conditions" is forced, and "two-sided" is the generic shape of admissible boundary
data.

**(ii) Weinstein states exactly this, unprompted, in the primary corpus.** Prong A
(source-extraction) logs it verbatim: TOE-40yrs `[01:17:10]` -- *"if I think
upstairs on the 14 manifold ... there are no initial conditions because that's a
co-dimension one concept. Really what you have is boundary conditions. And now good
luck."* The author names the **need** (boundary, not initial) and disclaims the
**specification** ("good luck", the ultrahyperbolic Cauchy problem "not stuff I
know"). This is the source-owned half of the two-time reading: the *type* of data
is stated (boundary), the *selector* is absent (the `S-SILENT-OPEN` gap).

**(iii) Prong 0 exhibits a genuine finite boundary the operator cannot cross.** The
`q<0`, `K_S`-null stratum (~8% of genuine flat fiber ends, on and off the Weyl
slice) is where the K-definite section cut **does not exist**, so the operator
`A~` is **not constructible from committed structure at all** -- "not merely
non-self-adjoint but not constructed." That is precisely a **boundary/horizon
locus**, not a bulk point: the natural home for a boundary condition rather than an
initial condition. Member 3 reads it as the **genesis seam**; the orthodox reading
(Member 1) reads the same locus as an **APS boundary** where a Dirac-type operator
is not self-adjoint until a boundary condition is imposed. Both readings agree on
the structural fact: **there is a genuine boundary here demanding boundary data.**

**The sharpener (probe Q-SUPPORT).** To check that "two-sided boundary data is
natural for GU's signature" is a real structural claim and not a slogan, the probe
runs the two mirror regimes on a linear mode operator and measures the
**invertibility margin** (`min|eigenvalue|` of the two-point BVP operator = its
distance from singular; the raw condition number is a discretization artifact that
grows with `N` for both regimes and is deliberately NOT used):

| regime | mode eq. | IVP (initial data) | two-time BVP (initial + final) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **elliptic-in-time** (ultrahyperbolic `(9,5)` proxy) | `x'' = +kappa^2 x` | amplification `e^{kappa T} = 4.03e2` -- **ill-posed (Hadamard)** | margin `= 45.9`, bounded from 0 -- **well-posed** |
| **hyperbolic** (ordinary single-time) | `x'' = -omega^2 x` | amplification `= 1` -- **well-posed** | margin `= 0.089` (near a Dirichlet resonance) -- **over-determining / near-singular** |

The two regimes are exact mirror images: **two-sided boundary data is the natural
well-posed problem exactly where the initial-value problem fails (the elliptic /
no-Cauchy directions GU has), and it OVER-determines ordinary single-time hyperbolic
evolution** (once the initial data is fixed the final value is already determined,
so an independent final condition is redundant-or-contradictory, and the two-point
operator is singular at the resonances `omega = k pi / T`). This rejects the
**planted control PLANT-1** ("two-time is natural for any signature") -- it is not;
it is natural *specifically* for GU's signature.

**Honest scope of the support.** What GU supports at this grade is a **two-SIDED
boundary-value formulation**. That "two-time in the ultrahyperbolic sense" (data at
both ends of the timelike directions) is not automatically "two-time in the
Gell-Mann--Hartle / Aharonov cosmological sense" (a **final cosmological time**
condition producing future-conditioned *statistics*). The former is owned; the
latter requires projecting the 14-manifold's fiber-boundary structure down to a

## 2. The decisive discriminator: does two-time make a DISTINCT prediction, or is it LambdaCDM in disguise?

Prong C's control (prereg): a two-time story that predicts nothing new is `C-SCAFFOLD`
at best, not `C-FALSIFIABLE`. So the load-bearing question is whether a
future-conditioned (two-boundary) field is **observationally distinct** from a
one-boundary (initial-condition, LambdaCDM-analog) field.

**The probe answers this cleanly (Q-DISTINCT, Q-WASHOUT).** Take a Gaussian field
with action `(1/2) x^T L x`, precision `L = A2 + kappa^2 I` (the natural elliptic
regime); its two-point function `<x_i x_j>` is the Green's function *with the imposed
boundary conditions*. On a **fixed present-day window** `[0, T_now]`, compare:

- **one-boundary** (Dirichlet at `t=0`, free at the far end): `G(t,t') = sinh(kappa
  t_<) e^{-kappa t_>} / kappa` -- the half-line Green's function, **no dependence on
  any final time.** This is the LambdaCDM analog: present statistics set by the past
  boundary only.
- **two-boundary** (Dirichlet at `t=0` **and** at `t=T_final`): `G(t,t') = sinh(kappa
  t_<) sinh(kappa (T_final - t_>)) / (kappa sinh(kappa T_final))` -- this **knows
  about `T_final`**: the correlation is pinned toward zero as either time approaches
  the final boundary. That is a **future-conditioned correlation the one-boundary
  field cannot produce.**

**Q-DISTINCT = True.** At a finite final boundary just beyond the window
(`T_final = 1.2`), `max|C2 - C1| = 2.7e-2` on the present window, and the
self-correlation at the last in-window point is suppressed (`0.139` vs `0.166`) --
a concrete future-conditioned signature. **So YES, in principle a two-time field
predicts something a one-time field forbids.**

**Q-WASHOUT = True -- and this is what pins the tier.** Recede the final boundary and
re-measure the same present window:

| `T_final` | `max|C2 - C1|` on the fixed present window |
|---|---|
| 1.2 | `2.73e-2` |
| 1.5 | `4.51e-3` |
| 2.0 | `2.25e-4` |
| 2.5 | `1.12e-5` |
| 3.0 | `5.57e-7` |
| 3.5 | `2.77e-8` |
| 4.0 | `1.38e-9` |

Fitted decay `~ exp(-6.00 * T_final)`, matching theory `-2 kappa = -6.00` to three
figures. **The distinct correlation decays exponentially as the final boundary
recedes.** As `T_final -> infinity` (a final condition at the de Sitter far future,
the generic cosmological choice) the two-time field becomes **observationally
identical to the one-boundary LambdaCDM field** at any fixed present-day separation.
This rejects the **planted control PLANT-2** ("two-time always makes a distinct
observable") -- it does not; the distinctness lives entirely in a **finite, fixed
final-boundary locus.**

**The consequence for the tier.** A distinct third-person-visible prediction exists
**iff** GU supplies a **finite, structurally-fixed final-boundary locus.** GU has a
*candidate* for such a locus -- the null stratum is a genuine **finite** boundary
(not at infinity), which is exactly what a non-washed-out future condition needs.
But the null stratum is a locus of the **fiber** (`GL(4,R)/O(3,1)` timelike-dominant
flat ends), not a cosmological time slice. Fixing `z*` from it is the **unowned
bridge**. Absent that bridge, the two-time posit is a scaffold, not a falsifiable:
it is either washed out (final boundary at infinity) or its imprint scale is unfixed.

  FALSIFIABLE`): a construction-grade fiber-null-stratum `->` finite-`z*` map (the
  Prong-A domain-selector gap, carried to a cosmological locus).
