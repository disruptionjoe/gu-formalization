# Stub: the missing count as the universe's boundary axiom

## The riff (Joe, plain form)

Physics splits into laws (inference rules) and initial conditions (the state the rules act on).
The initial condition is an **axiom, not a theorem**: no amount of running the laws derives the
starting point. Godel-flavored reading: there are truths (the actual boundary data of the universe)
that the laws cannot prove but that must be part of any complete account -- and every attempt to
promote the initial condition INTO the laws (no-boundary, Weyl-curvature hypothesis, CPT-mirror)
just adds an axiom whose own independence recurs, second-incompleteness style. "Perhaps there's
something in the space between proving with the laws and proving with some other method."

## The typing this suggests for the program

  generation count is *located, not forced*: the interior names the order-3 slot and provably
  cannot fill it ("external by structure"). This stub proposes the external datum's TYPE: an
  **initial-condition-class axiom** -- unprovable from the interior laws, checkable against them
  (witness-verification shape: cosmology as NP-witness checking, not derivation).
- **Second-theorem shape, third instance.** (i) Godel II: no system certifies its own consistency
  from within. (ii) The firewall arithmetic: the interior cannot mint its own count certificate.
  (iii) Initial conditions: the laws cannot derive their own boundary data. Same architecture,

## Honest boundary

"The laws of physics" are not (yet) an effectively axiomatized formal system, so the Godel mapping
is heuristic; the rigorous outposts are the spectral-gap undecidability result, MIP* = RE, and this
repo's own firewall arithmetic. The initial-conditions independence alone is generic
underdetermination (weaker than incompleteness); the Godel-grade content is the self-certification
regress and the located-not-mintable certificate shape.
