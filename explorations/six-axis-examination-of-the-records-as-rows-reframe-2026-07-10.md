## The honest status: SCOPE-EXIT and a generative FRAME, not a disciplined evasion

Three disciplines from the protocol cut it down to honest size:

1. **Scope-exit, not shadow (same grade as GU's Distler-Garibaldi exit).** As stated the reframe says
   "the no-gos don't apply because the object is a sequence model, not their category." That is a
   change-of-unit (L1 scope exit, DG-A7 shaped), NOT a shadow computed within the class. The
   load-bearing open burden is identical to every non-geometric proposal (I12): exhibit, for at least
   one no-go, a smoothing functor from the sequence-model object to that no-go's image. **Absent that
   functor, this is not an evasion — it is a relabeling.** Not a win.

2. **The L4 leg confronts a LIVE in-repo NEGATIVE.** The causal-set leg is not free: TaF's **T223**
   (the uniform finite finality-colimit ensemble does NOT concentrate on manifoldlike causal sets)
   is the sharpest known obstruction to exactly this move. The reframe inherits it. The honest first
   test is the T223 confrontation — run the existing Myrheim-Meyer / ordering-fraction screens on a
   causal-masked attention matrix and ask whether ANY such structure has a Lorentzian large-scale
   limit.

3. **Multi-axis simultaneity is itself the discipline's warned failure mode.** The six-axis method
   exists to ISOLATE the ONE dropped assumption; the L7 doc explicitly warns against "smearing the
   single load-bearing novelty" across axes. A candidate dropping six axes at once smears maximally.
   So the honest classification is: the reframe is a **GENERATIVE FRAME** (it says WHERE to look — the
   unoccupied L2-L7 region — and supplies a unifying object), NOT a disciplined single-axis evasion
   candidate. To become one it must be DECOMPOSED: pick one axis, name the one dropped assumption,
   exhibit its functor/shadow.
