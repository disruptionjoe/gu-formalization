## 4. Survivor-bias disclosure

This method preferentially finds vocabulary that already resembles today's
frontier. It can miss a relevant mechanism expressed in different language,
spread across distant transcript passages, dependent on equations omitted by
the audio, or obscured by transcription errors. Conversely, lexical matches
such as “three,” “cyclic,” “kernel,” “torsion,” and “involution” can look more
specific than their types warrant. Only hit neighborhoods were read; Grok
packs were finding aids at most; no primary paper was reopened; no algebra was
run. Therefore the sweep is high-confidence as a **queue-triage result**, but
low-confidence as evidence that the candidate mechanisms identify with GU
objects. All five findings remain hearsay-grade and non-moving.
