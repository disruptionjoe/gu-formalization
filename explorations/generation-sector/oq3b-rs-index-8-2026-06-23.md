### 3.2 Does L^2(SL(4,R)/SO_0(3,1)) have discrete series?

discrete series. The condition is sufficient, not necessary. We need to examine the question
from a different angle.

**Oshima-Matsuki (1984) Classification.** Oshima and Matsuki classified all reductive
symmetric pairs (G, H) for which L^2(G/H) has a discrete spectrum. Their criterion
(Theorem 6.11 of Oshima-Matsuki 1984) is:

```
disc spectrum exists  iff  rho_H - rho_G is real and dominant for a suitable Borel.
```

For (SL(4,R), SO_0(3,1)):

rho_G = half-sum of positive roots of sl(4,R) = (A3) = (3/2, 1/2, -1/2, -3/2) (in the

so(3,1) has real rank 1 (the non-compact part has rank 1). The real positive roots of
so(3,1) embedded in sl(4,R) via the standard Lorentz embedding are the roots that are
positive restricted roots of so(3,1). The full positive root system of so(3,1) (as a
real Lie algebra of real rank 1) consists of:

  projected onto the restricted root direction.
- The compact roots (roots of so(3) = compact part of so(3,1)): contribute to rho_H
  but vanish on a_q.

The projected rho difference rho_H - rho_G on a_q_B (the rank-3 diagonal Cartan):

For the Oshima-Matsuki condition, this difference must be in the open negative Weyl

**However**, the Oshima-Matsuki criterion applies to the full discrete series, not to

For the (SL(4,R), SO_0(3,1)) case, the relevant theory is that of Kobayashi (1994) on

more than one component, or the constraint count is wrong for the 4D Lorentzian theory),
the chiral RS fiber dimension changes and ind_H != 8.

exactly 1 SM generation's worth of Weyl fermions. This was established in
generation-count-sm-branching-closure (CONDITIONALLY_RESOLVED). If this branching is
