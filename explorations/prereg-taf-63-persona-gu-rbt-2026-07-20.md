# Pre-registration: TaF 63-persona GU roses, buds, and thorns

## Objective

Use every numbered expert persona in the canonical Time as Finality panel as
an independent evaluator of the current GU/Observerse repository. The panel is
imported as a lens system only. Time as Finality supplies the personas; GU owns
the evaluation artifact and every conclusion about GU.

The task is repository/program evaluation, not a vote on whether Geometric
Unity is true. Each persona must identify:

- **rose:** a demonstrated strength worth preserving;
- **bud:** a promising, high-information direction not yet earned;
- **thorn:** a concrete structural risk, invalid inference, missing object, or
  research-process liability.

## Independence contract

No persona may see another persona's response before all 63 individual
responses are complete. Batch agents receive the same common GU brief and
write separate numbered sections. Within a batch they must reset to the named
discipline for each section and may not cite, summarize, or react to another
persona's answer.

Every persona uses the TaF panel's standard structure:

1. summary of understanding;
2. strongest insight;
3. strongest criticism;
4. hidden assumptions;
5. rose;
6. bud;
7. thorn;
8. confidence from 1 to 10;
9. suggested experiment;
10. suggested theorem or mathematical direction; and
11. suggested falsification test.

Each substantive judgment must name a GU evidence surface or explicitly say
that it is a cross-domain analogy. Personas are lenses, not evidence.

## Synthesis gates

Synthesis starts only after coverage checks prove:

- exactly 63 numbered responses;
- every integer 1 through 63 appears once;
- every response contains rose, bud, thorn, confidence, experiment, theorem
  direction, and falsifier;
- no batch contains a pre-synthesis cross-persona conclusion; and
- no claim-status or canon movement occurred.

The synthesis must preserve:

- convergences supported across at least four genuinely different
  disciplinary clusters;
- informed minority objections;
- disagreements caused by using different construction forks;
- the strongest warning;
- the strongest next executable goal; and
- a bounded claim-status recommendation.

No majority vote can promote a physics or mathematics claim. Quadratic voting
is omitted unless a genuine choice among already-typed next actions remains
after evidence synthesis.

## Predeclared outcomes

- `RBT-COHERENT`: the panel identifies a small, cross-disciplinary set of
  strengths, opportunities, and risks with executable follow-ups.
- `RBT-FRAGMENTED`: the panel's recommendations depend strongly on discipline
  or construction fork and no unified priority is justified.
- `RBT-THORN-DOMINANT`: a cross-disciplinary liability invalidates the current
  research posture or top work program at its stated grade.
- `RBT-BUD-DOMINANT`: a new high-information direction survives hostile lenses
  and outranks the current next move.
- `RBT-METHOD-OBSTRUCTION`: the repo is too large, stale, or internally
  inconsistent for the frozen panel brief to support honest evaluation.

This is Lane 1/Lane 3 diagnostic work: it evaluates truth-testing strategy and
hardens scope. It does not alter Lane state, scientific verdicts, canon, or
public posture.
