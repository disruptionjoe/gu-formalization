# Comparative-tensions ledger — cosmology + gravity

**What this is.** A fair landscape for one cluster of physics — cosmology and
gravity — that positions GU honestly against the major theories. For each theory
we catalog three things: (1) its **earned keep** — what it genuinely, uniquely
predicts that is confirmed / new / useful, with **confirmed** vs **contested**
split; (2) its **tensions** — anomalies, open problems, failures; (3) **how GU
compares** — `BETTER` / `TIES` / `SILENT` / `WORSE` with a one-line why.

**The discipline that makes it fair (and keeps GU honest).** GU is **narrow**. On
the established repo grades it is exactly two things in this cluster:

   crossing, ever (Prediction Packet 1, frozen internal, toy/symbol grade,

   locked to the external orientation bit `sigma`.
2. **A parsimony-on-deep-joints argument** — the QM Krein sign, the GR vacuum-
   cancellation sign, the DE sign, and the arrow of time are **one external `Z/2`
   bit `sigma`**, where the standard patchwork carries four separate brute facts.
   Of those four, **exactly one (the DE sign) is observable**; the other three are
   internal unification, not independent evidence.

**Everything else in cosmology/gravity, GU is SILENT or WORSE on**, and this
ledger says so in most cells. GU derives **no** absolute dark-energy amplitude
(`PRED-NORM-RANK = RESOLVED_NO_GO`; the ~10^-120 magnitude is a pure import into
the blocked B5 source-action slot), has **no built dynamical Einstein recovery**

**Verdict key.** `BETTER` = GU renders non-brute / predicts something the rival
leaves brute or free, at equal coverage. `TIES` = same standing / same open
problem / same brute fact. `SILENT` = GU does not address this domain (the
majority). `WORSE` = the rival genuinely predicts or fits here and GU does not
compete or is disadvantaged. All GU grades are consumed from the inputs.

### 5.3 The Weinberg self-tuning no-go — stated, and assessed as class-relative

**Statement (Weinberg 1989).** No dynamical adjustment mechanism in which a
*finite number of local fields* relaxes the effective cosmological constant to
zero can do so **without fine-tuning**, *under the assumptions*: (i) 4D general
covariance, (ii) a finite set of local fields, (iii) a **static equilibrium** with
(iv) **constant field values** at that equilibrium. At such a stationary point the
field equations over-determine the vacuum energy — demanding it vanish is itself a
tuning. This is the classic obstruction every self-tuning proposal must face.

**Is it class-relative? YES — and this is decisive for Joe's idea.** The no-go's
teeth are entirely in assumptions (iii)-(iv) (static, constant-field equilibrium)
and (ii) (local, finite fields). Known mechanisms evade the **letter** of the
theorem by violating exactly those:

- **Fab-Four / Horndeski self-tuning** gives up the *static* assumption — the
  scalar **never fully settles** (a runaway/rolling attractor), and Minkowski is a
  solution for any `Lambda`. Weinberg (iii) violated.
- **Vacuum sequestering** introduces **global / non-local** variables — not local
  finite fields. Weinberg (ii) violated.
- **Cosmological relaxation** uses a **dynamical scan with backreaction** rather
  than a static stationary point. Weinberg (iii) violated.

So the Weinberg no-go is a **class-relative no-go**: it forbids a *static, local,
constant-field self-adjuster*, **not** every conceivable feedback loop. This is
exactly the repo's own standing posture ("a no-go may be class-relative — check
the tool's frame before propagating 'impossible'"). **Crucial honesty:** evading
the *letter* is **not** *solving* the CC problem — the evading mechanisms are
contested and reintroduce subtler tuning/coincidence. The no-go is a real bar that
a feedback/PID version *can* clear in principle, and that none has cleanly cleared
in practice.

### 5.4 Joe's PID / feedback dark energy — could `Lambda` be a control-loop output?

**The idea.** `Lambda` is not a constant but the **output of a feedback (PID)
controller** whose loop drives the vacuum-energy amplitude to a small value.

**(a) Does it plausibly close the `Lambda`-magnitude fine-tuning via a self-tuning
attractor?** *Partially, and only in the same sense the existing self-tuning
attractors do.* A feedback controller driving an error signal to zero **is** a
self-tuning attractor — the same object as Fab-Four/relaxation, viewed through
control theory. Such attractors demonstrably exist and hit small/zero `Lambda`
without per-vacuum tuning. **But** a PID loop has a **setpoint**, and to land the
*observed small-but-nonzero* ~meV value you must explain why the loop's fixed
point (or its residual) sits at ~meV rather than exactly zero — which is the
magnitude/coincidence problem **re-expressed as a property of the loop**. So the
idea plausibly converts "why is `Lambda` ~10^-120" into "why is the controller's
attractor at ~meV" — genuine *reframing*, respectable and live, but **not an
obvious elimination** of the tuning. Grade: **legitimate member of the self-tuning
class; not a solution, a relocation.**

**(b) Weinberg's no-go for it:** as in 5.3 — **class-relative**. A *dynamical*
feedback controller (never-static, or with backreaction/global structure) can
evade the letter of Weinberg exactly as Fab-Four/sequestering/relaxation do. It is
**not** forbidden a priori. It is *also* not thereby solved — it inherits the whole
class's residual-tuning problem.

**(c) How GU's `sigma` sign-lock constrains any such controller — GU's one genuine
value-add.** GU forbids `w < -1` **pointwise, ever** — a **hard one-sided rail at
`w = -1`, sign-locked to `sigma`** (PP1; the readout identity `rho_DE + p_DE =
eps * Z * Bdot^2` with `eps` fixed by orientation, and `tau = +1` the only value
with the GR cancellation alive — there is *no* configuration with a phantom-side
mode and a surviving cancellation). In control-theory terms, **crossing `w = -1`
is the DE-sector analog of the controller overshooting its setpoint** — and a
**generic PID controller overshoots** (integral action in particular drives
overshoot / limit cycles). **GU forbids the overshoot.** So *if GU is right*, any
feedback-DE controller must be a **sign-locked, no-overshoot, one-sided** loop that
approaches the rail from the quintessence side and **never crosses**. That is a
sharp, falsifiable constraint on the *entire controller class* — and it is the
**same DESI/Euclid kill test** (a robust pointwise `w < -1` crossing kills both the
GU sign-lock and the "GU-compatible sign-locked controller").

**What is GU vs what is a NEW hypothesis (the honest split).**
