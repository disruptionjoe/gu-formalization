# Can a native map connect the Krein sign to finality polarity?

## Corrected question

The previous formulation began by calling the remaining GU bit the orientation
of issuance or finality. That assigned the desired interpretation before the
adapter was proved.

The honest question is:

> Does one source-owned construction produce a non-constant,
> branch-preserving map between the GU Krein-sign torsor and an independently
> defined TI or TaF polarity object, and does a physical principle anchor one
> target branch to positive norm?

## What the neighboring results actually provide

- TI E155 provides a finite extension-confluence and fork-locus result.
- TI E160 provides the shape of a `Z/2` transport functor only when the
  source-side admissibility rule already carries a composition-compatible
  parity label. Bare loops do not derive it.
- TaF T39 provides a binary same/different CSP whose obstruction is signed-graph
  parity conflict.
- TaF T18 provides a conditional D1-monotone direction theorem.
- TaF H7 weakens its physical-arrow reading, and T57 leaves the relevant
  direction circularity open.

These results share useful forms. They do not yet define one common native
object or identify a sign value.

## Current obstruction

The simplest profile map forgets the fork: opposite proposition values have
the same D1 profile. A D1Field-style target can retain proposition values, but
then the values form an additional discrete fiber over the profile. A global
flip exchanges two equally admissible anchors.

## Positive-result burden

A positive answer requires:

1. exact source and target categories owned by their respective repositories;
2. an explicit non-constant functor preserving identities, composition, and
   the polarity fork;
3. an independent reason the target polarity is finality-native rather than an
   imported label;
4. a physical direction surviving TaF's H7 absorbers;
5. a map to the actual GU Krein-positive branch; and
6. a prediction or invariant that changes if the identification is wrong.

Until then, the similarity remains a candidate bridge. `bar(b)` and `H59`
remain open.
