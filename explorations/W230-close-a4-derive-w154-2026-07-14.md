## 1. Persona 1 -- first-principles-derivation specialist: the identity is dynamical, not kinematic

Ask the cleanest question: is `theta = J` an **identity of functionals** (true for all field
configurations, hence derivable) or a **field equation** (true only on a constraint surface, hence
resting on the action that defines that surface)? It is the latter, and the proof is one line of
kind-counting reproduced from W160 (block `[MISMATCH]`): `J[Psi]` **changes** under `Psi -> Psi'`
(it is a matter bilinear), while the bare distortion `theta = pi - eps^{-1} B eps` is built from
connections with **no matter field** and does **not** change under `Psi -> Psi'`. Two functionals
with different variations cannot be equal as functionals. So `theta = J` is not an algebraic identity
that any first-principles manipulation could expose; it can hold only **on** the surface where
`theta` satisfies a field equation that ties it to `Psi`. This is the honest reason a "derivation"
must locate a specific **dynamical** input -- an action -- and cannot be a pure kinematic argument.
It immediately tells us where to look: the coupling term in `theta`'s action.

## 2. Persona 2 -- Noether / current-identification specialist: symmetry leaves a full 14-dim family

equivariant divergence-free family is at least 14-dimensional; an explicit `Psi`-independent member

The identity picks **one ray** out of a 14-dimensional cone. **Noether II / equivariance cannot
single it out** -- it is exactly the "shared by a whole space of currents" point W160 made, now
quantified. This rules out COMPLETED-DERIVED via the symmetry route: the divergence-free MECHANISM is
built (and reproduced here) but it is agnostic to *which* equivariant current is the dark-energy
term. A further input is mandatory.
