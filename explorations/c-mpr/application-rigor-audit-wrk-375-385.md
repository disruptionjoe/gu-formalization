# Application Rigor Audit: WRK-375 Through WRK-385

Date: 2026-05-31

Scope: audit how the other agent applied the C_MPR / wall / 9-tuple synthesis across WRK-375 through WRK-385. This document redirects those applications without deleting the earlier results. Earlier outputs remain useful as exploratory maps, but their load-bearing claims must be reclassified.

## Global Redirect

Across these cards, the shared mistake is layer promotion: a pattern discovered across several research threads was upgraded into a theorem, category, or obstruction wall before the proof obligations were discharged.

The right posture:

- C_MPR is a proposed schema, not a completed category.
- The 9-tuple is a provisional checklist, not a complete invariant.
- BvN is a suggestive obstruction lens, not a universal wall.
- PCP-blindness is an open theorem target, not an established consequence.
- Signed/global readout versus monotone/local provenance is the strongest surviving core.

Every application should be reworded through this stack:

1. Proved or directly checkable.
2. Concrete candidate instantiation.
3. Internal heuristic/checklist.
4. Conjectural bridge.
5. Speculative analogy.

## WRK-375: Six-Axis Framework

Status: mostly salvageable.

What holds:

- The six-axis frame remains useful.
- The L7/C_MPR option is correctly treated as optional in some sections.
- The example material keeps enough caution to be recovered.

Misstep:

- The v2 integration risks treating the 9-tuple as a recommended classifier rather than an experimental overlay.
- "Same functor" language in examples overstates what has been defined.

Redirect:

- Keep the six axes primary.
- Treat the 9-tuple as a temporary stress-test appendix.
- Do not add L7 as a permanent axis until C_MPR has object/morphism/composition laws.
- Replace "same functor" with "analogous projection role" or "candidate readout pattern."

## WRK-376: No-Go Map

Status: useful map, overclaims its bridge.

What holds:

- The sector-by-sector no-go mapping is still valuable.
- The distinction between local constraint satisfaction and global obstruction remains important.

Misstep:

- It treats `phi_local` and `phi_onsite` as the same functor before either functor is formally defined.
- It lets the BvN wall become the deep structural reason for multiple no-go cases.

Redirect:

- Recast the bridge as "similar local-to-global projection pressure" rather than functor identity.
- Use BvN only for contexts that explicitly involve distributive versus non-distributive event/value logic.
- Add a column separating "formal obstruction", "analogy", and "open bridge."

## WRK-377: Type II1 Checklist

Status: high-value, but must be carefully scoped.

What holds:

- The card already contains the right self-correction: if the Type II1 issue is pre-pairing, then the readout-recovery axis may be not applicable.
- The distinction between native II1 structure and bridges into discrete/countable/computational models is essential.

Misstep:

- The Connes-channel pairing is treated too quickly as a C_MPR object with evidence, readout, and observer projection.
- The BvN wall can wrongly appear to apply to Type II1 itself rather than to a proposed reduction/bridge out of Type II1.

Redirect:

- Preserve the three-way split: native II1, bridge into II1, bridge out of II1.
- Mark C_MPR language as speculative unless the evidence object and observer projection are explicitly defined.
- Use BvN only as a possible obstruction to classical/discrete reduction, not as a direct verdict on Type II1 viability.

## WRK-378: Nielsen Protocol / PCP Reframe

Status: promising scaffold, too theorem-shaped.

What holds:

- Nielsen-style protocol thinking remains a productive way to ask what can be certified locally and what requires global coordination.
- The card correctly gravitates toward accumulator/readout/finality structure.

Misstep:

- The chain `C_CALM -> C_PCP -> C_LR-Signed -> C_GW` is presented like a category path before those categories or arrows exist.
- PCP-blindness is treated as if it follows from the architecture.
- Factorization is allowed to stand in for a proof of meaningful recoverability.

Redirect:

- Retire the category-chain claim for now.
- Replace it with an operational test:
  - What is the local accumulator?
  - What is the signed/global readout?
  - What can be certified before finality?
  - What finality, nonlocal witness, or negative contribution is required?
- Treat PCP-blindness as a named open lemma with proof-model requirements.

## WRK-382: Stochastic Parity

Status: interesting analogy, current C_MPR application overreaches.

What holds:

- Stochastic substrates do raise observer/readout/finality questions.
- A parity-style signed or path-dependent readout may be a good concrete test.

Misstep:

- "Constructible in principle" is too strong.
- Sigma-algebra refinement, conditional expectation, and evidence updating can be nonmonotone in the relevant value space.
- Sample-path evidence is not automatically a PCP-style local certificate.

Redirect:

- Treat stochastic C_MPR as a candidate case study.
- First define the filtration/evidence order, observable, and convergence/finality semantics.
- Require a theorem about what refinement preserves or destroys before using CALM or PCP language.

## WRK-384: Sorkin / Causal Set

Status: promising target, but BvN application is too broad.

What holds:

- Causal-set and Sorkin-style substrates are relevant because they put observer-accessible order, locality, and global action/readout in tension.
- The engineering distinction between local propagation and global observable remains valuable.

Misstep:

- Order ideals being distributive does not by itself make the physical value/readout lattice distributive in the BvN-relevant sense.
- Signed real-valued actions or BD-action terms do not automatically induce an orthomodular-lift obstruction.
- The verdict shifts too quickly toward impossibility.

Redirect:

- Separate causal order, observable algebra, action functional, and observer protocol.
- Use BvN only if a concrete event/value logic translation is defined.
- Prefer a bounded-pass test: identify one signed/global causal-set observable and prove what a local observer can or cannot certify.

## WRK-385: RG Universality / Form C

Status: highest-risk application; requires major demotion.

What holds:

- The L5/L6 coupling remains important.
- Distler-Garibaldi-style obstruction deserves to be kept in the no-go and representation-theoretic map.
- Anomaly matching remains a valuable constraint.

Misstep:

- Form C is treated like a clean PCP iff cell.
- Anomaly polynomial matching is not the same as monotone readout recovery.
- RG invariance and local-certificate irreducibility are being conflated.
- Representation multiplicity/distributive structure does not automatically yield a BvN obstruction.

Redirect:

- Keep Form C as "anomaly/constraint matching across scales", not as a PCP theorem.
- Require an explicit accumulator, readout, and locality model before assigning `rr=monotone-reducible`.
- Treat DG/BvN relations as separate possible obstructions until a shared formal category is proven.
- This card should not be public-facing as proof; it should become a counterexample/stress-test card.

## Cross-Card Publication Posture

For public repo scaffolding, do not lead with C_MPR as if the foundation is settled. Lead with bounded, contributable tests:

- Prove signed-readout monotonicity and positive-negative decomposition.
- Build small examples where local monotone evidence fails to recover signed/global readout.
- Find counterexamples where C_MPR language trivializes.
- Formalize one candidate object and one candidate morphism.
- Test whether CALM, PCP, and BvN analogies survive precise definitions.

The highest-value contributor tasks are not broad synthesis essays. They are narrow formal tests that can kill or strengthen the architecture quickly.
