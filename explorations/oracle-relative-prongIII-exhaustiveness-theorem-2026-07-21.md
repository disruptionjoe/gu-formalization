## 1. The exhaustiveness argument at operator grade -- the hostile check

### 1.1 What the fixture-grade defense actually rested on

The Q2 defense-attorney (DEFENSE-FAILS, the kill holds) closed the flagged seam
positively: "unwelded alpha-odd" is not a genuine third horn. Any first-person
handle is (i) welded to sigma -> Horn 1 (presupposition), (ii) alpha-even in the
relevant part -> Horn 2 (blind), or (iii) an independent unsourced coin whose
coherence with sigma is a product-of-odds = alpha-even, fixing the RELATIVE sign
but never the ABSOLUTE value -> relocated Horn 1. Its stated ground: **"the
trichotomy is exhaustive because alpha-parity is a grade-independent Z/2 typing:
no new parity option appears at any grade."** That sentence is the whole promotion
gate. P1 asks whether it is a THEOREM or a lean.

### 1.2 The promotion: exhaustiveness is a completeness statement, not a census

The hostile worry is precise: the fixture argument exhibited only finitely many
first-person features (the record arrow; the TaF retraction). At operator grade
there are infinitely many operators. Could there be an operator-grade alpha-odd
operator that is first-person accessible AND unwelded that the finite census
missed? If yes -> III-REOPENS-Q2.

The answer is no, and the reason is structural, not census-based. At operator
grade `alpha = Ad(U_h)` is an **involutive automorphism** of the reading algebra:
`U_h` implements the K_S-sign flip and `Ad(U_h)^2 = id` (because `U_h^2` is
central -- `U_h = -(e0 e9)(e3 e11)(e4 e12)` is a product of commuting
bivector-rotations, so `U_h^2` is a scalar). An involutive automorphism splits the
WHOLE algebra into its `+1` and `-1` eigenspaces:

> `O = O_even + O_odd`,  `O_even = (O + alpha O)/2`,  `O_odd = (O - alpha O)/2`,
> with `O_even` unique alpha-fixed and `O_odd` unique alpha-negated.

This is **exhaustive by completeness of the eigenspace decomposition** -- every
operator IS one of the two parities plus nothing else. There is no "third parity"
an operator could hide in; the decomposition spans the algebra. So the
exhaustiveness of the trichotomy does not rely on having listed every feature; it
relies on the Z/2 grading being a complete decomposition, which is automatic at
every carrier dimension. **That is exactly what "grade-independent Z/2 typing"
means as a theorem, and it is the promotion.** Probe Part A verifies it on actual
matrices at `n=2` and `n=8`: `Ad(U)^2 = id`; `even + odd = M`; `even ^ odd = 0`
(unique, no middle); the sigma-datum is alpha-odd and swaps fixpoint-freely; and
the exactly-clause `F(M) - F(alpha M) = 2 F_odd(M)` holds with defect `< 1e-9`.

The receipts already carry this at true operator grade (not just my toy):
boundary-law-operator-lift Section 1a records that `alpha`, the label grading, the
G-sequence, the exactly-clause, and the plant exclusion are all
grade-INDEPENDENT identities, machine-EXACT at operator grade in
`operator_grade_end` (`J_op` commutes with the full collar operator, defect 0.0;
deck covariance `U_h N_delta(t) U_h^{-1} = -N_delta(t+1)` pointwise-exact
`4.8e-13 .. 2.7e-12`, "deck-oddness at operator grade is ALGEBRA, not a limit").
So the algebraic skeleton of the exhaustiveness argument is not merely
promotable -- it is already promoted and machine-verified in the operator-grade
receipts.

### 1.3 Horn 2 at operator grade: the internal reading class is alpha-even, blind

The one place an escape could still hide: is every INTERNAL (first-person
accessible) reading map alpha-even at operator grade? If an internal alpha-ODD
reader existed, it would supply sigma and Q2 would reopen. It does not, and the
operator-grade reason is the same one Q2 Section 3 gave, now lifted:

- An alpha-even functional is sigma-BLIND: `Tr(G_even . s) = 0` because the
  product of an even (block-diagonal) operator with the odd (block-off-diagonal)
  sigma-datum is off-diagonal, trace zero -- structural, not numerical (probe
  Part B, 200 draws). Even readings cannot separate `{+s, -s}`; the two sigma
  values are one alpha-orbit and every even functional is constant on it.
- The ONLY functional that reads sigma is alpha-ODD (`Tr(G_odd . s) != 0`) -- but
  an alpha-odd functional is itself a symmetry-BREAKING selection. At operator
  grade this odd reader is precisely the section reading `M_op` (deck-ODD), whose
  very existence as a bounded operator is the OPEN section-theory resolvent
  theorem, and which the boundary law types as the external ORIENTATION DATUM (a
  lift / a section), not an internal map on states. Reading is an even map (empty
  on sigma); selecting is an odd lift (a broken-symmetry section). They are the
  two legs of one parent and do not contradict -- exactly the Q2 DIFFERENT-access
  resolution, and it lifts verbatim because the parity assignment lifts exactly.

So there is no internal alpha-odd reader; the "sigma-reader" is the external datum
itself. Horn 2 holds at operator grade.
