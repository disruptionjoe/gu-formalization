## PART I -- The five divergent-specialist scopings (condensed)

### Lens 1 -- von Neumann / Tomita-Takesaki algebraist

*Question: is there a modular theory for non-definitizable Krein-selfadjoint operators?*

- **What is a theorem.** The modular FLOW half survives a Krein space (Gottschalk 2002: `Delta^{it}` =
  boost, BW analyticity). The modular CONJUGATION half is a theorem **only at rank of indefiniteness 1**
  (Shulman 1997, Pi_1, via the *quasivector* device). GU is `(+64,-64)` per point and a QFT region algebra
  has infinitely many modes -> **infinite rank**, so Shulman does not reach it.
- **Routes scoped.** (a) *Relative / generalized modular theory* -- build `J` from Connes cocycles
  **without a positive weight** (the algebraic Radon-Nikodym structure is metric-independent; W67 T5).
  (b) *Half-sided modular inclusions in the indefinite metric* -- Wiesbrock/Araki-Zsido give the boost
  generator `P >= 0` and the conjugation from an inclusion `N subset M` with a common cyclic-separating
  vector; **but the literature develops this ONLY on Hilbert space** (confirmed 2026-07-13). A Krein
  version is UNwritten. (c) *Ciolli-Longo-Ruzzi / Buchholz-type* algebraic constructions of the modular
  objects from the net alone -- again positive-metric. (d) *Does type III_1 have a resource the
  finite-rank quasivector method lacks?* -- plausibly yes: III_1's modular flow is ergodic and
  state-independent up to cocycle, so the CONJUGATION might be obtainable from the FLOW + a Krein
  Reeh-Schlieder without ever constructing a positive state. **This is the single most promising
  algebraist route** and it coincides with H61's "re-found H63 on the algebraic skeleton" reframe.
- **Finding.** The conjugation is NOT known-impossible (rank-1 theorem exists, no rank>=2 no-go), but every
  standard construction of `J` routes through a positive/definitizing structure the genuine ghost lacks.
  The live opening is to get `J` from the FLOW + inclusion algebra, bypassing the state. **FRONTIER-HARD,
  not dead.**

- **Routes scoped.** (a) *A kappa-quasivector / infinite family of quasivectors* to patch the infinitely

  *Langer generalized resolvents / spectral functions with critical points* -- definitizable operators have
  a spectral function with finitely many critical points; the question is whether GU's `Delta`, though not
  definitizable in general, is definitizable **in the specific class** the induced-`|II|^2` action produces
  (a decidable per-theory criterion, not a general theorem). (c) *Is non-definitizability a genuine
  obstruction or the failure of ONE method?* -- honest answer: it is the failure of the ONLY known method

  method-relative, which is exactly what keeps Branch 2/3 LIVE and Branch 5 (no-go) worth attempting.
- **Finding.** The genuine open is a **definitizability criterion for the region algebra's `Delta` at
  infinite rank** (would convert the residual into a decidable test) or a **kappa-quasivector** extension.
  **FRONTIER-HARD.**
