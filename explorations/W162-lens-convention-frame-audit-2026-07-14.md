## 2. Persona 1 -- BRST/constraint specialist: is the scalaron constrained away? (Q1)

The bare Stelle count (W78) gives `2 + 5 + 1 = 8` propagating DOF, the `+1` being the scalaron,
present iff the `R^2` coefficient is nonzero. The audit question: GU imposes three constraints that
plain fourth-order gravity does not. Could one of them be a second-class constraint that pairs with
the conformal-metric momentum and removes the scalaron?

The answer is no, and it is a **sector** fact, grounded in W131's exact results:

- **`ker Gamma` (the projection `Pi`).** `Pi = I - Gamma^dag Gamma / 14` is an idempotent
  projection on the Rarita-Schwinger bundle `T*Y14 (x) S(9,5)` -- the **record/spinor fiber**
  sector. W131 proved `[nabla, Pi] = 0` exactly (its A1-A3): `Pi` is covariantly constant, so
  `Pi (gamma.nabla) Pi = (gamma.nabla)` restricted to `ker Gamma` and the projected operator
  **acquires no curvature or metric-sector terms from the projection**. `Pi` constrains the record
  field `Psi`; it does not touch the base metric's conformal mode.
- **Soldering.** W131's Persona 2: `Gamma` is parallel for *every* metric-compatible connection;
  the soldering only **selects which** metric connection (its P2). Selecting a connection adds no
  new second-class constraint on the metric-conformal momentum.
- **Krein `K`.** `K = eta_V (x) beta_S` is covariantly constant (`nabla K = 0`, W131 A4). It is a
  **grading/norm** operator. Per W122 Section 4, a grading acts on norms and residues, not on pole
  locations -- it cannot be the constraint that removes a propagating mode.

So all three of GU's constraints live in sectors **disjoint** from the base metric-conformal
scalaron: fiber-spinor, frame-isomorphism, and norm-grading respectively. The Dirac count is a
sector-local bookkeeping: a second-class constraint removes half a DOF from the canonical pair it
touches. **Zero** of GU's tower's second-class pairs land on the conformal-metric momentum
(test Q1a-Q1c). Therefore the count under the full tower is the same `2 + 5 + 1 = 8` (test Q1d),
with the negative control confirming the count is sensitive -- a constraint that *did* remove the
scalaron would drop it to 7 (Q1e).

**Q1 verdict: PHYSICAL propagating DOF, not constrained-away.** W78's count is re-derived through
GU's actual constraint structure, not merely ported from Stelle. The scalaron is a genuine
independent DOF; GU's extra constraints thin the *record* sector, not the *gravitational* one.

*Honest residual.* This is a sector / second-class-pair argument resting on W131's `[nabla, Pi]=0`
and the standard Dirac count, not a from-scratch BFV/Dirac reduction of the fully coupled induced
`|II|^2` + record Hamiltonian. That reduction was not attempted (PARTIAL boundary). It would be the
settling computation only if one doubted the sector-disjointness, which W131 makes hard to doubt.

## 3. Persona 2 -- Krein / PT-QFT specialist: does the physical inner product flip the sign? (Q2)

This is the subtlest escape, and the one the brief flags as the live re-audit: W78/W122 established
positive **norm**, but norm and mass-**sign** are different readings. Could the naive inner product
be misleading us, and the physical C-operator (Krein) inner product turn the "tachyon" into a
healthy mode?

Two computed facts close it, and they close it hard.

**(a) The mass sign is SIMILARITY-INVARIANT.** Changing from the naive inner product `<.,.>` to the
physical one `<.,.>_C = <., C.>` (with `C > 0` the C-operator, Mostafazadeh's positive metric) acts
on the mode operator by a similarity transformation `H -> C^{1/2} H C^{-1/2}`. Similarity
transformations preserve the spectrum. The scalaron mass-squared is a **spectral (pole)** quantity,
so it is inner-product **invariant**. The test builds an explicit `2x2` model of the spin-0 mode
operator carrying the tachyonic eigenvalue `m_0^2 = -1/4` alongside a healthy companion, conjugates
by an arbitrary invertible `S`, and confirms the characteristic polynomial and both eigenvalues are
unchanged (Q2b, Q2c): the negative eigenvalue survives **every** inner-product frame. An inner
product is a choice of metric on state space; it can move norms, never eigenvalues. This is the
exact structural point W122 Section 4 made ("grading cannot move a pole"), now sharpened to the
general C-operator and computed.

**(b) The C-metric does not even EXIST in the tachyonic band.** The pseudo-Hermitian theorem
(Bender; Mostafazadeh) says a positive-definite physical metric `C` -- a genuine Hilbert-space
inner product -- exists **iff** the spectrum is real (PT-unbroken). The tachyon's soft modes have
`omega^2 = k^2 + m_0^2 < 0` for `0 <= k < |m_0| = 1/2`: **complex** frequencies, PT-**broken** in
exactly that band (test Q2d). The band edge `k = |m_0| = 1/2` is the exceptional point (Q2f). So in
the regime the escape would need to invoke -- the unstable long-wavelength modes -- there is **no**
positive physical inner product to reinterpret under. The frame-change escape is **self-defeating**:
it presupposes the real spectrum that the tachyon denies. You cannot use "the physical Hilbert-space
inner product makes it healthy" when the pathology is precisely that no such inner product exists
there.

**Q2 verdict: FRAME-ROBUST.** The mass sign cannot be flipped by the physical inner product (it is
similarity-invariant), and the physical inner product is not even available in the tachyonic band
(PT-broken). Both the mechanism and the precondition of the escape fail. This is the honest
Lorentzian + Krein reading the brief asked for: the tachyon is a genuine PT-broken spectral
instability, not a naive-inner-product misreading.

## 4. Persona 3 -- conventions/normalization auditor: is the SIGN convention-robust? (Q3, Q4)

W157 taught the discipline: distinguish what is convention (the magnitude) from what is invariant
(the sign). This persona re-runs that discipline for the sign specifically and for the
Euclidean/Lorentzian reading.

**Euclidean vs Lorentzian (Q3).** The scalaron pole sits in the covariant propagator denominator
`~ (p^2 + m_0^2)` with `m_0^2 = 1/(6 c_R) = -3/8 < 0` (test Q3a). Wick rotation `p^0 -> i p^0_E`
relabels the Lorentzian `p^2` as the Euclidean `p_E^2` but leaves the pole PARAMETER `m_0^2`
untouched: the root in `p^2` is `-m_0^2 = 3/8` in both signatures (Q3b). The mass-squared and its
sign are a Wick-**invariant**. This is categorically different from the Euclidean conformal-factor
object: W122 proved the exact conformal reduction of Einstein gravity is **kinetic-only**
(`sqrt(-g) R[e^{2phi}eta] = 6 e^{2phi}(dphi)^2 + total deriv`) -- it carries **no mass parameter**
at all (Q3c). The GHP/Euclidean "conformal-factor problem" is about the sign of *that* massless
kinetic term for a *constrained* mode; it shares no parameter with the scalaron mass. So `m_0^2 < 0`
is not a wrong-signature artifact: it is a Lorentzian pole whose sign survives Wick rotation, living
on a different object than the Euclidean folklore.

**Sign robustness (Q4).** Reproducing W157/W159: the magnitude keystone broke (`a2_MSS = -1/9` slice
vs `c_R = -4/9` covariant), but `sign(a2/a1) < 0` holds in **both** bases (`-1/3` slice, `-4/3`
covariant; Q4a). Under an overall rescale `W -> N W` all coefficients scale by `N > 0`, so
`sign(c_R/a1)` is normalization-invariant while the `a2 = -a1^2` magnitude identity holds only at
`N = 1` (Q4b). The slice coefficients are signature-blind (W157 T4), so `c_R < 0` persists in
Euclidean `(4,0)` -- **not** a `(9,5)`-indefiniteness effect (Q4c). And the W159 shape-family
`det = 4/9 != 0` shows the sign is not FORCED by attraction (sign-free, only a positive-cone
correlation), while at GU's actual point `(alpha,beta)=(1,0)` the sign is unambiguous (Q4d).

**Q3/Q4 verdict: the SIGN is frame-robust.** Basis, normalization, signature, and Wick rotation all
leave `m_0^2 < 0` intact; only the magnitude was ever convention-dependent. The one genuine
misinterpretation the geometry offered -- the `a2 = -a1^2` magnitude -- was already caught by W157.
There is no second one on the sign side.
