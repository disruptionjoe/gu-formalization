## 1. Five-persona team (inline, sequential, single context)

### Persona 1 -- HD-gravity RG specialist: split the all-orders question into a SPINE and a RESIDUAL

The question "is HORN K all-orders?" does not have a single yes/no; it splits into **what is structural to
all orders** and **what is genuinely scheme/higher-loop-conditional**. Three legs are all-orders firm.

**Spine S1 -- `f_2^2* = 0` is a Gaussian fixed point of the pure-higher-derivative-sector beta to ALL
ORDERS.** In the pure Weyl sector the beta is a series `beta_HD = -kappa sum_{n>=2} b_n (f_2^2)^n` -- every
loop term is degree `>= 2` in `f_2^2` (degree `>= 4` in `f_2`), because a `C^2` renormalization is at least
one Weyl vertex in and one out. So `beta_HD(0) = 0` **for any loop coefficients whatsoever**. A Gaussian FP
is a zero of the pure-sector beta **to all orders**; no higher loop can create a fixed point at zero or
remove the one there. This needs **no symmetry** -- it is the structure of perturbation theory around free
fields. (Verified in W95 S1 over 500 random up-to-8-loop series: `|beta_HD(0)| = 0` identically.)

**Spine S2 -- the leading coefficient is UNIVERSAL and positive, so the AF is one-loop-robust in the QCD
sense.** The one-loop coefficient `b_2 = 133/10` (+ ker-Gamma RS `+0.70`) is the **leading** coefficient of
a marginal coupling, hence **scheme-independent/universal** (the first coefficient of a marginal beta does
not move under coupling redefinitions). It is `> 0` (and stays `> 0` across GU's ker-Gamma content, H60
band `[14.32, 15.12]`), so `f_2^2 = 0` is **UV-attractive** -- asymptotic freedom. Crucially, AF means the
coupling is **weak in the UV**: near `f_2^2 = 0` the one-loop term dominates every higher-loop term (the
ratio `b_{n>=3} (f_2^2)^n / b_2 (f_2^2)^2 -> 0`). So "the Weyl coupling flows to zero in the UV" is
**self-consistent to all orders**, in exactly the sense QCD's asymptotic freedom is one-loop-trustworthy:
higher loops are suppressed by powers of the vanishing coupling.

**Spine S3 -- UV 4th-derivative DOMINANCE is kinematic/all-orders, and it sets the physical `Z_h`.** The
graviton TT inverse propagator carries both kinetic terms, `(1/f_2^2) P_4(z) + Z_N P_2(z)`, with
`P_4 ~ z^2`, `P_2 ~ z`, `z = -Box`. The ratio `(4th-order)/(2nd-order) = z -> inf` in the UV **for any
fixed couplings** -- a kinematic fact, not a one-loop artifact. So the graviton's **physical** wave-function
normalization is set by the **leading 4th-order (Weyl) term**, `Z_h = 1/f_2^2`, and its physical anomalous
dimension `eta_h -> eta_Weyl`, which at the marginally-irrelevant Weyl direction `-> 0`. Therefore
`eta_C = -eta_h c_reg -> 0`. This is the **structural argument for `eta_C = 0`**, and it is all-orders in
its kinematic core (the 4th-order term always wins in the UV).

**The residual R1 -- where all-orders firmness stops.** `eta_C` is nonetheless a **scheme-conditional**
object. The EH-adapted identification (`eta_h = eta_N* = -2`) gives `eta_C = +2 c_reg > 0` and a lifted
HORN-Q root `f_2^2* = eta_C/(kappa b_2) ~ 0.14`; the Weyl-adapted identification gives `eta_C = 0` and only
HORN K. **`f_2^2* = 0` persists as a root in BOTH schemes** (S1: the `eta_C f_2^2` term also vanishes at
zero). So the horn is decided NOT by whether `0` is a fixed point -- it always is -- but by **which root
the physical UV trajectory sits at**, i.e. `sign(eta_C)`, i.e. the `Z_h` scheme. S3 **leans** Weyl-adapted
with an all-orders kinematic reason, but that lean is a physical argument, **not a theorem**: the FRG
`eta_C` is genuinely regulator/scheme-dependent (W89), and translating "the UV propagator is 4th-order
dominated" into "`eta_C = 0`" requires the correct regulator adaptation, which is argued, not proven.

### Persona 2 -- MATH REFEREE (grade FIRMABLE-NOW vs FRONTIER vs FALSE)

- **Ruling 1 -- the spine is genuinely all-orders and needs no symmetry.** S1 is exact (a Gaussian FP is a
  zero to all orders); S2's leading-coefficient universality is standard RG; S3's UV dominance is kinematic.
  These are not truncation-bounded. **So HORN K is not a mere one-loop accident; it has an all-orders
  structural spine.** Grade on the spine: **HIGH**.
- **Ruling 2 -- but it is NOT FIRMABLE-NOW to a theorem.** FIRMABLE-NOW would require a
  symmetry/non-renormalization argument that pins `f_2^2* = 0` as the **selected** UV FP. There is none: the
  `C^2` coupling **is** the Weyl-anomaly coefficient, and the Weyl anomaly is precisely the statement that
  `f_2^2` **runs** (`b_2` is the anomaly coefficient). No symmetry forbids the running; conformal/Weyl
  invariance is anomalous, not protective. And S1 shows `0` is *a* fixed point always, but not that it is
  *the* one the trajectory reaches -- that is the residual `sign(eta_C)`, which is scheme-conditional and
  whose deciding all-orders computation (complete two-loop-with-graviton-loops Weyl RGE) is not known.
  **So the grade cannot be FIRMABLE-NOW.**
- **Ruling 3 -- it is NOT FALSE either.** FALSE would need higher loops to plausibly lift `f_2^2* > 0`. The
  evidence runs the other way: the universal `+b_2`, the weak-coupling self-consistency of AF, the 2024
  physical-running result (AF survives without tachyons), and the fact that the HORN-Q lift requires the
  EH-adapted scheme S3 argues is the **wrong** adaptation (mis-adapting a 2nd-order cutoff to a 4th-order
  propagator, double-counting the graviton normalization). No computation lifts `f_2^2*>0` for GU's content.
- **Verdict: FRONTIER (firmable-leaning).** All-orders structural spine + genuinely open scheme residual.

### Persona 3 -- ADVERSARY ("higher loops lift it")

- *Press 1: "the leading `b_2` is one-loop; two loops could flip the AF sign."* **Answered.** The leading
  coefficient of a marginal coupling is universal; near the Gaussian FP it dominates (S2b). Two loops cannot
  flip the sign of the *approach* to a Gaussian FP that AF itself keeps weakly coupled. The genuine
  two-loop worry is not a sign flip of `b_2` -- it is a **new additive `f_2^2` (eta_C-type) term** from
  graviton loops, which is the residual R1, not a defeat of S1/S2.
- *Press 2: "the Newton-induced four-derivative fixed point (Codello-Percacci) IS the lift -- `eta_C > 0`
  is real."* **Conceded as logically possible, not realized.** This is the EH-adapted side, and it is
  genuinely in the FRG literature. But (a) it is the scheme S3 argues is unphysical for a 4th-order
  propagator; (b) the competing FRG result (BMS: the `C^2` direction is **marginally irrelevant** = AF =
  HORN K) is equally in the literature; (c) the 2024 physical-running paper argues the standard betas that
  produce such lifts are gauge/scheme-dependent and the physical running keeps AF. So the adversary
  establishes the fork is real, not that the lift wins. **The literature is two-sided and does not settle
  the all-orders sign -- which is exactly why the grade is FRONTIER, not FALSE.**
- *Press 3: "complete two-loop HD-gravity RGEs would settle it -- and they don't exist, so you cannot claim
  HORN K."* **Agreed, and it is the honest core of the grade.** Salvio-Strumia have only **partial** 2-loop
  RGEs; the complete two-loop-with-graviton-loops Weyl RGE is not available. That is precisely the named
  residual. It blocks FIRMABLE-NOW -- but the adversary's own inability to exhibit a computed lift for GU's
  content equally blocks FALSE.
