# Independent responses: P29-P35

Each response below uses the same frozen brief. The personas did not react to
one another, and no cross-persona synthesis is attempted here.

### P29. Systems Biologist

- **Summary of understanding:** The repository presents GU/Observerse as a multiscale construction problem: ambient geometry on $Y^{14}$, section-relative fields on $(X^4,g)$, an unbuilt physical quotient, and finally an operational record. Its current search ledger asks whether one source object can support GR, QM, cosmology, and Standard Model legs without sector-specific repair.
- **Strongest insight:** The typed construction map makes pleiotropy visible. For example, `lab/process/construction-space-map.json` records that the same C1 branch hosts Standard Model structure only with imports while failing GR and cosmology at Rung 0. **Cross-domain analogy:** this resembles a regulatory network in which one intervention helps one phenotype while harming another; the analogy is methodological, not evidence that GU is biological.
- **Strongest criticism:** The program has identified a missing interscale map but has not built it. `explorations/source-wave-observer-view-adjudication-2026-07-20.md` separates pullback (`Obs_g`) from physicalization (`Fam_g`) and record (`Ref_a`); without `Fam_g`, an ambient carrier cannot be said to generate physical families any more than expression of a regulator proves a stable organism-level phenotype.
- **Hidden assumptions:** The four sector legs are assumed to be jointly constrainable by one source object; small changes in metric section or boundary data are assumed not to reorganize the physical spectrum discontinuously; and typed import count is assumed to track biological-style fragility or fine-tuning. The last two are **cross-domain analogies**, not established properties.
- **Rose:** `lab/process/source-object-interface-contract.md` freezes shared provenance, normalization, loss, and non-retuning requirements before an instance exists. That is a strong systems-level safeguard against declaring four separately repaired pathways to be one mechanism.
- **Bud:** Treat the proposed observation-to-family object as a robustness problem: define perturbations of section, source, and boundary data, then ask whether chirality, multiplicity, positivity, and vacuum support persist in the same admissible neighborhood. This would add an earned notion of canalization to the present existence ladder; "canalization" is a **cross-domain analogy** until translated into operator/cohomology estimates.
- **Thorn:** A shared source object can become a mathematically unconstrained "master regulator" that explains every failed leg only after its interface has been tailored. This thorn targets the missing **native GU BV-BFV/source construction**. A conventional BRST/Higgs/integration-out package could supply familiar reductions, but success there would not establish the native GU mechanism.
- **Confidence:** 7/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Freeze one candidate source instance and a normed perturbation family before looking at sector outputs. Ablate or perturb each source component separately, recompute all four frozen leg certificates, and report a response matrix rather than four pass/fail summaries. Reject any candidate that requires sector-specific renormalization after perturbation.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Prove a compositional robustness theorem for a candidate functor $\Theta_{\mathcal S,g}$: under stated graph/domain and BV hypotheses, a common open neighborhood of $(\mathcal S,g)$ preserves acyclicity of the extra-mode cone, physical cohomology dimension, Krein discharge, and the four leg interfaces.
- **Suggested falsification test:** Exhibit two arbitrarily close admissible sections or source data with identical declared native inputs but different physical family multiplicity, or show that no single frozen normalization can satisfy two of the GR/QM/COSMO/SM legs. Either result kills robustness of that candidate, not the conventional-physics fork or GU globally.
- **Evidence touched:** `lab/process/construction-space-map.json`; `lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md`; `lab/process/source-object-interface-contract.md`; `explorations/source-wave-observer-view-adjudication-2026-07-20.md`; `GEOMETER-VS-PHYSICS-OBJECTS.md`.

### P30. Neuroscientist

- **Summary of understanding:** The repository distinguishes a third-person GU field, geometric observation by section pullback, a missing physical-family reduction, and a first-person operational record. It also separates exact mathematics and finite computations from interpretations about observation or consciousness.
- **Strongest insight:** `explorations/source-wave-observer-view-adjudication-2026-07-20.md` makes a crucial type distinction between choosing an observer-relative section and producing a readable record. **Cross-domain analogy:** predictive-processing accounts likewise distinguish latent-world dynamics, an internal representation, and a report; that analogy helps organize the question but supplies no physical evidence for GU.
- **Strongest criticism:** "Observer" still risks importing phenomenology into a geometric restriction. The repository has no operationally complete preparation, probability rule, memory update, or readout channel connecting `Fam_g` to `Ref_a`. `VERIFICATION.md` is appropriately explicit that the source-action-as-observer physical story is not established.
- **Hidden assumptions:** Section-relative information is assumed to be sufficient for an agent-accessible state; a stable record is assumed to exist without a supplied dynamics of memory; and first-person invariants are assumed not to depend pathologically on preparation or reference. None follows from pullback alone.
- **Rose:** The repo corrected rather than defended the category mistake: raw ambient dimensions neither prove nor refute observed generations, and the post-hoc scattering involution was retracted when it failed to be a natural reader. That is unusually good hygiene around observer language.
- **Bud:** Define a minimal operational observer protocol with preparation, admissible measurement, probability normalization, state update, and record persistence. It should be tested for two distinct preparations that agree on the ungraded third-person ensemble but differ under a declared orientation.
- **Thorn:** A geometrically selected section may be mistaken for a conscious or measurement-theoretic first person. This thorn applies to the **native observer-section interpretation** unless the missing BV-BFV and operational maps are supplied. Importing a conventional positive-Hilbert measurement scheme could repair operational semantics only on the conventional fork; it would not validate Krein keep-and-grade physicalization.
- **Confidence:** 8/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Construct two finite observer models with the same third-person carrier and `Obs_g` output but different candidate `Fam_g` or `Ref_a` kernels. Ask which record statistics are identifiable from section data alone. A failure of identifiability would quantify exactly what an observer datum must add.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Formulate an observer-record non-identifiability theorem: characterize when the functor from source/section/physicalization triples to record distributions is non-faithful, and give sufficient conditions under which an added orientation or preparation makes a specified statistic identifiable.
- **Suggested falsification test:** For any claimed observer-readable sector label, vary the allowed reference/preparation while holding the third-person geometry fixed. If the label changes without a declared covariant transformation law, or if no normalized positive record distribution exists after native Krein discharge, the claimed reading fails.
- **Evidence touched:** `explorations/source-wave-observer-view-adjudication-2026-07-20.md`; `VERIFICATION.md`; `RESEARCH-PROGRAM.md`; `lab/process/source-object-interface-contract.md`; `GEOMETER-VS-PHYSICS-OBJECTS.md`.

### P31. AI Learning Theory Researcher

- **Summary of understanding:** GU Formalization behaves like an explicit hypothesis-class search. Construction cells are evaluated on frozen sector constraints, graded for consistency/hosting/native selection, charged for imports, and revised through tests and correction records.
- **Strongest insight:** `lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md` converts an open-ended theory search into a partially enumerable model class with anti-revisit and breadth-first rules. That makes overfitting and generalization questions askable rather than rhetorical.
- **Strongest criticism:** The search is highly adaptive: failed tests, source interpretations, and target-sector demands all influence later candidate design. Non-retuning rules reduce leakage, but the repo does not yet expose a strict training/validation split or an adaptivity budget. Rung-0 survival can therefore measure fit to a repeatedly inspected benchmark rather than generalization beyond it.
- **Hidden assumptions:** The predeclared cells span the relevant hypothesis class; import count is a useful proxy for description length; numerical fixtures generalize to continuum objects; and the frozen sharp lists contain sufficiently independent constraints. These assumptions need tests rather than intuitive acceptance.
- **Rose:** `VERIFICATION.md` separates L1 proof, L2 computed-model results, and L3 physical interpretation, while `lab/process/construction-space-map.json` records imports and failed cells. This is a strong representation of epistemic labels that a learning system can actually condition on.
- **Bud:** Add a held-out prediction protocol: freeze a construction and all normalization choices using three sectors, then score it on an unrevealed fourth-sector constraint or a newly arriving datum. The goal is not a leaderboard but a leakage-resistant estimate of explanatory compression.
- **Thorn:** Flexible switching between the native GU fork and conventional physics can silently enlarge the effective hypothesis class. A Krein/native success, a positive-Hilbert conventional repair, and a hybrid should incur distinct model identities and complexity charges; otherwise post-selection can masquerade as unification.
- **Confidence:** 8/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Run leave-one-sector-out reconstruction across GR, QM, cosmology, and SM. For each fold, freeze the candidate, imports, fork identity, and coefficients on three sectors, then reveal the fourth sector's full sharp list. Compare held-out pass rate with in-sample rung and import count.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Define a minimum-description-length or PAC-Bayes-style bound for the finite construction ledger in which typed imports, fork switches, and adaptive probes carry explicit complexity cost. Prove a bound relating benchmark survival to held-out constraint risk under stated independence assumptions.
- **Suggested falsification test:** Seed blinded constraints or adversarial twins that are not visible during candidate selection. If high-rung, low-import constructions systematically fail them, or if results reverse under regulator/domain changes within the declared class, the present search score is not a reliable generalization signal.
- **Evidence touched:** `lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md`; `lab/process/construction-space-map.json`; `VERIFICATION.md`; `tests/README.md`; `RESEARCH-POSTURE.md`; `GEOMETER-VS-PHYSICS-OBJECTS.md`.

### P32. Reinforcement Learning Researcher

- **Summary of understanding:** The repository runs a sequential research policy: protect a high-value Lane-1 objective, choose probes by information gain and cost, bank negative results, limit rescue swings, and use lower-value proof hardening without letting it displace the North Star.
- **Strongest insight:** The operating distinction in `AGENTS.md` between North-Star pursuit and a finishable quick payoff correctly recognizes that both are exploitation at different value scales. **Cross-domain analogy:** this is hierarchical reinforcement learning with a durable top-level option and shorter subpolicies; the analogy evaluates search control, not GU physics.
- **Strongest criticism:** Expected information gain remains mostly qualitative. Without a recorded acquisition score, cost estimate, and posterior update, a council can rationalize whichever action already feels attractive. `lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md` gives good structural constraints but not a reproducible research policy.
- **Hidden assumptions:** Probe outcomes are sufficiently stationary to learn from; a cell's failure transfers to nearby cells; search actions have comparable costs; and the value of a negative result is captured by cells dispositioned rather than by scientific importance. External source arrivals also make this a partially observed, nonstationary environment.
- **Rose:** Breadth-first search, no-silent-revisit history, and the three-swing cap are strong defenses against local-minimum capture and sunk-cost behavior.
- **Bud:** Represent the construction search as a constrained POMDP or budgeted bandit: state is the typed coverage ledger plus uncertainty, actions are exact probes, observations are gates, and reward is expected elimination or promotion of high-value constructions subject to compute and truth-grade constraints.
- **Thorn:** The policy can reward-hack on cheap, clean test receipts while the missing native physicalization remains hard and underdefined. The fork must be part of the state: success from conventional BRST/positive-Hilbert repair cannot deliver reward for the native Krein/BV-BFV objective.
- **Confidence:** 7/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Retrospectively replay a bounded set of past probe choices from commit-visible state. Compare the actual heuristic policy against cheap-first, random, and explicit information-gain baselines on time-to-decisive-kill, surviving-cell value, compute cost, and number of revisits.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Under a stated conditional-independence model for sector constraints, test whether elimination value is adaptively submodular. If it is, prove a greedy information-gain policy has an approximation guarantee; if not, characterize the complementarity terms that require planned probe sequences.
- **Suggested falsification test:** At each decision point, preregister the selected probe's expected dispositions and cost plus the best rejected alternative. If repeated outcomes show materially lower realized information gain per cost than feasible alternatives, or repeated deep probes occur while cheaper reachable first probes remain, falsify the current policy rationale.
- **Evidence touched:** `AGENTS.md`; `lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md`; `lab/process/construction-space-map.json`; `NEXT-STEPS.md`; `RESEARCH-POSTURE.md`; `lab/process/source-object-interface-contract.md`.

### P33. Cognitive Scientist

- **Summary of understanding:** The repo supplies several mental models for the same research program: evidence levels, construction rungs, lanes, native-versus-conventional forks, and a four-level observer stack. These devices aim to stop category mistakes while preserving historical corrections.
- **Strongest insight:** The observer adjudication demonstrates the value of typed conceptual decomposition. Separating third-person field, section-relative field, physical family slots, and first-person record dissolves a false dimension contradiction while revealing the real missing map.
- **Strongest criticism:** The number of overlapping schemas creates high cognitive load. A new reader must reconcile `RESEARCH-PROGRAM.md`, `RESEARCH-STATUS.md`, the current top of `NEXT-STEPS.md`, verification levels, construction rungs, and correction banners. Git and prose preserve the distinctions, but human working memory is likely to collapse "hosted," "conditional," and "native" into one vague success state.
- **Hidden assumptions:** Readers find the newest owner surface; terminology is used consistently across eras; symbols such as "observer," "boundary," and "physicalization" evoke the same concept in each document; and a prose correction is enough to overwrite an earlier mental model.
- **Rose:** `GEOMETER-VS-PHYSICS-OBJECTS.md` is an excellent anti-category-error artifact: it names alternative constructions and insists that a no-go be scoped to the construction it actually tests.
- **Bud:** Build a one-screen, generated claim-and-concept map that exposes current status, weakest dependency, viewpoint level, and construction fork for every live object. Its primary role would be cognitive, not evidential: reduce conceptual substitution before an agent or reader begins a derivation.
- **Thorn:** Historical terminology can anchor readers on a superseded model even when a correction exists. In particular, "observer restriction" can be remembered as "physical reduction," and a conventional measurement interpretation can be smuggled into a native geometric pullback. This is a comprehension failure that can cause later mathematical errors.
- **Confidence:** 9/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Give independent technically trained readers a blinded set of current-status questions: what is proved, what is conditional, which observer map exists, and which fork a no-go covers. Measure agreement, answer time, and cited source before and after adding a generated current-state map.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Formalize the observer stack as typed categories/functors and prove composition guards: `Obs_g`, `Fam_g`, and `Ref_a` have distinct domains/codomains, so no term inhabiting the type of a physical family map can be constructed from section pullback alone without an explicit additional morphism.
- **Suggested falsification test:** Sample current high-level claims and ask readers to reconstruct their weakest dependency and fork from repository entry points. If competent readers reliably infer stronger or different claims, or compose `Obs_g` directly into physical records, the repository's conceptual interface fails even if its buried corrections are accurate.
- **Evidence touched:** `explorations/source-wave-observer-view-adjudication-2026-07-20.md`; `GEOMETER-VS-PHYSICS-OBJECTS.md`; `VERIFICATION.md`; `RESEARCH-PROGRAM.md`; `RESEARCH-STATUS.md`; `NEXT-STEPS.md`; `lab/process/runbooks/claim-status-consistency-quality-workflow.md`.

### P34. Git Version Control Expert

- **Summary of understanding:** GU uses Git as an append-honest research record containing claims, corrections, executable receipts, construction ledgers, and historical exploration. Current truth is nevertheless distributed across Markdown owner surfaces, JSON maps, tests, and supersession notices.
- **Strongest insight:** The repository treats correction history as an asset. `VERIFICATION.md` foregrounds a retraction, while `lab/process/runbooks/claim-status-consistency-quality-workflow.md` requires stale stronger wording to be removed or marked historical instead of silently rewriting provenance. That is good semantic-history practice.
- **Strongest criticism:** Git guarantees textual history, not semantic merge correctness. Two individually clean branches can update different copies of a claim and merge without a conflict while leaving `RESEARCH-STATUS.md`, `NEXT-STEPS.md`, a paper, and a test at different grades. Long historical files increase the chance of a false clean merge.
- **Hidden assumptions:** The newest correction is discoverable from entry points; path-based citations remain stable; every status-changing branch runs the consistency sweep; and conventional/native construction identity is never lost when two derivation branches are combined.
- **Rose:** Stable test paths, explicit correction IDs, owner-surface precedence, and evidence pointers make `git blame` and historical bisect useful for scientific provenance rather than merely code archaeology.
- **Bud:** Add a semantic-diff manifest for each claim-changing commit: stable claim ID, old/new grade, weakest dependency, construction fork, affected owner surfaces, and verification receipts. A merge gate could reject incompatible concurrent edits even when line-level Git sees no conflict.
- **Thorn:** A native-GU result and a conventional-physics result can be textually merged into one apparent conclusion if the fork is only prose. Construction identity should be a merge-relevant field; otherwise a no-go scoped to positive Hilbert space or a repair scoped to Krein geometry may contaminate the other branch.
- **Confidence:** 9/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Select a sample of recent correction-bearing claims and reconstruct current state independently from `HEAD` using only documented entry points. Then run targeted history searches for their earlier stronger phrases. Record disagreements, orphaned paths, and changes that would have merged without textual conflict.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Define a three-way semantic merge invariant for a claim DAG: if both branches preserve dependency monotonicity, fork identity, and owner-surface consistency relative to the base, characterize sufficient conditions under which their merge also preserves those invariants.
- **Suggested falsification test:** Create a test branch that downgrades a claim in its owner artifact while leaving a stronger copy in a live roadmap or paper, and another that changes its construction fork without changing the claim ID. If the current gates accept either merge, the repository does not yet enforce semantic history integrity.
- **Evidence touched:** `lab/process/runbooks/claim-status-consistency-quality-workflow.md`; `VERIFICATION.md`; `RESEARCH-STATUS.md`; `NEXT-STEPS.md`; `tests/README.md`; `GEOMETER-VS-PHYSICS-OBJECTS.md`; `CONTRIBUTING.md`.

### P35. Database Systems Architect

- **Summary of understanding:** The repository is a hybrid research database. Structured JSON records construction cells, grades, imports, gates, and evidence pointers; Markdown holds claim ledgers and owner narratives; executable tests act as integrity checks; Git supplies versioned transactions at file granularity.
- **Strongest insight:** `lab/process/construction-space-map.json` is a valuable move toward explicit state rather than prose-only memory. It records rung semantics, conditional grades, shared-import counts, evidence pointers, and the bounded meaning of failures. `lab/process/runbooks/claim-status-consistency-quality-workflow.md` also defines a clear source-of-truth order.
- **Strongest criticism:** The state is denormalized across multiple stores without enforced referential integrity or atomic scientific transactions. A JSON evidence pointer can go stale, a Markdown status can change without its dependent claim, or a test can continue to validate an obsolete interpretation. Schema version `1.0` alone does not provide a migration or constraint system.
- **Hidden assumptions:** File paths are durable keys; every write updates all materialized views; dependency edges are complete; test success means the tested interpretation is current; and serialized local execution prevents logical write skew among concurrent research branches.
- **Rose:** The owner-surface precedence rule and "downstream cannot outrank weakest dependency" constraint are exactly the kinds of invariants a reliable claim store needs.
- **Bud:** Create one normalized, machine-validated claim/provenance registry with stable IDs, dependency edges, evidence objects, viewpoint level, and an explicit `construction_fork` discriminator. Generate human-facing status tables from it while leaving papers and explorations as narrative views.
- **Thorn:** The native/conventional fork is presently protected mainly by reading discipline. Without a schema discriminator, a conventional BRST or positive-Hilbert repair can be joined to a native Krein/BV-BFV claim as if they referred to the same row. That is a type-confused join, not evidence for native physicalization.
- **Confidence:** 9/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Parse every evidence pointer and dependency in `lab/process/construction-space-map.json` and the current claim ledger; check file existence, unique stable IDs, status monotonicity, and reciprocal references. Then mutation-test the validator with a stale path, an orphan dependency, a stronger child status, and a fork mismatch.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Specify a serializable claim-transition algebra. Prove that transactions satisfying referential integrity, dependency-grade monotonicity, fork typing, and owner-view refresh cannot expose a committed state in which a live downstream claim outranks or changes construction relative to its weakest dependency.
- **Suggested falsification test:** Intentionally rename an evidence artifact or update one owner status without its materialized views and dependencies. If repository checks still pass and two queries of "current claim state" return different answers, the present integrity layer is insufficient.
