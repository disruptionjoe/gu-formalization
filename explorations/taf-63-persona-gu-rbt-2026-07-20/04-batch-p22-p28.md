# Independent responses: P22-P28

### P22. Information Theorist

- **Summary of understanding:** GU currently has a typed sequence from a third-person field on (Y^{14}), through section pullback, to a still-missing physicalization map and only then to a first-person record. The exact (3E_+\oplus3E_-\oplus X) branching is information present in an observer-relative representation; it is not yet a channel that produces three physical families (`explorations/source-wave-observer-view-adjudication-2026-07-20.md`; `explorations/observation-to-family-b5-campaign-2026-07-20.md`).
- **Strongest insight:** The most informative next distinction is between information *preserved* by (g^*), information *discarded* by the proposed BV/low-energy quotient, and target information *injected* by an external orientation, (S_3) reduction, or boundary datum. Treating those arrows as channels is a proposed information-theoretic formalization, not an asserted equivalence to a classical or quantum communication channel.
- **Strongest criticism:** No input ensemble, physical observable algebra, probability rule, or normalized output statistics exist for the native physicalization, so mutual information, capacity, sufficiency, and even a literal bit cost are presently undefined. Calling the remaining selector a "2-bit residual" or counting one interface import is useful bookkeeping, but it is not yet an information-theoretic derivation (`VERIFICATION.md`; `lab/process/source-object-interface-contract.md`).
- **Hidden assumptions:** The program assumes that the desired family label survives the lossy reduction, that observer choices do not leak the answer as side information, that import counts are comparable despite unequal descriptive content, and that a target-free source fixes a probability law rather than merely a representation.
- **Rose:** The L1/L2/L3 verification split and the source-interface loss-ledger requirement make information loss and target leakage auditable instead of hiding them in interpretive prose (`VERIFICATION.md`; `lab/process/source-object-interface-contract.md`).
- **Bud:** Specify each stage of the B.5 diagram by its input/output algebra, admissible states, retained sufficient statistic, and externally supplied side information; then report both a structural import count and, only after an encoding/prior is frozen, a description-length or information cost.
- **Thorn:** This thorn is native-fork specific: until the Krein/BV physicalization is a defined map, an observer section, orientation bit, or family local system can silently operate as a high-bandwidth target side-channel. A conventional positive-Hilbert/BRST channel could be well defined, but importing it would not validate the native GU arrow.
- **Confidence:** 8/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Freeze a finite B.5 proxy before attaching Standard Model names, randomly relabel the candidate target sectors and observer frames, and ask whether the same source-fixed map still decodes the three retained slots while rejecting (3E_-\oplus X). Compare performance with and without orientation/(S_3) side information.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Prove a data-processing/no-asymmetry statement: for a physicalization functor that is natural in (g) and commutes with the explicit mirror exchange (mathcal J), every output statistic odd under (mathcal J) has zero distinguishability on mirror-symmetric inputs. State separately the extra side-information resource needed to evade it.
- **Suggested falsification test:** Refute the side-channel concern by exhibiting one source-owned, pre-target physicalization with a complete probability/observable interface that retains exactly the three (E_+) provenance lines across randomized admissible sections while its output is independent of hidden target labels and no undeclared orientation or family key is supplied.
- **Evidence touched:** `VERIFICATION.md`; `lab/process/source-object-interface-contract.md`; `explorations/source-wave-observer-view-adjudication-2026-07-20.md`; `explorations/observation-to-family-b5-campaign-2026-07-20.md`.

### P23. Resource Theory Researcher

- **Summary of understanding:** The native GU carrier is mirror-balanced and Krein-graded. The desired physical outcome requires chirality, orientation, positive physical probabilities, and a three-fold multiplicity label that are not currently free outputs of the released differential. The construction-space protocol types and counts imports but has not yet defined which transformations and states are free (`GEOMETER-VS-PHYSICS-OBJECTS.md`; `lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md`).
- **Strongest insight:** Chirality/orientation should be treated as an asymmetry resource. The exact mirror lemma in the B.5 campaign is already the seed of a resource monotone: operations commuting with (mathcal J) cannot turn a balanced complex into cohomology containing only (3E_+). This is a direct resource-theory lens on the stated symmetry, not evidence that GU is a quantum resource theory.
- **Strongest criticism:** Import counting assigns unit-like costs to objects of radically different power. A boundary adapter capable of supplying a quotient, positivity, dynamics, chirality, and selection may contain far more resource than several small normalization choices. Without free operations, conversion rules, and monotones, "one shared import" can underprice the entire physical sector (`lab/process/source-object-interface-contract.md`; `lab/process/construction-space-map.json`).
- **Hidden assumptions:** The native super-IG/BV operations form a closed allowed class; the observer pullback is resource-nongenerating; Krein keep-and-grade can yield a positive operational sector without an unpriced polarization; and the same external datum cannot be reused catalytically across the GR, QM, cosmology, and SM legs without cost.
- **Rose:** The R0/R1/R2 ladder correctly distinguishes compatibility, hosting with priced imports, and native selection, and it refuses to count conventional positive-Hilbert or replicated-family structure as a native GU resource (`lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md`).
- **Bud:** Define separate native and conventional resource theories. For the native theory, freeze free complexes, (mathcal J)-equivariant chain maps, section pullbacks, admissible homotopies, and boundary operations; then seek monotones for chiral imbalance, positivity, family labeling, and target-dependent tuning.
- **Thorn:** Native-fork thorn: if the proposed source object breaks (mathcal J), chooses a polarization, and supplies an (S_3) frame, it may be the entire scarce resource rather than a small adapter to GU. The conventional BRST/Higgs fork can purchase these resources differently, but that comparison must not be credited to the native construction.
- **Confidence:** 9/10
- **Suggested experiment:** In the finite associated-grade ledger (3E_+\oplus3E_-\oplus X), enumerate source-compatible linear differentials under progressively relaxed symmetry sets. For each relaxation, record the minimum declared resource that permits an acyclic unwanted complement and test whether the same resource works without retuning across observer sections.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Extend the mirror-chain lemma to a monotonicity theorem: under (mathcal J)-equivariant chain maps and homotopy equivalences, an appropriately defined chiral-asymmetry monotone cannot increase from zero; any deformation retract onto (3E_+) therefore has a nonzero symmetry-breaking resource cost.
- **Suggested falsification test:** Produce a native, source-fixed differential and domain with zero cost under the declared free-operation rules whose cohomology retains (3E_+), kills (3E_-\oplus X), and remains natural over admissible sections. That would falsify the proposed asymmetry monotone or show the free-operation set was typed incorrectly.
- **Evidence touched:** `GEOMETER-VS-PHYSICS-OBJECTS.md`; `lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md`; `lab/process/construction-space-map.json`; `lab/process/source-object-interface-contract.md`; `explorations/observation-to-family-b5-campaign-2026-07-20.md`.

### P24. Constructor Theory Researcher

- **Summary of understanding:** GU has many candidate states, representations, and conditional operator constructions, but the decisive claim requires a reliable transformation: one frozen native source object must repeatedly turn the ambient rolled/Krein data into a physical sector satisfying the SM, QM, GR, and cosmology constraints without sector-specific repair (`lab/process/source-object-interface-contract.md`; `lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md`).
- **Strongest insight:** B.5 is better posed as a task than as an object: specify substrates, allowed inputs, outputs, accuracy, repeatability across metric sections, retained catalysts, and forbidden target-dependent controls. The current deformation-retract certificate is a strong single-instance algebraic condition, but constructor-grade success also requires stability and composability.
- **Strongest criticism:** Existence of a fitted map or a nonresonant boundary phase does not show that the corresponding transformation is possible under a reusable, source-owned constructor. The graph-domain result explicitly proves many admissible (T)'s while admitting that GU selects none; this is possibility under external choice, not a native construction (`explorations/continuum-pencil-graph-domain-certificate-2026-07-20.md`).
- **Hidden assumptions:** The observer/source datum survives repeated use without being consumed; the construction tolerates perturbations of (g), boundary data, and lower-order terms; the four sector transformations compose on one substrate; and the native Krein discharge is dynamically maintainable rather than an imposed final projection.
- **Rose:** The predeclared sharp constraints and Rung-2 demand already approximate constructor theory's possible/impossible discipline: success requires a task to work under fixed conditions, and a bounded no-go is not inflated beyond its construction class (`lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md`).
- **Bud:** Rewrite the shared source-object interface as a task specification with explicit error parameters and constructor lifecycle: initialization, one-cycle transformation, residual/catalyst state, repeatability over a family of sections, and composition of the four legs.
- **Thorn:** Native-fork thorn: the missing BV-BFV source may be described as a passive boundary condition when the required role is an active, repeatable physicalizing constructor. A conventional laboratory preparation or positive-Hilbert projection can perform another task, but substituting it changes the construction.
- **Confidence:** 8/10
- **Suggested experiment:** On a finite proxy, freeze one source/differential and run the physicalization task over a perturbation ensemble of observer sections and input representatives. Measure output-sector error, unwanted-mode leakage, resource degradation, and whether a second use requires retuning.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Prove an impossibility theorem for the native allowed-task class: if every elementary task commutes with (mathcal J), then every finite composition, catalyst-assisted composition with a (mathcal J)-invariant catalyst, and stable limit preserves mirror-balanced cohomology.
- **Suggested falsification test:** Exhibit one target-free native constructor whose fixed controls robustly and repeatedly yield the required physical quotient across an open neighborhood of sections, with the unwanted complement contractible and all catalyst/resource changes accounted for.

### P25. Philosopher Of Physics

- **Summary of understanding:** The program distinguishes four levels that had previously been compressed into "observer": third-person GU geometry, section-relative pullback, physical reduction, and first-person operational record. Only the pullback is manuscript-defined; the physical reduction is the missing load-bearing arrow (`explorations/source-wave-observer-view-adjudication-2026-07-20.md`).
- **Strongest insight:** The first-person/third-person distinction dissolves a false dimension objection without solving the physics. Ambient carriers need not resemble observed family slots, but an explicit physicalization is then indispensable. This is a philosophically clean correction because it separates representation, perspectival access, and operational content.
- **Strongest criticism:** "External" currently alternates among boundary condition, observer frame, orientation, superselection datum, and physical cause. Relocating chirality or generation selection outside the balanced interior is explanatory only if the outside object has an independently specified ontology, dynamics, and observable consequences; otherwise the explanandum has merely moved (`RESEARCH-PROGRAM.md`; `lab/process/source-object-interface-contract.md`).
- **Hidden assumptions:** The first-person record supervenes on a well-defined physical algebra; different section-relative presentations are empirically equivalent where claimed; a Standard-Model-shaped external datum is less arbitrary than competing inputs; and explanatory compression survives when the interface's full structure is counted.
- **Rose:** The repo now states explicitly that observer restriction is not physical reduction and that process discipline is not physics evidence. Its willingness to retract the (T_{192}) family reading and modular realization is a substantive epistemic strength (`VERIFICATION.md`; `explorations/source-wave-observer-view-adjudication-2026-07-20.md`).
- **Bud:** Give the four-level stack an operational semantics: identify preparation procedures, observable algebras, probability assignments, equivalence classes of observer descriptions, and at least one outcome whose distribution differs from a conventional reconstruction with the same imported data.
- **Thorn:** Fork-dependent underdetermination: a native Krein/BV physicalization and a conventional positive-Hilbert/BRST reduction may reproduce the same low-energy records while positing different unobservable structures. Until a discriminating observable is derived, success at hosting grade would not decide between them.
- **Confidence:** 9/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Build the strongest conventional comparator with the same observed spectrum and an explicit ledger of priors/imports. Blindly compare its target-independent quantitative outputs, parameter sensitivity, and explanatory cost with the frozen native candidate rather than comparing GU to an underspecified foil.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Define an operational-equivalence quotient on physicalization functors and prove conditions under which two section-dependent constructions induce the same state/observable probabilities. Then isolate which native structural invariant, if any, survives that quotient and could have empirical meaning.
- **Suggested falsification test:** If every native prediction after physicalization can be matched by the conventional comparator with no greater fixed input cost, and no native-only observable or constraint survives the operational quotient, falsify the claim that the current native structure provides a physically distinctive unification rather than a redescription.

### P26. Philosophy Of Mathematics Researcher

- **Summary of understanding:** GU's strongest results concern structural invariants and obstructions, while its open frontier repeatedly turns on the difference between an object existing, being selected canonically, and being forced naturally. The rank-three multiplicity object exists after observer restriction, but no natural (S_3)-labeled splitting or physicalization functor has been supplied (`explorations/observation-to-family-b5-campaign-2026-07-20.md`).
- **Strongest insight:** The key question is invariance under the correct automorphism group. A basis-dependent decomposition can display three lines without defining three mathematical individuals. The retirement of the (T_{192}) weight spaces as families is a good example: the full symmetry mixes the weights, so the apparent labels were presentation structure rather than invariant structure (`explorations/source-wave-observer-view-adjudication-2026-07-20.md`).
- **Strongest criticism:** Terms such as "canonical," "natural," "forced," and "external" are not always tied to a declared category of objects and morphisms. Without that category, a naturality claim may be made true or false by quietly shrinking the morphisms—for example, by fixing an orientation, Cartan axis, boundary phase, or observer frame.
- **Hidden assumptions:** The chosen observer groupoid is the physically relevant equivalence notion; the native real form and its complexification preserve the structures being compared; a functorial (S_3)-reduction is meaningful globally; and proof-assistant formalization of a set-level theorem transfers only the theorem, not its observer interpretation.
- **Rose:** `Lean/GUFormalization/ResidualSelection.lean` and the L1/L2/L3 map show exemplary separation between exact formal structure and optional physical interpretation (`VERIFICATION.md`). The construction-fork table likewise prevents equality of names from substituting for equivalence of objects (`GEOMETER-VS-PHYSICS-OBJECTS.md`).
- **Bud:** Define two explicit categories—native GU/Krein/BV and conventional Hilbert/BRST—with a comparison functor only where justified. State B.5 as a natural transformation or derived functor with a universal property, including exactly which orientation and boundary data are objects rather than hidden background.
- **Thorn:** A result proved after rigidifying the native object by an observer axis or (S_3) frame may be mathematically correct but about the enlarged pair ((g,a)) or ((g,L)), not about GU geometry alone. Conversely, a conventional construction cannot be dismissed by a no-go whose morphisms exist only in the native category.
- **Confidence:** 9/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Perform an automorphism audit on every proposed family selector: apply the full declared stabilizer/groupoid action and test whether the selector, its kernel, and its labels commute. Formalize the smallest rank-three case in Lean before attempting the full derived setting.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Prove a no-natural-labeling theorem for the rank-three multiplicity bundle under its full frame automorphisms, followed by a sharp existence/obstruction theorem for an (S_3)-reduction over the admissible observer groupoid. State the extra rigidifying datum as part of the object when it is necessary.
- **Suggested falsification test:** Construct a source-owned natural transformation from the native rolled complex to (E_+\otimes\mathbb C^3) that is equivariant for the full declared morphism class, independent of basis/Cartan choices, and compatible with composition over section space. That would defeat the claimed structural underdetermination.
- **Evidence touched:** `VERIFICATION.md`; `GEOMETER-VS-PHYSICS-OBJECTS.md`; `Lean/GUFormalization/ResidualSelection.lean`; `explorations/source-wave-observer-view-adjudication-2026-07-20.md`; `explorations/observation-to-family-b5-campaign-2026-07-20.md`.

### P27. Philosophy Of Science Researcher

- **Summary of understanding:** The repo treats GU as a high-information conjecture used to generate precise tests, preserves GU-independent survivors, and explicitly permits clean branch kills. Its current construction-space protocol predeclares sharp constraints, import accounting, and bounded verdict scope rather than asking for an immediate global GU verdict (`RESEARCH-POSTURE.md`; `lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md`).
- **Strongest insight:** The correction and retraction trail is unusually valuable. Publishing the failed modular realization, the retired family dictionary, and conditional rather than global operator results makes the research process inspectable and enables cumulative learning rather than narrative repair (`VERIFICATION.md`; `explorations/source-wave-observer-view-adjudication-2026-07-20.md`).
- **Strongest criticism:** Local scope discipline does not by itself prevent global auxiliary-hypothesis elasticity. As constructions, observer meanings, source objects, and comparison classes change, the program needs an aggregate ledger showing which risky GU commitments became narrower, more predictive, or more costly—not just why each individual failure was non-global.
- **Hidden assumptions:** Repeated production of interesting GU-independent mathematics indicates progress on GU's unifying claim; the predeclared construction space is broad enough to test the relevant hypothesis; typed import count tracks explanatory burden; and a conjecture remains scientifically fruitful without a distinctive passing prediction.
- **Rose:** The R0/R1/R2 ladder, expressiveness guard, predeclared endings, and hostile falsifiers explicitly block compatibility from being reported as derivation (`lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md`; `lab/process/construction-space-map.json`).
- **Bud:** Add a severity ledger for the program-level hypothesis: before each major swing, freeze the load-bearing claim, auxiliaries, expected risky outcome, comparator, and how each outcome changes construction-space volume, import burden, and prediction rank.
- **Thorn:** "Located, not forced" can be a strong result, but it becomes an immunizing move if every missing datum is reclassified as an acceptable external input without a comparative cost ceiling or a novel consequence. This thorn applies to the native bet; a conventional model must face the same cost and severity accounting rather than serving as an unexamined default.
- **Confidence:** 9/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Pre-register one target-independent quantitative output from a frozen native source/normalization packet and one serious conventional comparator, conceal the confrontation data until both pipelines are fixed, and have an independent verifier adjudicate the declared exclusion regions.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Formalize the claim/dependency graph so that branch kills induce monotone, machine-checkable updates to the surviving construction class and import-cost bounds. Prove that no allowed reclassification can restore a killed cell without adding a declared axis delta and a new falsifier.
- **Suggested falsification test:** If a complete disposition of the frozen construction map leaves an empty joint R0 intersection, accept the protocol's bounded falsification of that class. Separately, falsify the method's severity if repeated failed risky tests neither shrink the live class nor increase the recorded auxiliary/import cost.

### P28. Evolutionary Biologist

- **Summary of understanding:** The repo is exploring a population of construction families under four sector constraint sets, retaining survivors, recording bounded no-go boundaries, and attempting to weave a jointly viable construction. Its central missing map is from ambient/observer-relative carrier content to a stable physical phenotype (`lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md`; `lab/process/construction-space-map.json`).
- **Strongest insight:** **Cross-domain analogy, not GU evidence:** the geometry-to-physicalization relation resembles a genotype-to-phenotype map more than an identity. The same ambient carrier can have different observer-relative presentations, and a desired family pattern matters only if a specified developmental map robustly produces it. Selection language cannot replace that mechanism.
- **Strongest criticism:** **Cross-domain analogy:** the search currently grades isolated constructions more clearly than their mutational neighborhoods. A cell that fits only at one tuned parameter point may look selected while lacking robustness, evolvability, or resistance to small changes in section, normalization, or boundary data. Biology would treat that brittleness as a major part of fitness.
- **Hidden assumptions:** The four-sector constraints define an adequate environment; nearby constructions are meaningfully parameterized; robustness in mathematical input space correlates with physical naturalness; external boundary data are heritable/fixed rather than retuned per instance; and survival of a branch is not an artifact of which variants happened to be generated.
- **Rose:** **Cross-domain analogy:** the append-honest coverage ledger and ban on silent revisits preserve the equivalent of extinct lineages and negative selection data. That reduces survivorship bias and makes the explored landscape visible (`lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md`).
- **Bud:** Add a robustness/evolvability layer after Rung 0: for each survivor, map the neighborhood of admissible source coefficients, sections, and domain choices; measure joint-sector persistence, pleiotropic tradeoffs, and the number of independent repairs required.
- **Thorn:** **Cross-domain analogy:** sector-specific repairs are overfitting to separate environments. A source object that yields GR, QM, cosmology, and SM only through independently tuned downstream adapters has not demonstrated integrated adaptation. This applies most sharply to the native shared-source claim; conventional constructions should be assessed on the same perturbation budget.
- **Confidence:** 7/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Run a blinded perturbation assay around every joint survivor: vary all non-forced parameters and admissible observer/domain choices within predeclared neighborhoods, then measure the fraction retaining each sharp constraint and the joint intersection without retuning.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Prove a structural-stability theorem for any claimed Rung-2 construction: under a declared topology on source objects and physicalization functors, the required cohomology ranks, positivity certificate, and sector outputs persist on an open class—or else identify the codimension and symmetry that protect an isolated solution.
- **Suggested falsification test:** Falsify native-selection robustness if arbitrarily small admissible perturbations destroy the physical quotient or family content while recovery requires target-specific parameter restoration. Conversely, an open, source-fixed neighborhood of joint survivors would falsify the brittleness concern.
