# Independent responses: P50-P56

These responses were produced independently from the frozen common brief. They
are disciplinary evaluations, not evidence and not a cross-persona synthesis.

### P50. Scientific Skeptic

- **Summary of understanding:** GU has earned exact representation facts, bounded no-gos, conditional operator theorems, and unusually explicit evidence grades, but it has not constructed the native physical quotient that would turn observer-relative carrier branching into physical families. In particular, `explorations/observation-to-family-b5-campaign-2026-07-20.md` proves the branching \(3E_+\oplus3E_-\oplus X\), not the removal of \(3E_-\oplus X\).
- **Strongest insight:** The repo's strongest credibility signal is not a positive GU claim but its willingness to break its own preferred proxies: the \(T_{192}\) family reading and construction-wide scattering involution were retired, while the continuum claim was narrowed to a conditional compact graph-domain theorem in `explorations/source-wave-observer-view-adjudication-2026-07-20.md`.
- **Strongest criticism:** The external-boundary story can become immunized against failure if every missing physical feature is charged to an unspecified source object. `RESEARCH-PROGRAM.md` properly calls this a bet, but the standing boundary-adapter axiom risks operationally converting the central absence into a permanent premise unless an explicit construction class and exhaustion condition are frozen.
- **Hidden assumptions:** A source-owned BV-BFV differential exists; it can remove every mirror and extra mode without deleting the three desired provenance lines; finite-model certificates survive domain and continuum limits; and the eventual boundary datum is independently motivated rather than inferred from the target physics.
- **Rose:** `VERIFICATION.md` separates proved mathematics, computed models, and physical interpretation and records decisive objections and retractions alongside successes.
- **Bud:** The predeclared middle-differential acyclization swing is a genuine high-information test because it targets the exact unwanted complement and admits construction, obstruction, and source-gap outcomes.
- **Thorn:** This thorn applies to the **native GU** fork: until \(\operatorname{Sp}(32,32;\mathbb H)\), Krein keep-and-grade, and super-IG data produce a source-owned physicalization map, conventional BRST, positive-Hilbert projection, or tuned boundary phase cannot validate the claimed native bridge. Conversely, ordinary nonunitarity objections to the conventional fork do not by themselves kill the native Krein construction.
- **Confidence:** 9/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Freeze the source-licensed class of first-order middle symbols and domains before examining cohomology; enumerate it completely; require planted mirror-preserving and mirror-breaking controls; then measure whether \(3E_-\oplus X\) is acyclic while all three \(E_+\) lines survive.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Prove either a natural deformation-retract theorem for the rolled BV-BFV complex or a class-relative obstruction theorem showing that every source-compatible real/Krein first-order differential induces mirror-paired cohomology.
- **Suggested falsification test:** Return `B5-MIDDLE-OBSTRUCTION` if every frozen source-compatible symbol/domain commutes with mirror exchange or violates ellipticity, square-zero closure, or native real-form compatibility when it breaks that exchange. Return `B5-MIDDLE-SOURCE-GAP`, not success, if the source cannot delimit the class.
- **Evidence touched:** `RESEARCH-PROGRAM.md`; `VERIFICATION.md`; `GEOMETER-VS-PHYSICS-OBJECTS.md`; `explorations/observation-to-family-b5-campaign-2026-07-20.md`; `explorations/source-wave-observer-view-adjudication-2026-07-20.md`; `explorations/continuum-pencil-graph-domain-certificate-2026-07-20.md`.

### P51. Research Program Architect

- **Summary of understanding:** GU is organized around a protected truth-testing North Star, three evidence rungs, four sector tracks, and a construction-space coverage ledger. The active program seeks one construction that survives QM, SM, GR, and cosmology without sector-specific repair, while `lab/process/construction-space-map.json` currently records conditional corridors and typed imports rather than a unified instance.
- **Strongest insight:** The construction-space protocol correctly changes the falsification unit from one favored candidate to the fully dispositioned joint Rung-0 intersection. This prevents both premature global rejection and endless rescue of one failed branch.
- **Strongest criticism:** The program has two dependency narratives that are not yet fully reconciled: a broad construction-space search and a privileged shared boundary/source object. Because the adapter is a standing conditional axiom, conditional progress can accumulate faster than the missing shared instance is constrained. A dependency graph should make explicit which cells genuinely share one frozen import and which merely use similarly named, sector-tuned repairs.
- **Hidden assumptions:** The current axes span the relevant construction class; import count is a meaningful complexity proxy; the four sector constraint lists are comparably sharp; a single source object can satisfy all four interfaces; and historical correction banners are sufficient for contributors to reconstruct active dependencies.
- **Rose:** `lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md` combines breadth-first probes, import accounting, no-silent-revisit rules, and predeclared endings without allowing Rung 0 or 1 to masquerade as native unification.
- **Bud:** Turn the P6 conditional corridor into a single versioned interface-satisfiability packet: one identity, normalization ledger, loss ledger, branch assumptions, and rollback rule evaluated simultaneously against all four parameter cards.
- **Thorn:** The main program risk is dependency drift: four individually plausible sector adapters could be welded only by hidden retuning. This threatens the **native GU** Rung-2 claim. A conventional positive-Hilbert/BRST implementation may demonstrate hosting, but it changes construction and must remain a priced Rung-1 import rather than a native recovery.
- **Confidence:** 8/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Derive a machine-readable dependency DAG from `lab/process/construction-space-map.json` and the source-object contract, then run a no-retuning audit in which every shared parameter is frozen before any sector test and every sector-specific degree of freedom increments the import count.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Formulate an interface-composition theorem: sufficient and necessary compatibility conditions for four sector realizations to descend from one source object, with an invariant that detects when nominally shared data are actually independent repairs.
- **Suggested falsification test:** Reject the present welded corridor if two sectors demand incompatible values or transformation laws for one shared datum, or if satisfying all four requires target-dependent maps not represented in the frozen interface. That falsifies the corridor, not every GU construction.

### P52. Mathematical Minimalist

- **Summary of understanding:** Much of the present frontier can be compressed to two small mathematical objects: a chain complex whose unwanted summand is \(3E_-\oplus X\), and a first-order boundary pencil whose compact invertibility is controlled by \(\det(\Phi_z(b,a)-T)\). Neither small object alone supplies physical meaning or a native source.
- **Strongest insight:** `explorations/observation-to-family-b5-campaign-2026-07-20.md` has already reduced the family problem from an open-ended search for “three” to an acyclicity question for one explicitly typed complement. That is the correct minimal decision object.
- **Strongest criticism:** The proposed global filtered BV-BFV superconnection may be more structure than is needed for the next decisive result. Starting with the full action, family transport, spectral section, and record map at once can hide the smallest obstruction: whether any allowed local differential can break mirror exchange while preserving the desired three lines.
- **Hidden assumptions:** Every named layer is logically necessary at the first test; the full \(\operatorname{Sp}(32,32;\mathbb H)\) carrier must be retained during local symbol classification; and success at principal-symbol grade will extend to a global domain and cohomology.
- **Rose:** The repo increasingly states exact minimal certificates: a deformation retract for B.5 and a finite boundary-monodromy determinant for the compact pencil.
- **Bud:** Define the smallest observer-natural, real/Krein-compatible two-term or filtered complex containing the three provenance lines and the unwanted complement, and classify its admissible differentials before constructing the rest of BV-BFV machinery.
- **Thorn:** Minimalization must preserve construction typing. Replacing the native Krein/super-IG complex with an ordinary positive-Hilbert BRST complex may solve a smaller **conventional** problem without addressing GU. Conversely, retaining native labels that play no role in the differential creates decorative complexity rather than explanatory force.
- **Confidence:** 8/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Compute the finite-dimensional Hom spaces of all first-order principal symbols allowed by observer naturality and the native real form, quotient by equivalence, and test mapping-cone cohomology on each class before adding lower-order terms.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Prove the minimal mirror lemma at chain level: if every admissible differential and domain commute with an involution exchanging (E_+) and (E_-), then their cohomologies are paired and no chiral three-family retract exists in that class.
- **Suggested falsification test:** One explicit source-compatible, mirror-breaking differential with exact unwanted-symbol complex and a viable global lift falsifies the minimal obstruction. Failure to lift it falsifies only that candidate, not the entire first-order class.

### P53. North Star Visionary

- **Summary of understanding:** The program's ambitious target is not another isolated GU-compatible lemma; it is one frozen construction that yields a physical quantum sector, Standard Model content, Einstein dynamics, and cosmological perturbations without custom repairs. The current repository has valuable structural results but no construction at that bar.
- **Strongest insight:** The first-person/third-person distinction does not diminish the ambition; it identifies the missing central arrow. Ambient geometry, observer restriction, physical reduction, and operational record are different levels, so the highest-value object is the observation-to-family/source map that connects them without collapsing them.
- **Strongest criticism:** The program is vulnerable to “clean byproduct capture.” Conditional compact operator theorems, representation ledgers, and individual sector screens are publishable and tractable, but none should displace the construction that must make the four sectors cohere.
- **Hidden assumptions:** One source object is rich enough to weld the four sectors yet rigid enough to avoid tuning; external chirality is a compressed necessity rather than an arbitrary exception; and the construction-space axes include the decisive escape.
- **Rose:** `NEXT-STEPS.md` explicitly protects Lane 1 from demotion by difficulty, and the repo banks negative results without confusing them for the desired physical construction.
- **Bud:** Use the B.5 middle-differential target as the first load-bearing component of the shared source object, then force it to expose the same orientation, normalization, boundary, and selection data consumed by the other three sector cards.
- **Thorn:** “The answer comes from outside” can preserve the vision only if the outside is more constrained than the phenomena it explains. This is a thorn for the **native GU** story. Importing conventional BRST, Higgs, positive polarization, and boundary conditions can make a host, but it does not earn native selection.
- **Confidence:** 8/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Freeze one candidate source-object fingerprint before sector inspection and run it through all four sharp constraint lists in order, forbidding parameter refits; publish the complete pass/fail vector and import ledger even if the candidate dies early.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Seek a joint rigidity-or-obstruction theorem: under one native source action and one boundary datum, characterize when the physical cohomology, Einstein-sector residual, scalar cosmology, and three-family selector can coexist.
- **Suggested falsification test:** If the predeclared construction space is fully dispositioned with empty joint Rung-0 intersection, or every survivor needs independently adjustable sector maps, the frozen class fails the North-Star test. Do not reinterpret a branch-local kill as a global GU verdict.

### P54. Experimentalist

- **Summary of understanding:** GU has a large executable audit surface and a credible habit of predeclared controls, but its strongest physical claims remain upstream of any decisive observable. Recent work correctly demoted the numerical wall result: bounded finite slopes along one path are not a regular-Krein-critical theorem, and a fixed-\(\delta\) negative control did not establish power against the relevant singular limit.
- **Strongest insight:** The best next experiment is not more unconstrained numerics. It is a finite, completeness-audited search over source-compatible middle differentials with explicit positive controls, negative controls, and a frozen success certificate.
- **Strongest criticism:** Several attractive signals were generated by choices made after seeing the target: the scattering `sig` reversed observed eigenpairs, while a nonresonant graph-domain phase can be selected after inspecting the pencil. Such constructions test existence but not native prediction or selection.
- **Hidden assumptions:** Existing test families cover the dangerous failure modes; numerical convergence tracks the correct joint limit; the enumerated symbol class is complete; and observed data are not reused to tune coefficients later advertised as predictions.
- **Rose:** `tests/README.md` exposes claim-to-script provenance, and `VERIFICATION.md` distinguishes computation from proof and interpretation rather than treating test passes as theory confirmation.
- **Bud:** The B.5 acyclization test can be made unusually decisive: exact input character, enumerated candidate class, planted controls, mirror-commutation gate, symbol-exactness gate, and then a global-domain gate.
- **Thorn:** Post-selection is the central experimental hazard. On the **native GU** fork, the source must choose the differential, boundary relation, and normalization before outcomes are inspected. A conventional APS/positive-Hilbert choice is a valid comparator but remains an import and cannot serve as the native positive control.
- **Confidence:** 9/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Implement a manifest-driven enumerator for the frozen first-order symbol class. Plant (i) a mirror-commuting complex that must fail chirality, (ii) an intentionally non-Krein mirror-breaking candidate that must be rejected, and (iii) an acyclic toy complex that must pass the algebraic gate. Then evaluate every source-compatible candidate blind to its label.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Prove enumeration completeness and a lifting criterion separating principal-symbol exactness from a square-zero global BV differential on the chosen domain.
- **Suggested falsification test:** The test has no power if a planted mirror-preserving singular/paired control passes as physical or the acyclic control fails. With controls valid, absence of any candidate preserving \(3E_+\) while contracting \(3E_-\oplus X\) is a bounded class obstruction; a surviving candidate advances only to global validation.

### P55. Hashgraph / Gossip-About-Gossip Provenance Researcher

- **Summary of understanding:** The repository preserves a long causal history of conjectures, tests, corrections, and scoped no-gos. Its construction-space map is append-honest, but much of the “who established what from which assumptions, and what was known when” reconstruction still lives in prose, dated filenames, and correction banners.
- **Strongest insight:** **Cross-domain analogy:** a claim-and-evidence event DAG would make the repo's real strength—self-correction—queryable. Vertices would be claims, assumptions, tests, corrections, and scope decisions; edges would distinguish support, dependence, contradiction, supersession, and construction fork. This is provenance tooling, not a proof of physics.
- **Strongest criticism:** A reader can encounter a superseded strong statement before its correction or fail to see that two apparently independent results share one source assumption. The history is preserved, but its causal topology is not uniformly machine-addressable.
- **Hidden assumptions:** Filenames and frontmatter identify every dependency; correction banners propagate to every downstream consumer; timestamps approximate epistemic order; and repository readers can distinguish a later reinterpretation from new evidence.
- **Rose:** The repo retains retractions, verification receipts, and branch-local no-gos rather than rewriting history, while `lab/process/construction-space-map.json` forbids silent revisits and attaches evidence pointers to dispositions.
- **Bud:** Build a read-only derived provenance DAG for the B.5 and continuum-wall arcs, including the \(T_{192}\) retirement, post-hoc `sig` retraction, corrected operator expression, and compact-domain scoping. Attach hashes to evidence artifacts so regenerated views detect drift.
- **Thorn:** The DAG must label the **native GU** and **conventional physics** forks on every load-bearing node. Otherwise a conventional positive-Hilbert obstruction may appear to supersede a native Krein claim, or a native survivor may be misreported as standard-physics validation. The hashgraph analogy does not turn repeated records into scientific consensus.
- **Confidence:** 8/10
- **Suggested experiment:** Generate the two-arc DAG without editing claim sources, then answer automated queries: which active claims depend on a retracted node, which results share assumptions, which no-gos are fork-local, and whether every correction reaches all active summaries.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Formalize a provenance-consistency invariant: no active high-grade claim may have an unacknowledged dependency path through a retracted or lower-grade node, and every supersession relation must induce an acyclic partial order.
- **Suggested falsification test:** If an active summary or next-step priority is reachable only through the retired \(T_{192}\) dictionary, post-hoc scattering involution, or an unscoped continuum claim, the current provenance layer fails. If the generated DAG cannot distinguish fork and grade without subjective annotation, the proposed automation is under-specified.

### P56. Avalanche-Class Consensus Researcher

- **Summary of understanding:** GU accumulates confidence through many tests, hostile rechecks, and independent derivations, but scientific status is determined by proof and evidence grade rather than a quorum. The construction-space program is better understood as sequential hypothesis elimination than as consensus formation.
- **Strongest insight:** **Cross-domain analogy:** repeated subsampling can audit whether a research verdict is metastably robust to admissible changes in assumptions, evidence subsets, and probe order. It can reveal fragile conclusions and correlated “independent” checks, but it cannot convert agreement into a theorem.
- **Strongest criticism:** Counts such as many passing scripts or several agreeing methods can exaggerate confidence when they inherit the same representation ledger, source reconstruction, normalization, or boundary choice. The repo needs an assumption-overlap measure alongside witness counts.
- **Hidden assumptions:** Hostile agents and tests are conditionally independent; threshold choices are frozen before results; repeated probes sample the relevant construction space; and convergence of rankings implies robustness rather than shared bias.
- **Rose:** The program uses adversarial twins, correction receipts, and predeclared outcomes, and `lab/process/construction-space-exploration-protocol.md` requires coverage and first-falsification tests instead of treating repeated agreement as truth.
- **Bud:** Add an assumption-overlap matrix to the construction ledger and stress-test top conclusions by repeatedly sampling admissible assumption/evidence subsets. Stable results should retain their grade; unstable ones should expose the specific pivotal dependency.
- **Thorn:** Never pool the **native GU** and **conventional physics** forks into one confidence process. A positive-Hilbert/BRST success and a Krein/super-IG failure are different states, not votes on one proposition. Likewise, a stable majority of personas or scripts is not evidence beyond the underlying receipts.
- **Confidence:** 7/10
- **Suggested experiment:** For the B.5 and four-sector construction candidates, predeclare admissible assumption toggles and dependency clusters, repeatedly subsample non-overlapping evidence groups, and measure how often the disposition and next-probe ranking change. Report pivotal assumptions rather than a single consensus score.
- **Suggested theorem or mathematical direction:** Develop a robustness bound for a finite dependency hypergraph: conditions under which a disposition remains invariant after removal of any one shared assumption cluster, with an explicit certificate when it is supported by genuinely disjoint witnesses.
- **Suggested falsification test:** Remove or perturb each common source assumption, normalization, and domain choice in turn. If the conclusion flips frequently, or all nominally independent witnesses collapse when one shared node is removed, the confidence-accumulation analogy has exposed fragility and the verdict must not be described as metastably supported.
