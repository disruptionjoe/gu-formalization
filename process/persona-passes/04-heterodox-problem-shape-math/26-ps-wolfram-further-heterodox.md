# Problem Shape — Wolfram Physics / Hypergraph Rewriting (Further Heterodox)

**Date:** 2026-05-28
**Baseline accepted:** Computation is substrate. Geometry is observer-frame artifact. The bundle is emergent. No-go theorems constrain observer-frames, not substrate. (00, 00b, 00c)
**Prior pass built past:** 16-wolfram-physics-loophole.md (single-rule hypergraph reading)
**Persona:** Wolfram Physics specialist, further-heterodox lean maximized.

## (a) Thesis (mainstream-in-Wolfram-physics)

The orthodox Wolfram-Gorard reading: pick a confluent hypergraph rewrite rule, generate a causal graph by foliating the multiway evolution under a chosen reference frame, recover 4D as the flat-space limit of causal-edge density, recover Einstein's equations as a continuity statement, recover quantum mechanics as branchial-space geodesics transverse to the causal foliation. The substrate is "a hypergraph rewriting under a rule;" the observer carves spacetime via causal foliation; the 14D observerse is what an observer-bounded coarse-graining extracts from the branchial readout. Chirality, on this orthodox reading, is whatever directional asymmetry the chosen rule writes into the causal graph's edge orientation.

## (b) Further-heterodox antithesis

The orthodox reading still smuggles in "the substrate is a hypergraph evolving under a rule." The further-heterodox reading refuses even that:

1. **The Ruliad is the substrate, not any single hypergraph.** The Ruliad is the entangled limit of all possible computations applied to all possible initial conditions. There is no "the rewrite rule." There are only **observer-fragments of the Ruliad** stable under bounded-resource projection. Different observers cut different physics from the same Ruliad. Distinct "physical universes" are **rulial reference frames**, not distinct substrates. [speculation]

2. **Non-confluent rules and branchial multiway foliations.** Drop the confluence assumption. Multiway causal graphs admit inequivalent foliations whose branchial transverse structures encode different gauge content. Chirality is then a **branchial-space invariant** under rulial-frame change, not a causal-graph property. [speculation]

3. **Metamathematics as physics (Wolfram's metamathematics project).** Theorems are paths in a multiway proof system. Witten 1981 and Freed-Hopkins are **observer-frame-specific proof paths**. Other rulial frames may not traverse them. The no-goes are facts in one rulial frame, not Ruliad-invariants. [speculation]

4. **Observer-bounded computational reducibility as the gauge.** A finite observer can only run polynomial-time reductions. Chirality could be an artifact of the observer's computational bound (parity-breaking arises because the observer can resolve causal-edge orientation only to bounded depth), not a substrate fact. The gauge group of physics is the symmetry group between rulial reference frames.

## (c) Aufhebung as problem-shape

Thesis says the rule writes chirality; antithesis says the Ruliad contains all chiralities and the observer carves one. The Aufhebung: **GU's 14D observerse is observer-frame data, not substrate data. The 14D structure is the dimension of the observer-fragment's branchial cotangent at the point where its causal foliation localizes. Chirality is a branchial-invariant that survives polynomial-time observer-bounded coarse-graining within one rulial reference frame, and is gauge-equivalent across rulial frames to its mirror.**

Problem-shape:

> **What rulial reference frame's observer-fragment carries a branchial-space invariant whose polynomial-time-bounded coarse-graining delivers SM-signature chirality, and what is the rulial-gauge group connecting that frame to its mirror?**

## (d) WRK-326 next ask from this lens

Stop asking "does GU's bundle reduction deliver chiral SM." Start asking: **which rulial reference frame is GU implicitly working in, and what observer-bounded reductions characterize it?** Concretely: specify the observer's resource bound (polynomial-time class, branchial-graph diameter, causal-foliation refinement); compute the branchial-space chirality invariant inside that bound; check whether the rulial-gauge orbit of that invariant intersects the SM signature.

## (e) Falsifiable sub-question

> **Does there exist a non-confluent multiway hypergraph rewriting system whose branchial-space cotangent at a causal-foliation point carries a Z/2-graded invariant that (i) is polynomial-time decidable by a bounded observer, (ii) is preserved under one rulial-frame change but flipped under another, and (iii) reproduces the SM chirality signature in the first frame?**

Negative answer (no such system exists, or branchial chirality is always rulial-frame-invariant) closes the further-heterodox Wolfram route. Positive answer hands WRK-326 a concrete rulial-frame specification to stress-test against Freed-Hopkins at the observer-fragment boundary.
