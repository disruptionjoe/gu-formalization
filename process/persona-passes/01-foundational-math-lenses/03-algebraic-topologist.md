# Persona Pass 03: Algebraic Topologist

Discipline: cohomology, characteristic classes, homotopy of classifying spaces, K-theory, spectral sequences. First-principles only.

## (a) Strongest leverage

Questions 1 and 3. The dimension count and the spin/fermion content are both controlled by classifying spaces and characteristic classes, where my tools are sharp. Question 2 I can constrain but not derive. Question 4 is mostly outside my discipline (the GR limit is differential-geometric; the QFT limit is analytic).

## (b) First-principles construction attempt

Take a smooth oriented 4-manifold M. To build a 14-dim total space E -> M fibered with 10-dim fiber F, I need a structure group G acting on F. The classifying map is M -> BG, and the bundle is pulled back from the universal bundle over BG. Natural candidate for F when we want to "encode geometry of M": the bundle of metrics, or the orthonormal frame bundle SO(4)-quotient structure. The fiber Met(R^4) = GL(4,R)/O(4) has dimension 10 (symmetric 4x4 matrices). So 4 + 10 = 14 is forced by this choice, not arbitrary. This is the one place the dimension count is non-mystical: BO(4) governs the obstruction theory, and Met(R^4) is contractible (positive-definite symmetric matrices form a convex cone), so the bundle is trivial up to homotopy.

## (c) What fails or is forced

The bundle of metrics being contractible-fibered is fatal for the "extract gauge structure from the total space" move. H^*(E; Z) = H^*(M; Z) as graded rings via the projection. No new cohomology, no new characteristic classes, no new topology. Whatever gauge group you find on E was already encoded on M.

For the Standard Model: G_SM = SU(3) x SU(2) x U(1). H^*(BG_SM; Z) is a polynomial ring on Chern classes c_2(SU(3)), c_2(SU(2)), c_1(U(1)) in degrees 4, 4, 2. There is no canonical map BO(4) -> BG_SM. Equivalently, no natural homotopy class of map from the frame bundle's classifying data to BG_SM. The Standard Model group does not emerge from the topology of metric-bundles on 4-manifolds. It must be put in by hand. [speculation] One could try to embed G_SM into a larger group whose classifying space receives a natural map from BO(4) or BSpin(4), but the embedding choice is itself the input.

For fermions: w_2(M) in H^2(M; Z/2) is the obstruction to a spin structure. If w_2 != 0, no spinors at all. If w_2 = 0, spin structures form a torsor over H^1(M; Z/2). K-theory KO^*(M) classifies real vector bundles and via Atiyah-Singer governs the Dirac operator's index. None of this requires the 14-dim construction; it lives on M directly. The 10 extra dimensions add nothing to the fermion story unless they carry their own spin structure, which then must be put in by hand on the fiber.

## (d) Named obstructions

- Contractibility of Met(R^4) collapses H^*(E) -> H^*(M); no new characteristic classes generated by the lift.
- w_2(M) in H^2(M; Z/2): spin obstruction, lives on M, not produced by the construction.
- No canonical homotopy class [BO(4) -> BG_SM]; the Standard Model group is not a topological consequence.
- Pontryagin classes p_1, p_2 of M are the only natural degree-4 and degree-8 classes; insufficient to generate the c_1, c_2 spectrum of G_SM.
- Serre spectral sequence for E -> M with contractible fiber degenerates trivially: E_2 = H^*(M), no transgression to produce gauge curvature.

## (e) Verdict

The 14-dim total space is dimensionally natural but topologically empty; it cannot generate the Standard Model gauge group or fermion content without putting them in by hand on the fiber.
