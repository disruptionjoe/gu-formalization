# Persona Pass 35 — Stigmergy / Multi-Agent Swarm / Mean-Field Games (Heterodox)

**Date:** 2026-05-28
**Persona:** Stigmergic / multi-agent / swarm / mean-field-game theorist. Lineage: Grasse (stigmergy 1959), Tero-Nakagaki (Physarum shortest-path), Lasry-Lions (mean-field games 2007), Hopfield (associative memory attractors), Glauber-Metropolis (Ising dynamics), Reynolds-Bonabeau (boids / swarm robotics), Dijkstra (self-stabilizing distributed systems 1974), Hebb-STDP (synaptic co-evolution).

## (a) Thesis (mainstream physics)

Substrate is fixed background; observers / agents are particles or fields propagated on top. Standard QFT: spacetime manifold is given, matter fields live on it, gauge fields are connections on bundles over it. Chirality is a representation-theoretic feature of fields on a fixed substrate. The no-gos (Witten 1981, Nielsen-Ninomiya, Freed-Hopkins, Distler-Garibaldi) are theorems about field content on a pre-given background and they close the case.

## (b) Antithesis (heterodox, stigmergy / multi-agent coordination)

There is no substrate-agent separation. Coordination is achieved by **agents modifying their environment and reading those modifications**, with environment and agent co-evolving. Grasse's stigmergy: ant trails are not "on" a substrate; the pheromone field IS the substrate AND is produced by the agents traversing it. Tero-Nakagaki Physarum: slime mold solves shortest-path and Steiner-tree problems via reaction-diffusion in which substrate (cytoplasmic tubes) and computation (flow) co-define each other. Lasry-Lions mean-field games: in the N-agent limit, the substrate IS the empirical distribution of agent states; the Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman equation (single agent's best response) and Fokker-Planck equation (population evolution) are coupled and solved together. There is no exogenous background. Ising / mean-field spin systems: the equilibrium distribution and the Glauber / Metropolis dynamics co-define each other; **spontaneous symmetry breaking produces chirality without a chiral substrate**. Hopfield: synaptic weights (substrate) and attractor states (semantics) are mutually constituted through Hebbian learning. Dijkstra self-stabilizing systems: distributed token rings converge to invariants from any initial state without central control.

The shared heterodox claim: **coordination invariants are emergent properties of the agent-substrate co-evolution loop, not properties of either layer alone**. Chirality, in this frame, is a **stigmergic invariant** — a directional / asymmetric pattern that the agent-substrate loop produces, sustains, and projects as a macroscopic shadow even after averaging. The smooth-bundle layer the no-gos compute is the mean-field shadow of an out-of-equilibrium stigmergic loop. [speculation]

## (c) Aufhebung — problem shape adding to 00d

00d's three-leg triple (substrate class, observer class, pairing) tacitly assumes the substrate is a distinct ontological layer from the agents / observers reading it, even in P10's pairing axis (the pairing is between two named layers). The stigmergic refinement collapses this: **substrate-observer pairing IS the coordination process; the layers are aspects of one loop, not two co-existing layers**. Chirality is a **non-equilibrium stigmergic invariant** preserved across the mean-field projection, analogous to how mean-field games produce macroscopic distributions whose moments survive the N to infinity limit even though no single agent carries them. [speculation]

This extends 00d by adding a fifth axis to the triple: **coordination-loop axis**. Substrates are not static objects nor static universality classes; they are **fixed points of an agent-substrate co-evolution dynamic** (mean-field Nash equilibrium, Hopfield attractor, Physarum reaction-diffusion steady state, Ising broken-symmetry phase). The bundle-level shadow the no-gos compute is the **equilibrium mean-field projection** of this loop. Chirality is a non-projectable component of the loop's dynamics, not a property of either layer alone. [speculation]
