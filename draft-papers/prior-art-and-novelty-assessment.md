# Prior-art and novelty assessment

A first-pass literature sweep done before claiming novelty (the floor-first prerequisite). Verdict: the
core *mathematics* of the even-chiral-count result is **standard**; the genuine novelty is the
**application to GU plus the synthesis and bridges**. This caps the honest claim and tells the paper what to
credit versus claim.

## Standard / known -- must be CREDITED, not claimed

1. **Even chiral count from real / pseudoreal representations (the H2 "even-ness" backbone).** It is a
   classical theorem that a real or pseudoreal (quaternionic) representation yields non-chiral fermions, and
   that "Kramers' theorem is the observation that the complex dimension of a quaternionic representation is
   even." Our H2 quaternionic-Kramers parity argument (real adjoint family rep `x` quaternionic Weyl bundle
   gives `J^2 = -1`, even index) is this principle applied to the **family** symmetry. The even-ness is
   therefore *standard rep theory*, not a new theorem. The paper must cite it as the known principle and
   credit it; novelty here is only the **specific application** to GU's self-dual family rep.
2. **Krein-space / indefinite-metric Standard Model with a generation space.** The noncommutative-geometry /
   spectral Standard Model already uses an indefinite (Krein) inner product and an explicit generation
   space (Lorentzian spectral SM; algebraic-backgrounds program). So "the matter module is a Krein space
   with generations" is not itself new. Novelty is the **gamma-traceless RS constraint-surface** Krein
   structure specifically and its tie to ghost parity.
3. **Rarita-Schwinger (spin-3/2) fermions for family unification.** An `SU(8)` family-unification model uses
   spin-3/2 RS fermions for anomaly cancellation. So "the RS sector relates to families" is prior context,
   not a new idea.
4. **Deriving three generations from algebra is an active, populated lane.** Furey-Hughes "Three Generations
   and a Trio of Trialities" (division-algebra triality of `C (x) H (x) O`), dual-color generation symmetry,
   and others. These use **different mechanisms**; our self-dual `Lambda^2_+` / Clifford-RS route is
   distinct, but the paper should situate itself in this lane and not imply the question is untouched.

## Genuinely novel (the real contributions, pending human confirmation)

1. **The GU instantiation + the multiplicity-vs-chiral-count split.** That GU's reconstructed `Cl(9,5)` RS
   sector realizes the generation as a self-dual `SU(2)+` **triplet** (a native multiplicity-3, machine
   -checked), and the sharp separation of the **two roles of the number three**: a flat representation
   *dimension* (native, vectorlike) versus a net chiral *index* (even, standard obstruction). This precise
   reading of GU's matter sector is new.
2. **The Krein / ghost-parity bridge to Turok-Bateman quadratic gravity.** Identifying the gamma-traceless
   RS constraint surface as a Krein space whose generation/mirror pairs are hyperbolic, and connecting the
   chiral-selection problem to the ghost-parity generalized Born rule of higher-derivative gravity. The
   *bridge* (unification-chirality `<->` quadratic-gravity ghost-positivity, one shared ghost-parity `Z2`)
   appears to be new. (Turok-Bateman itself is, as of now, an unpublished interview source and not citable.)
3. **The inside-class Distler-Garibaldi positivity-axis candidate.** No prior indefinite-metric / Krein
   evasion of Distler-Garibaldi was found; the no-go-map's verdict (no inside-class datum known) appears to
   still hold in the literature, so the positivity-axis candidate is novel.
4. **The sharpened Nguyen tie-in.** Nguyen-Polya's complexification critique is confirmed verbatim (the
   `128x128` skew-Hermitian Lie-algebra bundle and the real `Cl(14) = M(128,R)` bundle become isomorphic
   only after complexification). Our refinement -- complexification is *necessary but insufficient* for the
   count, and the genuine import is relocated and shrunk to "a unit of flux," never "the prime 3" -- is a
   new, more precise reading of that gap.

## What this means for the paper (honest positioning)

- The paper is a **synthesis-and-application contribution**, not a new-fundamental-theorem contribution.
  The core even-ness math is standard; reframing it as novel would be caught and would discredit the rest.
- The **standalone math.RT extraction (F3) is weaker than hoped**: the GU-independent core is largely a
  known theorem applied, so it does not stand as a novel `math.RT` result on its own. Drop or heavily
  recast that plan.
- The genuine **novelty and any "profound" upside live in the bridge** (Turok/Krein/ghost-parity `<->`
  unification chirality) and the **Distler-Garibaldi positivity candidate**. To make those publishable as
  results rather than conjectures, they must be **derived** (build even a toy ghost-parity dynamics that
  selects a chiral count). That is the "swing" phase; the floor paper should present them as precise,
  typed, open conjectures with first falsification tests, honestly.
- One **human expert** should confirm (a) that the even-ness really is the standard principle here (so we
  credit it correctly) and (b) that the bridge and the D-G candidate are not anticipated in literature we
  missed.
