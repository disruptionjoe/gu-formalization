# Generation multiplicity versus chirality in a Clifford Rarita-Schwinger sector

*A representation-theory and index-theory note, with Geometric Unity as the motivating case.*

## 1. Abstract

We study a Clifford Rarita-Schwinger (RS) sector, the gamma-traceless module of a real Clifford algebra `Cl(p,q)` with `p+q = 14` acting on a 128 complex-dimensional spinor module, and we separate two questions that are easy to conflate: how many copies of a fixed unified-spinor representation the sector carries (its *multiplicity*), and how many *net chiral* (left minus right) copies it carries. These are different invariants, and the integer 3 plays a different role in each.

On the multiplicity side we report a positive fact [machine-checked]. Under the self-dual `su(2)_+` of any oriented 4-base (the rank-3 bundle of self-dual two-forms `Lambda^2_+`), the gamma-traceless module decomposes as `640` singlets plus `416` doublets plus `64` triplets, and exactly the triplet channel carries the pure `16` of `Spin(10)` (its `Spin(10)` Casimir matches the reference `16 / 16bar` value to machine precision). A native multiplicity-3 object exists. We also give a short *spinor 2-smoothness lemma* [proof-grade elementary lemma]: every spinor family channel has power-of-two multiplicity, hence is never divisible by 3, so an odd multiplicity can come only from a non-spinor (here self-dual) family channel. Combined with an enumeration of the `su(2)` subalgebras commuting with the unification group [machine-checked], this makes the self-dual route the canonical odd-multiplicity route *for family symmetries commuting with the full* `Spin(10)`; the count it forces is the triplet, robustly 3. The case of a family symmetry commuting only with the Standard-Model subgroup is left open.

On the chirality side we report an obstruction. The natural invariant inner product is indefinite (a Krein form), and on the generation triplet it is purely cross-chirality [machine-checked]: the same-chirality blocks vanish, so every maximal positive-norm subspace is half left and half right and the net chiral count is zero. The triplet is vectorlike. Turning on a net count requires gauging a family `su(2)` by topology on the base and reading an Atiyah-Singer index, and every such native route is *even* (`4k` for the self-dual adjoint, `12k` over the full multiplicity bundle), robust by a base-side quaternionic-Kramers parity argument together with Rokhlin's theorem [standard math applied; one human-expert check requested]. An odd net 3 is reachable only off the pseudoreal anti-self-dual doublet, and only by importing a unit of topological flux together with a sub-sector truncation.

Stated representation-theoretically: multiplicity and net chiral count are distinct invariants; the multiplicity-3 is native, while the net chiral 3 is a separate datum the internal algebra does not fix. The identification of the `16` of `Spin(10)` with a physical Standard-Model generation, and the reading of all of this against Geometric Unity (GU), are reconstruction-grade and collected as remarks in Section 8; they are not part of the core. This note takes no position on whether GU is correct.

## 2. Introduction and grading convention

This note isolates one representation-theoretic distinction in an explicit algebraic setting and reports exactly which side of it is forced by the internal data and which is not.

The setting is a Rarita-Schwinger matter sector realized as Clifford-algebraic data: a real `Cl(p,q)` with `p+q = 14`, acting on `V (x) S` where `V` is the 14-dimensional vector and `S` the 128-dimensional spinor, subject to the gamma-trace constraint that defines the irreducible (gamma-traceless) part. The question is whether the internal data fixes a copy count, and if so in what sense.

The answer separates two integers that are routinely conflated:

- a **multiplicity**, the dimension of the space in which one copy of a fixed representation sits inside the constrained module, and
- a **net chiral count**, an index (an analytic or topological asymmetry between left- and right-handed zero modes).

These are different invariants. We show the sector fixes the first (a native multiplicity-3) and does not fix the second (every native index route is even or zero; an odd net 3 requires an imported datum).

**The motivating case.** The construction was generated by interrogating Geometric Unity, the proposal of Eric Weinstein, which posits a 14-dimensional observerse and a Rarita-Schwinger matter sector. GU is why this particular `Cl(p,q)`, this `Spin(10)` content, and the self-dual family structure were singled out. We treat GU strictly as motivation: the results in Sections 3 to 7 stand on the explicit algebra alone, and the GU reading is collected in Section 8. We take no position on whether GU is correct, and we vindicate or refute no author.

**Grading convention (applied to every load-bearing claim).**

- **[machine-checked]** : exact linear algebra verified by the scripts in `tests/generation-sector/`, on the explicit `Cl(9,5) = M(64,H)` representation (residuals at or below `1e-13`).
- **[standard theorem]** : a textbook fact (Clifford-module classification, Atiyah-Singer, Rokhlin) cited, not re-derived.
- **[proof-grade elementary lemma]** : a short original proof assembled from standard facts (the spinor 2-smoothness lemma of Section 4.2).
- **[standard math applied]** : standard mathematics applied to the fixed representation content of this sector; analytically careful but not a new machine-checked computation. The index-theory layer of Section 5 carries this grade and is flagged for one human-expert check.
- **[evidence-grade]** : supported by finite search or finite sampling, not a closed proof.
- **[reconstruction-grade]** : depends on the GU reconstruction, including the identification of the `16` of `Spin(10)` with a Standard-Model generation. Confined to Section 8 and to clearly marked sentences.
- **[open]** / **[speculation]** : not established; named as such.

## 3. The crux: the two roles of the integer 3

It saves the reader trouble to state the organizing idea before the computations, because the whole result is one instance of it.

A "count of 3" can mean two arithmetically different things.

1. **A multiplicity (a dimension).** The number of independent copies of a representation living in a flat family multiplicity space. This is the *dimension* of a representation. When that space is the self-dual two-form bundle `Lambda^2_+` of a 4-base, its dimension is 3. A flat multiplicity space comes with its mirror: 3 copies plus 3 mirror copies, vectorlike, no left-right imbalance.

2. **A net chiral count (an index).** The signed difference, left zero modes minus right zero modes, of a Dirac operator coupled to a gauge field on the base. This is an *index*, an Atiyah-Singer integer, and it measures chirality, the property a vectorlike spectrum lacks.

A representation dimension and an operator index are not one number computed two ways; they are different quantities that happen, in casual speech, to share the word "three." The finding of this note is that the sector's internal data supplies the first (the multiplicity, natively, 3) and does not supply the second (the net chiral count), and that the second, when one tries to manufacture it, comes out *even* on every native route.

This is why the earlier framing ("a count of 3 is equivalent to importing the prime 3") is retired. That framing treated 3 as a single object to be either found inside or imported. The correct statement is that 3 appears natively as a multiplicity dimension and is *absent* as a native net chiral index, and what must be imported to get the chiral count is not "the prime 3" but a single unit of topological flux (which then rides the native 3). The import is relocated and shrunk, not eliminated, and it is not the integer 3.

## 4. The GU-independent core

The results in this section are facts about a `Cl(p,q)` Rarita-Schwinger module and the self-dual geometry of a 4-base. They do not require the GU reconstruction; GU is only what motivates looking at `p+q = 14` with a 4 plus 10 split.

### 4.1 Setup: the module, the constraint, and the `16` of `Spin(10)`

Let `Cl(p,q)`, `p+q = 14`, act on its spinor module `S` of complex dimension `2^7 = 128`. For the signatures of interest (including the `(9,5)` used for the explicit checks) the real Clifford algebra is `M(64,H)`, acting on `H^64` (real dimension 256, quaternionic dimension 64); the 128 is the complex dimension of the spinor carrying a quaternionic structure. We keep this distinction explicit because the chirality discussion later turns on reality type. The matter module is `V (x) S` with `V` the 14-dimensional vector, of total complex dimension `14 x 128 = 1792`.

**The gamma-trace constraint [machine-checked].** The gamma-traceless part is the kernel of the contraction `Gamma : V (x) S -> S`. Because every `gamma^a` is invertible, `Gamma` is surjective onto the 128-dimensional spinor, so

```
dim ker(Gamma) = (14 - 1) * 128 = 1664 = 2^7 * 13,
```

a total module dimension, signature-independent, verified across nine signatures spanning both real and quaternionic module classes (`signature_sweep_fast.py`, `signature_77_rerun.py`: `rank(Gamma) = 128`, `ker = 1664` in every case). Per chirality the count is `(14-1) * 64 = 832 = 2^6 * 13`. None of `1664`, `832` is divisible by 3.

**A non-sequitur we do not use.** It is tempting to argue from `1664 = 2^7 * 13` (no factor of 3) that a multiplicity cannot be 3. That inference is invalid: `1664` is a total dimension, and 3-freeness of a total dimension does not imply 3-freeness of a branching multiplicity. The multiplicity results below rest on the explicit family decomposition and a structural lemma, not on the arithmetic of `1664`. We retire that arithmetic as a multiplicity argument and keep it only as the correct, narrow statement about the constraint-surface dimension.

**The `16` of `Spin(10)`.** Under the chain `Spin(14) ⊃ Spin(10) x Spin(4)`, the spinor carries the `16` of `Spin(10)` [standard branching]. The identification of this `16` with a physical Standard-Model generation (a right-handed neutrino, anomaly-freedom, hypercharges `{0, ±1/3, ±2/3, ±1}`) is the GU reading and is **[reconstruction-grade]**; it is deferred to Section 8. Throughout the core we speak of "the `16` of `Spin(10)`" as a representation-theoretic object, and ask what fixes the *number* of copies of it.

**Anchors.** Two scalar invariants pin the reconstruction and are asserted as guards in every script: `||[Pi_RS, M_D]|| = 58.7215` and `C2 = ||Gamma M_D Pi_RS|| = 155.3625`, with Clifford error 0, numerically identical across signatures.

### 4.2 A native self-dual triplet: multiplicity 3 exists [machine-checked]

For any oriented 4-base, the self-dual two-forms `Lambda^2_+` form a rank-3 bundle and generate a self-dual `su(2)_+` (one factor of `so(4) = su(2)_+ ⊕ su(2)_-`) acting on `V (x) S` through both the vector and the spinor index. The three generators are the self-dual combinations `(01+23), (02+31), (03+12)`.

We build these explicitly, verify they close to a genuine `su(2)` (`[J_0, J_1] = -2 J_2`, residual `0`) and commute with the gamma-trace projector (`max ||[J, Pi]|| = 0`, so they act on `ker(Gamma)`), and decompose the constrained module under the Casimir (`h1_selfdual_family_kill.py`):

```
ker(Gamma) = 1664  =  640 (j=0)  +  416 (j=1/2)  +  64 (j=1)
                   =  640 singlets  +  416 doublets  +  64 triplets
                   =  640*1 + 416*2 + 64*3.
```

The triplet sector has dimension `192 = 64 * 3`. On all 192 states the `Spin(10)` Casimir equals the reference value of the `16 / 16bar` spinor (`-11.25`, spread `1.7e-13`), so the triplet channel carries the pure `16` of `Spin(10)`, three copies of it.

The intuition is elementary: the vector index contains the base vector `4 = (2,2)` of `su(2)_+ x su(2)_-`, and `2 (x) 2 = 3 + 1`; the odd 3 is the symmetric (self-dual) piece. A native multiplicity-3 family exists in this module, carried by the geometry of any 4-base. (This refutes the claim that the multiplicity *dimension* 3 must be imported from outside the algebra; it does not by itself supply a net chiral count, which Section 4.4 and Sections 5 to 7 address.)

### 4.3 The spinor 2-smoothness lemma and the odd route

The triplet is not just *a* route to multiplicity 3; for family symmetries commuting with the full unification group it is the canonical one, and a one-paragraph lemma says why.

**Spinor 2-smoothness lemma [proof-grade elementary lemma].** *Let a family symmetry `SO(m)` act on the matter module so that the multiplicity space is a representation of `SO(m)`. If that space is a sum of spinor representations of `SO(m)`, its dimension is a power of two, hence not divisible by 3. An odd multiplicity can therefore arise only from a non-spinor family representation (a vector, an adjoint, or a self-dual / principal-`su(2)` representation), whose dimension may be odd.*

*Proof.* The Dirac spinor of `SO(m)` has dimension `2^floor(m/2)`; the half-spinors of `SO(2k)` have dimension `2^(k-1)`. All are powers of two, and a power of two is never divisible by the odd prime 3. The vector (`m`), the adjoint (`m(m-1)/2`), and self-dual tensors are not so constrained; in particular `Lambda^2_+` of a 4-manifold has dimension 3. QED.

This converts an open-ended search ("is there any odd multiplicity?") into a sharp dichotomy: the spinor channels are provably 3-free, so any odd count must enter through a non-spinor family channel, which is exactly the self-dual `Lambda^2_+` of Section 4.2.

**Leg-3 closure [machine-checked for the commutant of the full unification group].** Inside `Spin(14) ⊃ Spin(10) x Spin(4)` the commutant of `Spin(10)` is `so(4) = su(2)_+ ⊕ su(2)_-`. Up to conjugacy `so(4)` has exactly three `su(2)` subalgebras: self-dual, anti-self-dual, and the diagonal (vector) `su(2)`. No `su(3)` embeds, by dimension (`dim su(3) = 8 > 6 = dim so(4)`). Enumerating the 16-dimensional multiplicity space under each (`leg3_family_embedding_enumeration.py`):

| family `su(2)` | multiplicity-space content | odd piece |
| --- | --- | --- |
| self-dual `su(2)_+` | `2(j=0) + 4(j=1/2) + 2(j=1)` | triplet `j=1`, dim 3 |
| anti-self-dual `su(2)_-` | `2(j=0) + 4(j=1/2) + 2(j=1)` (parity image) | triplet `j=1`, dim 3 |
| diagonal / vector `su(2)` | `4(j=1/2) + 2(j=3/2)` | none (all even) |

So the only `su(2)` family embeddings producing an odd multiplicity are the self-dual and anti-self-dual ones (related by orientation), and the odd piece is always the triplet, dimension 3, never a quintet or higher. The self-dual `su(2)_+` is the canonical odd-multiplicity route, and the count it forces is the triplet, 3.

**Honest residual [open].** This closes the question for family symmetries commuting with the *full* `Spin(10)`. A family symmetry commuting only with the Standard-Model subgroup `G_SM ⊂ Spin(10)` has a larger commutant in which a horizontal `su(3)` could in principle live. That case is not covered here; it is a bounded next computation, and the lemma already constrains it (any odd multiplicity it produced would again have to come through a non-spinor family channel).

**A corroborating finite search [evidence-grade].** Across all 21 maximal-rank `D5` branchings times the five native representations `{64, 64bar, 128, 14, 91}`, zero cases give a multiplicity or net-chiral count of 3 (`leg4_branching_multiplicity_search.py`). This is a finite enumeration, not a closed proof of non-existence, and is reported as such.

**Bridge between the two triplet counts.** Two "triplet" figures appear and must be reconciled. The leg-3 *multiplicity space* is 16-dimensional and contains 2 copies of the `su(2)_+` triplet (the `j=1` piece with multiplicity 2, which is exactly the `su(2)_-` doublet, so the object is a `(3,2)` of `su(2)_+ x su(2)_-`). The H1 decomposition of the full `ker(Gamma)` contains 64 triplets. The factor relating them is `32 = 16 + 16bar`: each abstract slot of the multiplicity space expands to the 32-dimensional `16 ⊕ 16bar` content of one generation, so `2 x 32 = 64` triplets, filling the `192 = 64 x 3`-dimensional triplet sector (`leg3_family_embedding_enumeration.py` prints the generation sector dimension `512 = 32 x 16`, confirming the factor).

### 4.4 The cross-chirality Krein structure: the triplet is vectorlike [machine-checked]

Because the spinor module is built on a Clifford algebra with timelike directions (`q > 0`), the natural invariant inner product is *indefinite*, a Krein form rather than a positive-definite Hilbert metric. Explicitly the `so(p,q)`-invariant form on `V (x) S` is `K = eta_V (x) beta_S`, where `eta_V` is the vector metric and `beta_S` the spinor Krein metric (Hermitian, `beta_S^2 = I`, pseudo-anti-Hermitian against every `so(p,q)` generator, residual `0` to machine precision). On the full 1792-dimensional module `K` has signature `(+896, -896)`; restricted to the 192-dimensional triplet it has signature exactly `(+96, -96, 0)` in `(9,5)`, `(7,7)`, and `(14,0)` (`ghost_parity_krein.py`).

The structural fact (`t1a_kinematic_chirality_kill.py`) is that on the triplet `K` is *purely cross-chirality*: the same-chirality blocks vanish, `||K(+,+)|| ≈ ||K(-,-)|| ≈ 1.8e-14`, so the form pairs a generation only with its mirror. It follows that every maximal positive-norm ("physical") subspace is exactly half left and half right, with net chirality zero. This "every" is a *structural consequence* of the vanishing same-chirality blocks; it is machine-verified directly for the one explicitly constructed positive-norm subspace (net `≈ 1e-15`, in both `(9,5)` and `(7,7)`, real and quaternionic spinor classes), and inferred for all such subspaces from the block structure, not separately enumerated over every subspace.

The plain reading: the native triplet is *vectorlike*, three copies plus three mirrors, bound in 96 hyperbolic (generation, mirror) null pairs (`96 = 3` self-dual triplet `x 2` doublet `x 16`). No purely kinematic operation, no algebraic parity or generation-mirror swap, can turn it into a net-chiral 3. A net chiral count is an index, identically zero at the representation-theory level, and it can become nonzero only through base-manifold topology or through a dynamics that selects the physical half of each pair. This pins exactly where any missing chirality must come from, and is the cleanest statement of why multiplicity (present) and chiral count (absent) are different invariants.

## 5. The parity obstruction: every native index route is even

Section 4.4 says the chiral count must come from a base-manifold Dirac index. The natural construction gauges a family `su(2)` by an instanton on a compact Euclidean 4-base `X` and lets Atiyah-Singer supply a net chiral count. This section shows every such native route is *even*. This layer is **[standard math applied]**: Atiyah-Singer index theory, Dynkin indices, a reality / Kramers parity argument, and Rokhlin's theorem, applied to the fixed representation content of Section 4. It is analytically careful but is not a new machine-checked computation; it is the one place we request an independent human-expert check. Where decisive it is over-determined by independent arguments, which is its robustness test.

The Atiyah-Singer index of the Dirac operator coupled to a bundle in family representation `R` is

```
ind(D_R) = integral_X [ -dim(R) * p_1(X)/24 + ch_2(V_R) ],     ind_gauge(D_R) = 2 * T(R) * k,
```

with `T(R)` the Dynkin index and `k` the instanton charge. For `su(2)` spin `j`, `T(j) = (1/3) j(j+1)(2j+1)`, normalized `T(1/2) = 1/2`:

| `j` | rep (dim) | `2 T(R)` | gauge index `2 T k` |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1/2 | doublet (2) | 1 | `k` |
| 1 | adjoint / triplet (3) | 4 | `4k` |
| 3/2 | quartet (4) | 10 | `10k` |
| 2 | quintet (5) | 20 | `20k` |

Two textbook anchors fix the normalization: the fundamental doublet (`T = 1/2`) gives `ind = k` (one left zero mode per doublet per instanton), and the adjoint (`T = 2`) gives `ind = 4k` (gaugino counting). For an (anti-)self-dual connection a Weitzenbock vanishing theorem lets the index be read as a net chiral count, with no cancellation.

The generation occupies a `(3,2)` of `su(2)_+ x su(2)_-` tensored with the `16 ⊕ 16bar` of `Spin(10)` (the 192-dimensional triplet sector is `3 * 2 * 32`). Four statements give even:

**(a) Per irrep.** The generation's `su(2)_+` carrier is the integer-spin adjoint (`T = 2`), index `4k`, divisible by 4.

**(b) Over the full multiplicity bundle.** Summing over the 16-dimensional multiplicity space using the leg-3 decompositions:

```
self-dual su(2)_+     :  2(0) + 4(k)  + 2(4k)   = 12k
anti-self-dual su(2)_-:  2(0) + 4(k)  + 2(4k)   = 12k
diagonal / vector     :  4(k)  + 2(10k)         = 24k
```

Even for every `k`, on every native `su(2)` choice.

**(c) Reality / Kramers parity, the value-independent backbone.** In four Euclidean dimensions `Spin(4) = SU(2) x SU(2)`, and each Weyl bundle `S_+` is quaternionic: there is an antilinear `j_S` with `j_S^2 = -1`, chirality-preserving. If the family representation is *real* (`j_R^2 = +1`, the case for the adjoint / triplet), then `J = j_S (x) j_R` satisfies `J^2 = -1`, commutes with the Dirac operator for any real connection, and preserves chirality. Each kernel is then quaternionic, of even complex dimension, so the index is even for *any* metric, *any* connection, *any* charge, self-dual or not. This is the base-side analogue of the cross-chirality Krein form of Section 4.4, and it is stronger than the bare `4k` formula in one respect: it closes the `SO(3)` fractional-instanton escape (where a fractional `p_1` could naively evade the integer `4k`), because it needs only reality of the adjoint and quaternionicity of `S_+`.

**(d) Gravitational term [standard theorem].** The untwisted index is `Ahat[X] = -sign(X)/8`, and Rokhlin's theorem gives `sign(X) ≡ 0 (mod 16)`, so `dim(R) * Ahat[X] = 3 * (even)` is even: the odd `dim(R) = 3` of the triplet cannot inject oddness through the gravitational channel.

**Scope of the obstruction (the honest correction).** "Even, never odd 3" is true and robust *for the real self-dual adjoint channel and the full multiplicity bundle*. It is *not* a property of the sector's base topology as an undifferentiated whole, because the Kramers argument is confined to real representations. The pseudoreal anti-self-dual `su(2)_-` doublet (`j_R^2 = -1`, so `J^2 = +1`, no Kramers constraint) escapes it. That is exactly the channel where an odd count can appear, and it is the subject of the next section.

**The Lorentzian complexification does not change this.** In Lorentzian `(3,1)` signature `*^2 = -1` on two-forms, so `Lambda^2` splits only over the complexification into two complex rank-3 conjugate pieces (the `sl(2,C)` self-dual / anti-self-dual Weyl structure). At the *object* level this strengthens Section 4.2: the rank-3 self-dual bundle is native to a Lorentzian 4-base, not a Euclidean artifact. At the *count* level even-ness survives for three reasons complexification does not touch: topology lives in the maximal compact (`SL(2,C)` retracts onto `SU(2)`, charge in `pi_3(SU(2)) = Z`); an index is intrinsically elliptic and Euclidean (computed after Wick rotation, where `sl(2,C) -> su(2)` and the Dynkin index is unchanged); and the real theory keeps `Lambda^2_+ ⊕ Lambda^2_- = 3 ⊕ 3bar`, returning the real adjoint where the Kramers argument applies. Complexifying the group cannot turn an even index odd.

## 6. Where the count-3 must come from

Collecting Sections 4.4 and 5: neither native route forces an odd chiral 3.

- **Kinematics** (Section 4.4): purely cross-chirality Krein form, every physical subspace half left and half right, net chirality 0. No algebraic parity chiralizes the triplet without dynamics.
- **Native base index** (Section 5): even on every native channel and over the full multiplicity bundle (`4k` per adjoint, `12k`/`12k`/`24k` over the bundle), robust by reality / Kramers plus Rokhlin. The only odd-capable channel is the pseudoreal `su(2)_-` doublet.

**The cheapest odd route [standard math applied].** Gauge `su(2)_-` by a charge-`k_-` anti-self-dual instanton, leaving `su(2)_+` flat, with the native triplet riding as a flat spectator. On the `(3,2)` generation piece the net is `3 k_-`, odd, equal to exactly 3 at the minimal charge `k_- = 1`. So the cheapest route to a net 3 is a charge-1 anti-self-dual instanton, with the odd factor 3 supplied for free by the native triplet multiplicity. This is softer than a charge-3 fundamental, and is the version of the import a referee should be shown. Two qualifiers keep it from being internally forced:

1. **It imports a nonzero topological charge.** With `k_- = 0` the count is 0. Every metric `so(p,q)` connection, including the natural self-dual one carrying the geometric 3 of `Lambda^2_+`, gives generation index identically 0 (`step9_selfdual_connection_index.py` [machine-checked, point evaluation]: all `so(9,5)` generators plus the three self-dual ones preserve a particle-hole symmetry, forcing a symmetric spectrum). So `k_- = 1` is external data.
2. **It requires a sub-sector truncation.** Over the full 16-dimensional multiplicity bundle, gauging `su(2)_-` also gives `12 k_-`, even. The odd `3 = 3 k_-` appears only when one isolates the `(3,2)` generation sub-sector and discards the rest.

A mod-2 / global-anomaly check (the Witten `Z_2` index) is nontrivial only for the pseudoreal doublet, where it registers a parity, and vanishes for the adjoint; in neither case does it produce the integer 3.

**The two alternatives.** The observed chiral 3 therefore requires exactly one of:

1. an **imported topological charge** (cheapest a charge-1 anti-self-dual instanton in the doublet channel, times the native multiplicity 3, with a sub-sector truncation; external data the sector does not force); or
2. a **ghost-parity-preserving dynamics** that selects the positive-norm half of each hyperbolic pair (Section 8.3). This is not an index and lies outside the parity argument; it is the one route that could select an odd physical count from the hyperbolic pairs.

The precise location of the gap is "supply one of: a unit of flux, or a physical-half-selecting dynamics." The native 3 is already there; what is missing is the chiral projection.

### The crux, restated

The integer 3 plays two roles, never both at once. **Ungauged**, the 3 is the *dimension* of the flat self-dual triplet multiplicity space (`Lambda^2_+`): a representation dimension, a vectorlike `3 + 3bar` (Section 4.2). **Gauged** by a base instanton, that same 3 is folded into an *index* (`4k` per triplet, `12k` over the bundle), which is even (Section 5). The tension dissolves because multiplicity (a dimension) and net chiral count (an index) are different invariants. The sector fixes the multiplicity; the chiral projection that would turn vectorlike `3 + 3bar` into a net 3 is a separate datum (an index, or a ghost parity), and that datum is even or absent on the sector's native side.

### 8.1 What GU fixes, and what it does not

GU's verified Pati-Salam `Spin(2k)` chain fixes the *structure* of one generation: the `16` of `Spin(10)` read as a Standard-Model generation including a right-handed neutrino, anomaly-free, with the stated hypercharges. The results here add that GU's own self-dual 4-base geometry natively carries a *multiplicity*-3 of that `16` (Section 4), and (for family symmetries commuting with the full `Spin(10)`) that this is the canonical odd-multiplicity route. What GU's internal data does *not* fix is the *net chiral count*: a separate invariant (an index, or a ghost parity) that is even or absent on every native route and becomes 3 only with an imported unit of flux or an unbuilt dynamics (Sections 5 and 6).

Stated without advocacy: GU supplies the shape and the multiplicity of one generation; it does not internally supply the number of *chiral* generations. Whether that is read as a defect (a closed-theory expectation) or as an interface specification (an open, sourced reading) is not decided by these facts; the facts are silent between those two readings.

### 8.2 Nguyen's complexification: necessary but insufficient

Timothy Nguyen's mathematical critique identifies an unannounced complexification in GU's shiab construction. The chirality story here meets that observation from the matter side. In Lorentzian signature, keeping `Lambda^2_+` and discarding its conjugate `Lambda^2_-` is a reality-breaking `Z_2` choice, the same complexification Nguyen flags.

- **At the multiplicity level this strengthens Section 4.2.** The rank-3 self-dual Weyl bundle is native to a Lorentzian 4-base; the multiplicity-3 is not a Euclidean artifact.
- **At the count level it changes nothing [standard math applied].** As Section 5 records, topology lives in the maximal compact, an index is elliptic and Euclidean, and the real theory returns the real adjoint where the Kramers argument applies. Complexification cannot turn an even index odd.

So the complexification step Nguyen identifies is *necessary* here (without the reality-breaking `Z_2` there is no chiral split to speak of) but *insufficient* (on its own it yields an even count). The genuine import is relocated and shrunk: not the integer 3, but a `Z_2` chiral projection plus a single unit of flux. This is a statement about the mathematics; it neither vindicates nor refutes Nguyen, and it does not rescue GU.

### 8.3 Krein space and the Turok-Bateman ghost parity

The hyperbolic pairing of Section 4.4 is the structure a ghost-parity quantization is built to handle. A hyperbolic pair with null basis `{u, v}` and `<u,v> ≠ 0` has one positive-norm combination `u + v` and one negative-norm `u - v`; a `Z_2` ghost parity swapping `u` and `v` (generation and mirror) labels physical versus ghost by its even and odd eigenspaces, and a projector Born rule of the type discussed by Turok and Bateman would then return positive probabilities with the ghosts removed, provided the parity commutes with the dynamics (`[P_ghost, S] = 0`).

**Status [speculation, with an exact kinematic core].** The kinematic facts (the Krein structure, the hyperbolic pairing, the existence of the `Z_2` as a linear map) are machine-checked exact. Two things are not established. First, whether the `Z_2` is a symmetry of GU's dynamics cannot be checked, because GU supplies no source action or S-matrix; so any "physical generations" statement is contingent on an unbuilt dynamics realizing the ghost parity, and is identical to GU's long-standing missing source action. Second, *counting*: the positive-norm (ghost-parity-even) half of the triplet carries `96 = 3 x 2 x 16` states, that is *six* copies of the `16` per chirality (the `su(2)_+` triplet times the `su(2)_-` doublet), not three. Reducing six copies to exactly three Standard-Model generations is a further step the projection must accomplish and that is not shown here (see `canon/ghost-parity-krein-synthesis.md`, "The bare 3"). We present this as a research direction, not a completed derivation.

(The external Turok-Bateman material is an unpublished interview source, June 2026; it is not a citable paper and is cited as motivation only.)

### 8.4 A candidate inside-the-class positivity axis (Distler-Garibaldi)

Distler-Garibaldi proves no single real form of `E8` carries three chiral generations as a complex `G`-representation; every published escape leaves the single-group class. Our triplet *reproduces* that obstruction exactly: it is genuinely vectorlike, net asymmetry 0 in every signature. It does not contradict the theorem or its representation-theoretic conclusion.

What the Krein structure names is one premise the no-go never states because it is universal background: that the fermion state space is a positive-definite *Hilbert* space, so that "vectorlike `G`-rep" forces "non-chiral physics." On a *Krein* state space, chirality could instead be a property of the physical (positive-norm, ghost-parity-even) subspace, with the mirrors sitting as negative-norm ghosts rather than physical fermions. The forgetful map that recovers the Distler-Garibaldi answer is then a metric-forgetting (it deletes the indefinite metric and the ghost parity), not a category change, and it stays *inside* the single-group class.

**Status [typed candidate; favored outcome is the kill].** A representation-theory crux makes the negative outcome the favored one: for the physical subspace to be a `G`-representation the ghost parity must be `G`-equivariant, but a `G`-equivariant generation-mirror swap is precisely a reality structure, the very object certifying vectorlikeness, so its eigenspaces carry net chirality 0. The physical subspace is chiral only if an extra symmetry-breaking datum (the open dynamics, or a base Dirac index) breaks that equivariant identification. This converts the no-go's hardest stress case from "no inside-class datum exists" to "here is one inside-class metric-forgetting shadow; whether its dynamical realization reproduces three chiral generations is the open question, and the representation theory currently favors no." It does not claim Distler-Garibaldi is evaded. Its falsification test is exactly the open condition of Section 8.3 (does GU's eventual dynamics preserve the ghost parity?).

## 10. Methodology note

This result was produced by adversarial, AI-driven interrogation of the reconstruction, in which each candidate claim was attacked until it survived re-checking or was killed. The discipline is reported here as *method, not as physics evidence*. Three kills are load-bearing, because each removed a wrong argument the early drafts relied on.

1. **The 1664 non-sequitur.** An early thesis argued from `1664 = 2^7 * 13` (3-free) that the multiplicity could not be 3. This is invalid: a 3-free total dimension says nothing about a branching multiplicity. The valid result (Section 4.2) rests on the explicit decomposition, and the arithmetic is retired as a multiplicity argument.
2. **The "import the prime 3" framing.** The same drafts framed the count as requiring an imported prime factor 3. The self-dual triplet refutes this for the multiplicity dimension; the genuine import shrank to a unit of topological flux (Section 6), a different and smaller claim, and not the integer 3.
3. **The index-theory anchor corrections.** Three sub-claims of the earlier reasoning were corrected: the generation is a `(3,2)` of `su(2)_+ x su(2)_-` (not "triplet, not doublet"); the cheapest odd-3 import is a charge-1 anti-self-dual instanton (not a charge-3 fundamental); and even-ness is a property of the real / adjoint channel and the full bundle specifically (robust by reality / Kramers), not of the base topology as an undifferentiated whole.

The surviving structure is mostly representation theory and index theory that needs no part of GU. Process discipline is offered as a way to find such structure, never as evidence for any physical claim, and the index-theory layer of Section 5 still wants one human-expert check.
