# Tri-Repo Division of Labor — GU Formalization's Leg

Joe ratified (chat, 2026-07-02) a standing division of labor across the three
sibling research repositories. This document records it GU-locally so the main
research line is legible as one leg of a joint effort. It changes no claim
grade, verdict, or canon status.

## The joint effort (stated with guards intact)

The three repositories independently drove three different questions to the
same structural residue:

> A bounded system, complete and self-consistent in itself, provably cannot
> supply one specific thing from inside — and the missing thing lives at its
> boundary. The honest ledger for that boundary is what the region can do,
> not what it can see.

- **gu-formalization:** the interior matter geometry is vectorlike and cannot
  force its own chirality / generation count; the count enters as external
  boundary data (the "located, not forced" floor; the Firewall-Boundary
  Hypothesis as primary question).
- **time-as-finality:** record/order facts are capability facts, not
  statistical facts; the arrow is capability loss across an access boundary.
- **temporal-issuance:** an interior cannot precontain its own genuine
  extension; if novelty enters, it enters across the interface.

**The identity claim ("one object, three shadows") is explicitly NOT made.**
Per `explorations/time-as-finality-crosswalk/capability-boundary-interface-signature-2026-07-02.md`,
it is gated behind adapter contracts: no identity claim before at least two
adapters prove out. The convergence is currently: same shape, three
independent arrivals, identity untested.

## The division

| repo | owns | leg |
|---|---|---|
| gu-formalization | **boundary content** — what the boundary must supply, in exact mathematics (anomaly classes, charges, discrete structure); the firewall-boundary attack | physics |
| time-as-finality | **the capability measure** — defining and testing the bounded-region capability object C(R); certificates; the region-indexed capability discriminator | measurement |
| temporal-issuance | **the source question** — whether anything genuinely crosses (issuance vs disclosure of a fixed whole); effect-typing; the D-FORK | source |

One sentence each: GU says what the boundary must deliver; TaF says what a
boundary is measured in; TI says whether anything truly crosses it.

## Verification tiers (shared cross-repo vocabulary, ratified by Joe 2026-07-02)

Each repo's native grade vocabulary remains authoritative for its own claims.
This three-tier overlay is for cross-repo communication and public writeups,
so "internally verified" never masquerades as "established":

1. **recorded** — result exists with runnable artifacts; not yet survived
   adversarial review.
2. **internally established** — survived the repo's own hostile review, with
   numbers independently re-derived from scratch and everything reproducible
   from the tree. (Kernel-checked Lean proofs are a strong sub-case: the
   proof is machine-verified by independent software; the modeling
   faithfulness remains internal.) Maps to the standard reproduced-vs-
   replicated distinction: reproduced, not yet replicated.
3. **externally established** — verified outside the process: independent
   replication, peer review, or named-specialist sign-off (the K9 gate).

Single-process ceiling: because internal reviewers are spawned by the same
process that produced the result, no internal step — however adversarial —
can promote a result to tier 3.

## Guards (standing, repo-local)

Unchanged from the crosswalk note; repeated here because this document is the
one most likely to be read as license to relax them:

- No identity claim before at least two adapter contracts prove out.
- GU is not evidence for TaF/TI capability language; capability language is
  not used to repair GU's source-action or generation-count gaps.
- No "count = capability lattice" identification.
- Cross-repo material enters only as clearly-labeled crosswalk exploration,
  never as support for a GU claim grade.
- Single-process caution: all three repos are maintained by the same research
  process; convergence among them is weaker evidence than convergence from
  independent processes, and the adapter tests are the only cure.
