## Interpretation

The logged loop completed its item queue in both recorded sessions (`19/19` total items) and recorded
`19` fixes against `24` total issues. That is real repair throughput, but it is not a convergence proof.

The strongest caution signal is pattern recurrence. Two of four detected failure-pattern categories recur
across both sessions:

- `same_session_self_resolution`
- `verdict_grade_inflation`

The resulting repeat-rate is `0.50` over the categories the current log can detect. That means the loop was
fixing problems while still reproducing two central failure modes: resolving flags too soon within the same
session, and assigning stronger verdict grades than the evidence supports.

## Instrumentation Limits

Three metrics requested by the convergence lane are not recoverable from the current tracked log without
inventing structure:

- `net_resolved_gain`
- `admitted_object_fraction`
- `status_transition_churn`

The log records item completion, issue counts, fixes, and recurring failure patterns. It does not encode a
stable per-item state transition table, admitted-object classification, or reopen/downgrade/upgrade ledger.
Future convergence reads should add those fields before claiming net convergence or thrash reduction.
