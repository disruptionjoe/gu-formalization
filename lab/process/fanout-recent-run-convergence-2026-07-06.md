all with matching commits, adding 20 versioned paths and modifying 11. The create/rework ratio is `1.818`,
so this window was still producing new packets, tests, and active-research objects rather than only revising
old files.

The warning sign is concentration. Seven of the 11 packets were in the same source-action attack-gate family,
and two more were Lean maintenance. The repeated-lane run share is `0.818`. That is not evidence of pure
thrash, because the source-action packets added distinct carrier tests, but it is evidence that undirected
fan-out was heavily exploiting one family of work.

The two immediately prior lanes, signed-readout UII and Sp(1) 2-primary anomaly, are intentionally singletons
in this snapshot. Future fan-out should not repeat them without new local evidence, and should treat additional
source-action carrier packets as lower priority unless they expose a genuinely new missing object rather than
another adjacent carrier route.
