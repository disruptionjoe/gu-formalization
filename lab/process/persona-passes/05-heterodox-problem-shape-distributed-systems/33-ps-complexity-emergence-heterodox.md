# Persona Pass 33 — Complexity Science / Emergence / Self-Organized Criticality (Heterodox)

**Date:** 2026-05-28
**Persona:** Santa Fe Institute complexity theorist. Lineage: Per Bak (SOC), Stuart Kauffman (NK landscapes, adjacent possible), Anderson ("More is Different"), Maturana-Varela (autopoiesis), Holland (emergence), Friston (free-energy principle), Wilson (renormalization group), Beggs-Plenz (criticality hypothesis in neuroscience).
**Heterodox lean.** Hegelian dialectic within complexity / emergence.
**Accepted baseline:** 00, 00b, 00c, 00d.

## (a) Thesis (mainstream physics)

Reductionism. Microphysics is fundamental; macroscopic patterns are bookkeeping summaries derivable in principle from the microscopic Hamiltonian. Chirality is a representation-theoretic feature of the bundle level; the no-gos (Witten 1981, Nielsen-Ninomiya, Freed-Hopkins, Distler-Garibaldi) are theorems about the microscopic-data-to-macroscopic-invariant map and they close the case.

## (b) Antithesis (heterodox, complexity / emergence)

Anderson 1972: "More is Different." New laws appear at each emergence scale by symmetry breaking; they are not derivable from finer-scale rules even in principle. Bak's SOC: extended dissipative systems self-tune to critical points without parameter tuning, generating power-law correlations whose **critical exponents are universal (depend on dimension and conservation laws, not on microscopic details)**. Kauffman NK landscapes: macroscopic structure (peak distribution, walk lengths) is parameterized by N and K but not derivable from any single instance; the adjacent possible expands as the system evolves, so the phase space is not pre-given. Maturana-Varela: living/autopoietic systems define their own boundaries; observer and substrate co-arise through operational closure. Friston: any system maintaining a Markov blanket minimizes free energy via active inference; observer and environment are not separable. Wilson RG: macroscopic physics is the IR fixed point of an RG flow whose **universality classes are insensitive to UV microscopic details** — the macroscale invariants are genuinely emergent, not microscale summaries. Beggs-Plenz: cortex sits at criticality where computation is optimized; 1/f noise is a universal signature across substrates regardless of microscopic constitution.

The shared heterodox claim: **macroscale invariants are not derivable from microscale rules; they are properties of universality classes / RG fixed points / SOC attractors / autopoietic closures**. The bundle level the no-gos compute is the lossy reductionist shadow of an emergent invariant living at a scale the microphysics does not contain. [speculation]

## (c) Aufhebung — problem shape adding to 00d

00d's substrate is a class of mathematical objects (subfactor, motivic cohomology, multiway hypergraph, QEC code, pre-geometric object). The complexity heterodox refinement asks: **what type of object is "substrate" supposed to be? A specific mathematical structure, or a universality class / RG fixed point / SOC attractor?**

If the substrate is a universality class rather than a specific structure, then **chirality is a critical exponent of an SOC / RG-fixed-point class** whose value is universal across all microscopic realizations in that class but whose forgetful image at the smooth bundle level is the null Witten/Freed-Hopkins datum. The no-gos compute the IR shadow of an RG-relevant operator that the smooth-bundle EFT integrates out. The substrate is not "underneath" the bundle in a static-object sense; it is the **attractor / fixed point / universality class** the bundle's RG flow runs toward (or away from). [speculation]

This extends 00d's three-leg specification by adding a fourth axis: **emergence-scale axis** (the scale at which the invariant lives) and the question of whether the substrate is **specifiable as an object** or only **specifiable as a universality class** (the class itself has no canonical representative).

## (d) WRK-326 next ask

Add to 00d's triple a fourth leg: **emergence-class axis** (specific-object substrate vs. universality-class substrate vs. SOC-attractor substrate vs. autopoietic-closure substrate). Specifying as a universality class is mathematically weaker than specifying as an object and may be the right granularity given that the no-gos are object-level theorems.

## (e) Falsifiable sub-question

For a candidate SOC / RG-fixed-point substrate class, does the class's critical-exponent spectrum contain a parity-breaking exponent whose RG-relevant operator's forgetful image at the smooth-bundle IR fixed point is the null Witten/Freed-Hopkins datum AND whose substrate-level value matches SM chirality content? Concretely: is there a known universality class with a chirality-class critical exponent matching SM generation/chirality content, and does its IR-EFT reduction map to the Witten-null bundle-level invariant?
