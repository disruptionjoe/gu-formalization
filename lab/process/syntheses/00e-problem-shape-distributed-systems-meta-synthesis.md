## The meta-dialectic deepened

### 00d's three-leg ask is the right shape but the wrong dimensionality

00d asked: "What is the substrate-level invariant whose forgetful image in the smooth bundle shadow is the chirality content the no-go theorems compute as null? Specify (substrate, observer, pairing) and test."

P11-P15 show this is structurally right but understated. The forgetful operation depends on at least six axes, not three. The triple-spec is a starting point; the actual specification needed is a sextuple.

### The convergent reframe of the no-go theorems

Every no-go theorem (Witten 1981, Nielsen-Ninomiya, Freed-Hopkins, Distler-Garibaldi) has implicit class assumptions that are visible only when you look from outside the smooth-bundle frame:

- **Witten 1981** assumes: smooth manifold, Levi-Civita connection, deterministic KK reduction, total-order time, specific-object substrate, smooth substrate-observer separation, smooth-CA / strong-consistency consensus class.
- **Nielsen-Ninomiya** assumes: locality, reversibility, translation-invariance, smoothness in lattice gauge field, deterministic CA, synchronous consensus class.
- **Freed-Hopkins** assumes: cobordism / invertible-field-theory anomaly framework, specific-object substrate, deterministic / synchronous protocol class, total-order causality.
- **Distler-Garibaldi** assumes: smooth Lie group embedding, ordinary cohomology, specific-object substrate.

Each no-go has class assumptions on multiple axes that can be dropped independently. The dialectic is: WHICH axis-droppings preserve the substrate-level invariant while making the bundle-shadow no-go null?

That is the load-bearing question for problem-shaping/2 of WRK-326.
