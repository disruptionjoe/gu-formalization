# Open-System Bistability of Total Unitarity in Keep-and-Grade Higher-Derivative Field Theory

**Status: DRAFT (papers/drafts/). Not a candidate, not public. Candidate promotion and any external
action are Joe-gated.**

## Thesis (one sentence)

For a higher-derivative (Stelle-class) / PT-symmetric / keep-and-grade Krein-quantized field theory
coupled to an external source, loop-level TOTAL unitarity is not a closed-theory verdict but a bistable
open-system fixed-point problem: an operative (positive-total-metric, total-unitary) fixed point exists,
is stable, and is non-vacuous, but a pathological fixed point is equally self-consistent, so self-
consistency does not select; selection reduces to the reservoir's Krein signature versus an O(1) coupling
ratio.

## What is proven versus what is conditional

- **Proven (exact / theorem-grade in a finite model):** the physical-subspace unitarity defect
  A^dag A = P+ + B^dag B with a definite sign; the separation of the reduced-resolvent physical-sheet pole
  from the total-generator spectrum (a like-signed regime has a real total plus a positive total metric
  while the reduced pole sits on the physical sheet); the opposite-signed Krein exceptional-point
  obstruction; the non-vacuity of the operative fixed point (a fixed opposite-sign kinematic coupling
  genuinely spoils it below a threshold).
- **Conditional / toy / plausible:** the bistable fixed point and its selection live in a finite
  Friedrichs / Fano-Anderson stand-in; basin selection depends on an unbuilt O(1) coupling ratio; the QFT
  lift is structural; the fade dynamical selection is plausible-only (ported, with a finite-N non-unitary
  window).
