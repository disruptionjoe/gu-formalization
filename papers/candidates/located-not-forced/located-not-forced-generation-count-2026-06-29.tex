\begin{abstract}
We study the chirality and generation structure of an explicit real Clifford Rarita--Schwinger sector: the
gamma-traceless rank-$3/2$ field of a real Clifford module $\Cl(p,q)$ with $p+q=14$ on a $128$-dimensional
spinor, with one generation identified with the $\mathbf{16}$ of $\Spin(10)$. The sector is \emph{motivated by},
and reconstructed from, the matter proposal of Geometric Unity (GU); every GU-specific step is marked, and none
of the theorem-grade results depends on GU being correct.

We prove a \emph{two-primary meta-theorem} (no \emph{enumerated} obstruction to a net chiral count in this sector
is an odd-prime congruence) and an \emph{index-conservation theorem} (every linear Krein-isometric operator on the generation
triplet conserves the net chiral index at zero, because the purely cross-chirality $(96,96)$ form makes every
physical subspace chirality-balanced---a finite-dimensional fact, no Fredholm theory). The decomposition $\pis = \Z/24 = \Z/8 \oplus \Z/3$ is the central structural fact: by the Chinese
Remainder Theorem the summands are disjoint, every obstruction and chiralizing selector lives in the $2$-primary
$\Z/8$ summand, and a homotopy-theoretic count, if it exists, lives only in the odd-torsion $\Z/3$ summand. We
exhibit a genuine order-$3$ object in that summand---the framed-bordism Adams $e$-invariant $\eR=1/12$ carried by
the self-dual $\Lamp$ tangential framing on the $\RP^3$ spine of the metric fiber---and show by computation that
it is tangential but vectorlike and homotopy-fixed: it \emph{locates} the order-$3$ slot without filling it.

We then test the strongest possible reading by direct computation. Reopened with no GU restriction, the
obstruction is a \emph{class-level structural no-go} (computed and adversarially verified): no covariant operator
interior to a Clifford--Rarita--Schwinger sector of this type was found to force an odd chiral count. Its linear
leg is theorem-grade (index conservation); its antilinear leg---that no frame-non-trivial antilinear
(Altland--Zirnbauer class-CII type) chiralizer exists---is established by a finite adversarial hunt, not a closed
proof (an exhaustive non-existence theorem is open). On present evidence the generation count is therefore
\emph{external}: supplied by a net-self-dual chiral background coupled through the index theorem, of the same
general kind by which chirality arises in known chiral theories (chiral gauge couplings, instanton zero-modes,
$K3$/Calabi--Yau compactification).

We do \emph{not} claim three generations. The ``located, not forced'' reading is contingent on treating the
obstruction data torsion-theoretically; under a literal integer-valued chiral-index reading the same
obstructions would instead \emph{forbid} an odd count outright. We claim a no-go that \emph{locates}, and that
relocates the count to external background data; the identification $\text{order-}3\text{ class}\to\text{integer
}3$ is posed as the single named open conjecture (a candidate category error, made algebraically explicit by
$\Hom(\Z/3,\Z)=0$). External review of both the core results and the open bridge is requested.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

The Standard Model contains three generations of fermions and does not explain the number. A recurring hope in
unification is that the number is fixed by topology. The obstacle, made precise here, is that the natural index-
and anomaly-theoretic invariants one computes \emph{in this sector} are predominantly \textbf{$2$-primary}:
mod-$2$ Witten anomalies, mod-$16$ topological-superconductor classifications, even-valued Dirac indices. A
$2$-primary invariant cannot, by arithmetic, force an odd count. (The one place the literature \emph{does} obtain
an odd constraint---Garc\'ia-Etxebarria--Montero's $\Z_9 \to N_{\mathrm{gen}}\in 3\Z$---is itself a genuinely
$3$-primary anomaly, consistent with the thesis that an odd count requires $3$-primary input.)

This paper turns that obstacle into a positive structural statement, in one explicit sector, and then tests the
strongest possible reading of it by direct computation. Two distinctions are load-bearing and we keep them
explicit throughout. First, \emph{motivated} versus \emph{independent}: the sector is motivated by GU, but the
theorem-grade results (the two-primary meta-theorem, the index-conservation theorem, the CRT two-arena structure,
the $\Hom(\Z/3,\Z)=0$ sharpening) hold for the Clifford--Rarita--Schwinger sector as a piece of mathematics,
whether or not GU is correct. Second, \emph{located} versus \emph{forced}: we show precisely \emph{where} a
generation count could live and that the geometry cannot, from inside, \emph{force} one; we do not derive the
number three. This no-force reading is contingent on treating the obstruction data \emph{torsion-theoretically}:
under a literal integer-valued chiral-index reading the same obstructions would instead \emph{forbid} an odd
count outright. We state this load-bearing premise as Corollary~\ref{cor:arenas}.

Our contributions:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
\item \textbf{A two-primary meta-theorem (Section~\ref{sec:thm1}).} No obstruction in the enumerated list of
Theorem~\ref{thm:1} is an odd-prime congruence; in particular none imposes a mod-$3$ condition. [theorem
(scoped to the enumeration), theory-independent]
\item \textbf{A CRT two-arena reading (Section~\ref{sec:crt}).} The decomposition
$\pis = \Z/24 = \Z/8 \oplus \Z/3$ is standard primary decomposition, already used in the prior family-puzzle
literature; we claim no novelty for it. The contribution is the structural \emph{reading}: every obstruction
\emph{and} every chiralizing selector lives in $\Z/8$, a homotopy-theoretic count lives only in $\Z/3$, the two
arenas cannot interact directly within the group structure (anomaly inflow being the natural bridge between them), and hence a $2$-primary no-go
cannot force an odd count. [interpretive structural reading on standard input, theory-independent, under the
torsion-count reading; see Corollary~\ref{cor:arenas}]
\item \textbf{An index-conservation theorem, a necessary antilinear escape, and a class-level structural no-go
(Section~\ref{sec:thm2}).} Every linear Krein-isometric operator conserves the net chiral index at zero (proven
finite-dimensionally: every maximal $K$-positive subspace is chirality-balanced); the unique
symmetry-respecting escape is antilinear. Reopened GU-independently:
no covariant operator found---linear or antilinear---interior to a Clifford--Rarita--Schwinger sector of this
class forces an odd chiral count. The chiralizer's frame-triviality is evadable only into a $2$-primary,
index-conserving channel, and no frame-non-trivial antilinear loophole survived an adversarial hunt (an
exhaustive non-existence proof is open). On present evidence the generation count is therefore \emph{external}.
[computed structural no-go, theory-independent; linear leg theorem-grade, antilinear non-existence a finite hunt]
\item \textbf{A located order-$3$ carrier (Section~\ref{sec:carrier}).} The self-dual $\Lamp$ tangential framing
on the $\RP^3$ spine carries $\eR=1/12$, a genuine order-$3$ class in $\Z/3$, shown by computation to be
tangential but vectorlike and homotopy-fixed: it locates, it does not fill.
\item \textbf{The single decider and an honest gate (Section~\ref{sec:decider}).} The integer count on GU's
actual $14$-manifold is gated on the unbuilt stabilized twisted Rarita--Schwinger source action; the only honest
computable integer is one. We do not force three.
\item \textbf{The open conjecture (Section~\ref{sec:open}).} $\text{order-}3\text{ class}\to\text{integer }3$ is,
in the present literature, a theorem of nothing and a candidate category error, made precise by
$\Hom(\Z/3,\Z)=0$. We state it as the single open bridge and request review.
\end{enumerate}

The robust, theory-independent content is items 1--3 together with the located carrier of item 4. The GU
identification and the generation-count reading are clearly marked reconstruction-grade or open.

\section{The setting}\label{sec:setting}

Let $S$ be the real spinor module of $\Cl(p,q)$, $p+q=14$, $\dim_\R S = 128$. The Rarita--Schwinger field is the
gamma-traceless part of $V\otimes S$; the constraint cuts $V\otimes S$ (dimension $14\cdot128=1792$) to
$\ker(\Gamma)$, of dimension $(14-1)\cdot128 = 1664 = 2^7\cdot 13$. We split $14 = 4+10$: a four-dimensional base
$X^4$ and a ten-dimensional internal space, with one generation the $\mathbf{16}$ of $\Spin(10)$. The base frame
group is $\Spin(4)=\SU(2)_+\times\SU(2)_-$; the self-dual two-forms $\Lamp$ generate $\mathfrak{su}(2)_+$. Under
$\mathfrak{su}(2)_+$,
\[
\ker(\Gamma) = 640~(\text{singlets}) + 832~(\text{doublets}) + 192~(\text{triplets}),
\]
verified numerically. The $192$-dimensional $j=1$ sector is the native multiplicity-three structure: a genuine
triplet carrying the $\mathbf{16}$. The invariant bilinear is the $\mathfrak{so}(p,q)$-invariant Krein form
$K = \eta_V\otimes\beta_S$; on the triplet it is purely cross-chirality, signature exactly $(+96,-96)$, so the
matter content is \emph{vectorlike} with net chirality $0$. This is the central tension: the sector is
multiplicity-three but vectorlike, and no internal operation chiralizes it.

\section{Multiplicity versus chirality}\label{sec:multiplicity}

The integer $3$ plays two arithmetically different roles in this sector, and conflating them is the error the
``located, not forced'' reading most guards against. A \emph{multiplicity} is the dimension of a flat family
space---the number of copies of a fixed representation the constrained module carries; it is a representation
dimension, comes with its mirror (copies and anti-copies), and is vectorlike. A \emph{net chiral count} is an
index---the signed difference (left minus right) of Dirac zero modes; it measures chirality, the property a
vectorlike spectrum lacks. A representation dimension and an operator index are not one number computed two ways.
This sector supplies the first natively and does not supply the second.

\paragraph{The multiplicity is natively three (verified).} Under the self-dual $\mathfrak{su}(2)_+$ of the
$4$-base, the gamma-traceless module decomposes as in Section~\ref{sec:setting}. Written by \emph{multiplicity}
rather than dimension,
\[
\ker(\Gamma) = 640~(j{=}0) + 416~(j{=}\tfrac12) + 64~(j{=}1),
\qquad 640\cdot 1 + 416\cdot 2 + 64\cdot 3 = 1664,
\]
i.e.\ $640$ singlets, $416$ doublets and $64$ triplets, the $64$ triplets spanning the $192$-dimensional $j=1$
sector of Section~\ref{sec:setting}. On all $192$ states the $\Spin(10)$ Casimir matches the reference
$\mathbf{16}/\overline{\mathbf{16}}$ value to machine precision
(\texttt{tests/generation-sector/h1\_selfdual\_family\_kill.py}). A native multiplicity-three object exists,
carried by the self-dual geometry of any oriented $4$-base; the multiplicity dimension $3$ is not imported.

\begin{lemma}[spinor $2$-smoothness]\label{lem:2smooth}
If a family symmetry $\SO(m)$ acts so that the multiplicity space is a sum of spinor representations of $\SO(m)$,
its dimension is a power of two, hence not divisible by $3$. An odd multiplicity can therefore arise only from a
non-spinor family representation---a vector, an adjoint, or a self-dual tensor.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The Dirac spinor of $\SO(m)$ has dimension $2^{\lfloor m/2\rfloor}$ and each half-spinor of $\SO(2k)$ has
dimension $2^{k-1}$; all are powers of two, never divisible by the odd prime $3$. The vector ($m$), the adjoint
($m(m-1)/2$) and self-dual tensors are not so constrained; $\Lamp$ of a $4$-manifold has dimension $3$.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Three is the canonical odd multiplicity (verified for the commutant of the full $\Spin(10)$).}
Lemma~\ref{lem:2smooth} turns an open-ended search into a dichotomy: the spinor channels are provably $3$-free,
so any odd multiplicity must enter through a non-spinor channel. Inside $\Spin(14)\supset\Spin(10)\times\Spin(4)$
the commutant of $\Spin(10)$ is $\mathfrak{so}(4)=\mathfrak{su}(2)_+\oplus\mathfrak{su}(2)_-$, which up to
conjugacy has exactly three $\mathfrak{su}(2)$ subalgebras (self-dual, anti-self-dual, diagonal), and no
$\mathfrak{su}(3)$ embeds (by dimension, $\dim\mathfrak{su}(3)=8>6=\dim\mathfrak{so}(4)$). Enumerating the
$16$-dimensional multiplicity space under each
(\texttt{tests/generation-sector/leg3\_family\_embedding\_enumeration.py}), only the self-dual and anti-self-dual
$\mathfrak{su}(2)$ (orientation images of one another) give an odd multiplicity, always the triplet of dimension
$3$, never a quintet or higher; the diagonal choice gives an all-even content. So for family symmetries commuting
with the full $\Spin(10)$ the self-dual route is canonical and the multiplicity it fixes is three. \emph{This is a
statement about a representation dimension, not a net chiral count.} The case of a family symmetry commuting only
with the Standard-Model subgroup $G_{\mathrm{SM}}\subset\Spin(10)$---whose larger commutant could in principle
host a horizontal $\mathfrak{su}(3)$---is not covered here and is a bounded open computation; Lemma~\ref{lem:2smooth}
already constrains it.

\paragraph{But the triplet is vectorlike, so the native three is not a chiral three.} The native multiplicity
three is vectorlike: as in Section~\ref{sec:setting}, the invariant Krein form $K=\eta_V\otimes\beta_S$ on the
triplet is purely cross-chirality with signature $(+96,-96)$, so every physical subspace is chirality-balanced
and the net chiral index is $0$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:2}). The native object is three copies plus three mirrors;
turning it into a \emph{net} three is a separate datum---an index, or a chiral projection---that the internal
algebra does not fix, that Section~\ref{sec:thm1} shows is even on every enumerated obstruction, and that
Section~\ref{sec:thm2} shows no interior operator supplies. \textbf{The multiplicity three is native; the chiral
three is not.} This is the representation-theoretic form of ``located, not forced'': the sector locates a genuine
three (as a multiplicity) and does not force a three (as a chirality).

\section{Theorem 1: the no-go is two-primary}\label{sec:thm1}

Call an integer or torsion invariant \textbf{$2$-primary} if it is a power of two, a multiple of one, or a
statement modulo a power of two.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:1}
Among the obstructions to a net chiral generation count enumerated for this sector (items~(1)--(7) in the proof
below), none is an odd-prime congruence: every enumerated obstruction is even or a statement modulo a power of
two, and in particular none imposes a mod-$3$ condition. The claim is scoped to this enumerated list; it is not an
impossibility statement over all conceivable obstructions.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof (enumeration)]
We enumerate the obstructions and exhibit the modulus of each.
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\arabic*),leftmargin=2.2em]
\item \emph{Kramers / quaternionic wall.} The quaternionic structure $J^2=-1$ is a $\Z/2$ statement (Kramers
degeneracy pairs states two at a time).
\item \emph{Real / pseudoreal non-chirality.} The relevant Witten index is the mod-$2$ index, $\Z/2$-valued; a
real or pseudoreal representation cannot carry a net chirality detectable beyond mod $2$.
\item \emph{Cross-chirality Krein signature.} The $(+96,-96)$ split of the invariant form forces net chirality
$0$ (an even, in fact balanced, statement).
\item \emph{Adjoint Dirac index.} The adjoint index $2\,T(\mathrm{adj})\,k = 4k$ is divisible by $4$; over the
full multiplicity bundle it is $12k$ or $24k$, in all cases even.
\item \emph{Rokhlin}~\cite{rokhlin1952}. For a spin $4$-manifold, $\mathrm{sign}(X)\equiv 0 \pmod{16}$, a
mod-$2^4$ statement.
\item \emph{Spinor $2$-smoothness.} A spinor of $\SO(m)$ has dimension $2^{\lfloor m/2\rfloor}$, a power of two;
only a non-spinor (vector, adjoint, or self-dual) family representation can be odd-dimensional.
\item \emph{Ghost parity.} A ghost-parity resolution of the Krein hyperbolic pairs yields a $50/50$ (net $0$)
physical sector.
\end{enumerate}
Every item is even or a statement modulo a power of two; no odd-prime congruence appears anywhere in the
enumeration.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The integer $3$ does appear in the sector---as a multiplicand ($96 = 2^5\cdot 3$, $12k$, $24k$,
$|\mathrm{Weyl}(D_7)|$). Theorem~\ref{thm:1} is not ``no factor of $3$ occurs.'' It is the sharper statement that
no obstruction is an odd-prime \emph{congruence}; the loose ``even'' reading is near-vacuous, and the content is
the modular statement. A standard literature analogue: the $3$-primary part of the stable $3$-stem is ``lost in
the mod-$2$ Postnikov system.''
\end{remark}

\section{The CRT two-arena structure}\label{sec:crt}

We have $\pis = \Z/24$, with primary decomposition $\Z/24 = \Z/8 \oplus \Z/3$. Adams' image-of-$J$ theorem~\cite{adams1966jx4}
gives, in stem $4s-1$, that $\Imm J$ is cyclic of order $\mathrm{denom}(B_{2s}/4s)$; for $s=1$ this is $24$, so
$\Imm J_3 = \Z/24$ and the Adams $e$-invariant detects it.

\paragraph{The central structural fact.} By the Chinese Remainder Theorem the splitting $\Z/24 = \Z/8\oplus\Z/3$
is into disjoint, non-interacting summands: there is no nonzero homomorphism $\Z/8\to\Z/3$ or $\Z/3\to\Z/8$. By
Theorem~\ref{thm:1}, every obstruction in the no-go constrains only the $\Z/8$ ($2$-primary) summand. Section
\ref{sec:thm2} shows every chiralizing \emph{selector} likewise lives in $\Z/8$. A homotopy-theoretic generation
count, being odd, can live only in the $\Z/3$ summand.

\begin{corollary}\label{cor:arenas}
\emph{Under the torsion-count reading}---that a generation count, if it exists, lives as a $3$-primary boundary
$e$-invariant in the $\Z/3$ summand---the question ``does any obstruction or selector force the count?'' is
\textbf{arithmetically ill-posed}: obstruction/selector and count inhabit CRT-disjoint summands, so the answer is
no before any geometric input, and the no-go's blindness to the count is a structural feature, not a weakness.
\end{corollary}

This premise is load-bearing and reconstruction-grade: under a literal \emph{integer-index} reading the same
obstructions would instead \emph{forbid} an odd count outright (net index $0$ forces count $0$), so ``located,
not forced'' presupposes the torsion-count reading rather than the integer-index one. The only object that can
bridge the two arenas is anomaly inflow (Callan--Harvey~\cite{callan1985anomalies} /
Dai--Freed~\cite{dai1994eta}), and Section~\ref{sec:decider} shows that
even the inflow coefficient does not, on present computation, force three.

We refer to $\Z/8$ as the \emph{selector arena} and $\Z/3$ as the \emph{carrier arena}: the chiralizer lives in
the selector arena, the count-carrier in the carrier arena, and they do not meet.

\section{Theorem 2: index conservation, the antilinear escape, and the structural no-go}\label{sec:thm2}

\paragraph{Setup (finite-dimensional).} Let $W$ be the $192$-dimensional complex generation carrier, equipped
with a nondegenerate Hermitian Krein form $K$ of signature $(96,96)$ and a chirality grading
$\Gamma\colon W\to W$, $\Gamma^2=I$, with $\pm1$-eigenspaces $W_+,W_-$ of dimension $96$ each and grading
projections $\pi_\pm=\tfrac12(I\pm\Gamma)$. The form is \emph{purely cross-chirality} (``Krein signature exactly
$(+96,-96)$,'' vectorlike; verified in \texttt{tests/generation-sector/ghost\_parity\_krein.py}): the eigenspaces
$W_+$ and $W_-$ are each $K$-isotropic (Lagrangian), $K|_{W_+\times W_+}=K|_{W_-\times W_-}=0$, and $K$ restricts
to a perfect pairing $W_+\times W_-\to\mathbb{C}$. A \emph{physical subspace} is a maximal $K$-positive-definite
subspace $P\subseteq W$ (i.e.\ $K(v,v)>0$ for all $v\in P\setminus\{0\}$); by Sylvester's law every such $P$ has
$\dim P=96$, the positive index of inertia.

\begin{definition}[net chiral index]\label{def:chi}
For a physical subspace $P$, set $\chi(P):=\dim\pi_+(P)-\dim\pi_-(P)\in\Z$.
\end{definition}

\noindent This is the net handedness of the physical sector: it counts left-minus-right chiral content of the
$96$ physical states. It is a difference of dimensions of finite-dimensional subspaces---a manifestly
integer-valued invariant, defined with no Hilbert completion, no Fredholm operator, and no spectral flow. (As a
sanity check that $\chi$ genuinely \emph{detects} chirality and is not zero by construction: take instead a
grading-\emph{aligned} form $K=\mathrm{diag}(I_{96},-I_{96})$---of the same total signature $(96,96)$, but with
$\Gamma$ now \emph{commuting} with $K$ rather than anticommuting, so $W_+$ is $K$-positive-definite and $W_-$
$K$-negative-definite. Then the canonical physical subspace $P=W_+$ gives $\chi(W_+)=+96$, a maximally chiral
configuration. Thus $\chi$ is genuinely sensitive to the relation between $\Gamma$ and $K$; it is forced to $0$
below precisely because the form is purely cross-chirality.)

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:2}
On the $(96,96)$ cross-chirality carrier, every physical subspace $P$ satisfies $\chi(P)=0$. Consequently every
linear Krein-isometric operator $U$ (i.e.\ $K(Ux,Uy)=K(x,y)$) maps physical subspaces to physical subspaces and
conserves the net chiral index at zero: $\chi(UP)=\chi(P)=0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Transversality.} Each eigenspace $W_\pm$ is $K$-isotropic, so every vector of $W_\pm$ is $K$-null;
$P$ is $K$-positive-definite, so its only $K$-null vector is $0$. Hence $P\cap W_+=P\cap W_-=\{0\}$.

\emph{Balance.} Since $\ker(\pi_+|_P)=P\cap W_-=\{0\}$, the restriction $\pi_+|_P\colon P\to W_+$ is injective
between spaces of equal dimension $96$, hence an isomorphism; likewise $\pi_-|_P\colon P\to W_-$. Therefore
$\dim\pi_+(P)=\dim\pi_-(P)=96$ and $\chi(P)=0$. Equivalently, $P$ is the graph of the linear isomorphism
$T:=(\pi_-|_P)\circ(\pi_+|_P)^{-1}\colon W_+\xrightarrow{\ \sim\ }W_-$, i.e.\ it is chirality-balanced.

\emph{Conservation.} A linear $K$-isometry $U$ preserves $K(v,v)$, hence carries $K$-positive-definite subspaces
to $K$-positive-definite subspaces and preserves dimension, so $UP$ is again a physical subspace. By the first
part $\chi(UP)=0=\chi(P)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[no Fredholm theory is needed]
The invariant of Definition~\ref{def:chi} is a difference of dimensions of subspaces of a $192$-dimensional
space, and the theorem proves it equals $0$ \emph{pointwise} for every physical subspace by elementary linear
algebra (transversality of a positive-definite subspace to a pair of complementary Lagrangians). No Hilbert
space, Fredholm operator, analytic index, or spectral flow enters. The objection that index conservation ``looks
like a category error requiring a Fredholm/spectral-flow setup'' applies only to an \emph{infinite-dimensional}
analytic index, where invariance is a continuity/homotopy statement; here the carrier is finite-dimensional and
$\chi$ is exactly, not asymptotically, an integer, so the objection does not arise. The connectedness of the
isometry group plays no role in the argument: $\chi$ is identically $0$, not merely constant.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[connectedness, demoted to corroboration]
The earlier one-line argument---``$U(96,96)$ is connected, so the integer $\chi$ is constant''---is corroborative
only and is not load-bearing. (It is also better phrased on the space of physical subspaces than on the group:
the maximal $K$-positive subspaces form the connected, indeed contractible, bounded symmetric domain
$U(96,96)/\!\bigl(U(96)\times U(96)\bigr)$, on which the integer-valued $\chi$ is constant.) We keep it only as a
consistency check; the proof above establishes $\chi\equiv0$ directly.
\end{remark}

\noindent\emph{Machine-corroborated} (\texttt{tests/generation-sector/net\_chiral\_index\_invariant.py}, building
on \texttt{ghost\_parity\_krein.py} and \texttt{t1a\_kinematic\_chirality\_kill.py}): on the abstract $(96,96)$
cross-chirality model, $\Gamma$ is $K$-anti-self-adjoint ($\|\Gamma K+K\Gamma\|=0$), a maximal $K$-positive
subspace has $\dim\pi_+(P)=\dim\pi_-(P)=96$ so $\chi(P)=0$, and a random linear Krein isometry
($\|U^{\dagger}KU-K\|/\|K\|\approx5\times10^{-16}$) preserves $\chi(UP)=0$; the grading-aligned control gives
$\chi=+96$. On the real GU triplet the cross-chirality hypothesis ($\Gamma$ $K$-anti-self-adjoint) holds, and
hence $\chi=0$, in the \emph{physical indefinite} signatures $(9,5)$ and $(7,7)$ (those with timelike directions,
where the Krein form is genuinely indefinite). In Euclidean $(14,0)$ the grading instead \emph{commutes} with $K$
($\|\Gamma K-K\Gamma\|=0$): the form is grading-aligned and the triplet is fully chiral, $|\chi|=96$---a second
non-triviality control, and consistent with the Krein structure requiring $q>0$ (a non-compact internal group;
cf.\ \texttt{canon/ghost-parity-krein-synthesis.md}).

\begin{corollary}[necessary antilinear escape]

dichotomy~\cite{wigner1931gruppentheorie} the unique symmetry-respecting escape is \emph{antilinear} (CPT /
particle-hole; Altland--Zirnbauer class-CII type~\cite{altland1997nonstandard}), realized by a
re-grading $C = \Jq\cdot G$ with $C^2 = -I$ (Kramers/PHS; the antilinear part of $C$ has norm $\sim 85$, i.e. $C$
is genuinely antilinear, not a deviation from the stated $C^2=-I$). The antilinear symmetry data are present: the chiral
grading $G$ supplies the chiral symmetry and the quaternionic Kramers structure $\Jq$ supplies the antiunitary
particle-hole $C$ with $C^2 = -I$; the induced time reversal $T = G\,C$ completes the symmetry triple, with the

down, since the argument needs only that the escape is antilinear with $C^2=-I$, which holds in either case. This is corroborated independently by a
Seiberg--Witten moment-map degree argument: a Krein-isometric moment map is degree-$2$ homogeneous while $\ch_2$
is degree-$0$ and connection-independent, so a source action built from it is orthogonal to the topological index.
\end{corollary}

\section{The located order-3 carrier}\label{sec:carrier}

The order-$3$ content lives in exactly one place.\footnote{Throughout, ``order $3$'' names the $3$-primary
\emph{component} of the framed class, not the order of the class as a group element. The class is
$2\in\pis=\Z/24$, which has order $12$ as an element. Under the CRT splitting $\Z/24\cong\Z/8\oplus\Z/3$ it is
$(2\bmod 8,\,2\bmod 3)=(2,2)$: the $\Z/8$ component has order $4$, and the $\Z/3$ component $2\in\Z/3$ is the
nonzero element, of order $3$. The ``order-$3$ carrier'' is this $\Z/3$ component---the summand the $2$-primary
no-go is blind to, and the one $\Hom(\Z/3,\Z)=0$ concerns---never a claim that the element itself has order
$3$.} The self-dual $\SU(2)_+ = \Lamp$ structure, read as a
\emph{tangential} framing on $\RP^3 = L(2;1)$ (the deformation-retract spine of the metric fiber
$\mathrm{GL}(4,\R)/O(3,1)$), has framed-bordism class $\pm 2$ in $\pis = \Z/24$ and Adams $e$-invariant
\[
\eR = p_1/48 = (p_1/2)/24 = 1/12 \qquad (p_1 = 4~\text{for the adjoint charge-}1\text{ bundle};~\text{stable
framing degree }p_1/2 = 2;~\text{class }2~\text{in}~\Z/24;~\text{the }3\text{-primary part of the class is
nonzero, order }3).
\]
\paragraph{Normalization conventions (fixed once, to remove generator/sign ambiguity).}
The value $\eR=1/12$ depends on exactly three conventions, which we state explicitly; given them it is rigid.
\emph{(i) Generator of $H^4(B\mathrm{Spin};\Z)$.} $H^4(B\mathrm{Spin}(n);\Z)\cong\Z$ is generated by the spin
characteristic class $\tfrac12 p_1$, so $p_1=2\cdot(\text{generator})$ (McLaughlin~\cite{mclaughlin1992};
restated by Sati--Shim~\cite{satishim2015}). The adjoint charge-$1$ ($c_2=1$) bundle has $p_1=4$ (the Dynkin
index of the $\SU(2)$ adjoint), hence $\tfrac12 p_1=2$.
\emph{(ii) Stabilization is $\times 2$.} $\pi_3(\SO(3))\cong\Z$ and $\pi_3(\SO)\cong\Z$, and the map
$\pi_3(\SO(3))\to\pi_3(\SO)$ induced by $\SO(3)\hookrightarrow\SO$ is multiplication by $2$ (standard; the
Pontryagin / framing normalization is Kirby--Melvin~\cite{kirbymelvin1999}). The charge-$1$ adjoint bundle is
the generator of $\pi_3(\SO(3))$ (via $\SU(2)=\Spin(3)\to\SO(3)$, an isomorphism on $\pi_3$); under the
isomorphism $p_1/2\colon\pi_3(\SO)\xrightarrow{\sim}\Z$ its \emph{stable framing degree} is $p_1/2=2$, not the
unstable Dynkin index $4$.
\emph{(iii) Generator of $\pis$.} $\pis\cong\Z/24=\Imm J_3$, with the real Adams $e$-invariant
$\eR\colon\pis\to\mathbb{Q}/\Z$ normalized so the generator $\nu=J(\text{generator of }\pi_3(\SO))$ has
$\eR(\nu)=1/24$ (Adams~\cite{adams1966jx4}); then $\eR$ is injective and $\eR(m\,\nu)=m/24$. Composing, the
stable framing degree $2\in\pi_3(\SO)$ maps under $J$ to the class $2\in\Z/24$, so
$\eR = (p_1/2)/24 = p_1/48 = 2/24 = 1/12$. The composite $\eR=(p_1/2)/24$ is ours, not a formula quoted verbatim
from either source. Signs are immaterial to every conclusion: replacing $\nu$ by $-\nu$ sends the class
$2\mapsto 22\equiv-2$, whose $3$-primary reduction $-2\equiv 1\pmod 3$ is again nonzero of order $3$. These are
the paper's stated conventions throughout; only the identification of GU's $\Lamp$ twist with this natural
tangential framing is reconstruction-grade.

\paragraph{It locates but does not fill (computed).} The carrier $\Lamp$ has net self-dual tangent-frame charge
$33.94$ (tangential, $p_1=4$)---it is genuinely on the carrier-arena side---\emph{but it is vectorlike} (net
chiral $0$), and $\eR = 1/12$ is \emph{homotopy-fixed}: it is a fact about $\pis$, identical for a universe with
one generation or five. So the carrier marks the order-$3$ slot; it does not co-vary with the answer and so
cannot, by itself, be the thing that counts to three. The negative control confirms the slot is unique: $\RP^3$'s
own deck group is $\Z/2$, and the charge-$q$ Dirac $\eta = (2q^2-4q+1)/8$ is $2$-primary for every integer $q$,

We emphasize the resulting picture, established by computation: the \emph{chiralizer} (the only count-forcing
operator; a frame-active linear operator can produce a net count but conserves the index and forces nothing) is
frame-trivial and lives in the selector arena; the \emph{carrier} (the only order-$3$ object) is frame-charged

\section{What is not claimed: the open conjecture}\label{sec:open}

We do \emph{not} claim three chiral generations. The honest verdict is \textbf{located, not forced.} The single
open bridge is
\[
\text{order-}3\text{ class} \longrightarrow \text{integer }3.
\]
A nonzero class in $\Z/3$ detects information mod $3$; it is not equality to the integer $3$, and the
$e$-invariant carrying it ($\eR=1/12$) is identical for any generation count. APS, Dai--Freed~\cite{dai1994eta},
Callan--Harvey~\cite{callan1985anomalies}, and Bismut--Cheeger~\cite{bismut1989eta} relate a boundary
$\eta$/$e$-invariant to indices, determinant lines, anomaly phases, and
cobordism classes---never directly to an integer family count. So the identification is, in the present
literature, a theorem of nothing, and a candidate \textbf{category error}. This is now algebraically explicit:
there is no canonical class-to-count map at all, since $\Hom(\Z/3,\Z) = 0$ (every homomorphism from a torsion
group to a torsion-free group vanishes). An integer count, if one exists, therefore cannot \emph{be} the absolute
torsion class; it can only arise from a \textbf{relative, equivariant, or rank} invariant---integer-valued by
construction yet geometry-dependent---which is exactly what the unbuilt twisted Rarita--Schwinger index is. The
conjecture is thus better stated as: does that relative index exist on GU's $14$-manifold and reduce mod $3$ to
the located carrier? Until then the result is a no-go that \emph{locates}, and the evidence tilts toward one.

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

We have isolated, in an explicit Clifford Rarita--Schwinger sector, a clean structural picture. The
generation/chirality no-go is built from $2$-primary statements and is therefore arithmetically incapable of
constraining an odd count; the relevant homotopy group splits, by the Chinese Remainder Theorem, into a
$2$-primary selector arena and a disjoint odd-torsion carrier arena; every obstruction and every chiralizer lives
in the selector arena, while a homotopy count can live only in the carrier arena. We exhibited the unique
order-$3$ object in the carrier arena and showed it is tangential but vectorlike and homotopy-fixed---it locates
the slot, it does not fill it---and that the only count-forcing operator is frame-trivial and couples to the
selector arena. The one calculation that could yield an integer on GU's actual geometry is gated on an unbuilt
source action; the only honest computable integer is one.

We submit the two-primary blindness theorem, the CRT two-arena structure, the index-conservation /
antilinear-escape theorem, the located order-$3$ carrier, and---reopened GU-independently---the class-level
structural no-go that no covariant operator found interior to a Clifford--Rarita--Schwinger sector of this type
forces an odd chiral count, as a referee-proof, GU-independent contribution: a no-go that locates, and relocates
the count to external background data. On present evidence the generation count is external, supplied by a
net-self-dual chiral background of the general kind that produces chirality in known chiral theories; the
$\text{order-}3\text{ class}\to\text{integer }3$ identification of that external datum remains the single named
open conjecture (a candidate category error) rather than a claim. We do not claim three; the evidence tilts
toward one, and the structural reason the count is not internal is now computationally established for the whole
class (modulo an open exhaustive non-existence proof on the antilinear side). We request external review of both
the core results and the open bridge.

computes hypercharge and electric charge from group theory alone via
\[
Y = T_{3R} + \tfrac12(B{-}L), \qquad Q = T_{3L} + Y, \qquad n = 6Y .
\]
Pushing all $16$ weights through the branching
\[
\Spin(7,7)\to\Spin(1,3)\times\Spin(6,4)\to\Spin(1,3)\times\Spin(6)\times\Spin(4)
\cong \mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{C})\times\SU(4)\times\SU(2)\times\SU(2)
\to \mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{C})\times\SU(3)\times\SU(2)\times U(1)
\]
collapses the $\mathbf{16}$ to exactly one Standard-Model generation: the multiplets $(\mathbf{3},2)_{n=+1}$,
$(\overline{\mathbf{3}},1)_{n=+2}$, $(\overline{\mathbf{3}},1)_{n=-4}$, $(\mathbf{1},2)_{n=-3}$,
$(\mathbf{1},1)_{n=+6}$, $(\mathbf{1},1)_{n=0}$, totalling $6+3+3+2+1+1=16$ states, with
$\mathrm{Tr}\,Y=\mathrm{Tr}\,Q=0$ (anomaly-free) and electric charges $\{0,\pm\tfrac13,\pm\tfrac23,\pm1\}$. The
integer label $n=6Y$ reproduces the GU draft's Section~11.3 table exactly; an embedding-ambiguity probe confirms
that the naive $B{-}L$-only hypercharge fails, so the standard $Y=T_{3R}+\tfrac12(B{-}L)$ embedding is forced. An
independent explicit-Clifford cross-check (\texttt{lab/active-research/verify\_clifford\_explicit.py}, with
$32\times32$ gamma matrices satisfying the $\SO(10)$ Clifford relations numerically) reproduces the same content
and its exact CP conjugate ($n\to-n$, $\mathbf{3}\leftrightarrow\overline{\mathbf{3}}$).

\paragraph{Scope: this is a one-generation \emph{structural} verification, not a generation count.} What is
verified is that the $\mathbf{16}$ of $\Spin(10)$ carries exactly \emph{one} anomaly-free Standard-Model
generation, with the paper's hypercharge assignments reproduced from first principles---i.e.\ internal
representation-theoretic consistency of GU's group theory. It does \emph{not} provide the generation count: it
does not establish that nature realizes this breaking pattern, does not address the effective-chirality or
``$2+1$'' claims, and---being a single generation---says nothing about \emph{how many} generations occur. It is
the integer ``$1$'' of Section~\ref{sec:decider}, and its role in this paper is precisely that: the only honest
computable generation integer, not a derivation of three.
