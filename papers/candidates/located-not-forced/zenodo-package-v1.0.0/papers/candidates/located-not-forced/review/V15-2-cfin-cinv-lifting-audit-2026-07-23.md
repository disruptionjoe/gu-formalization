# V15-2 — `C_fin` / `C_inv` lifting audit

## Verdict

`C_fin` is the exact theorem domain. It is the fifteen-constructor finite type
encoded by `ClassCGenerator` in
`Lean/GUFormalization/LocatedNotForcedFiniteCore.lean`, together with finite
expressions generated by product, gcd, and lcm. Lean proves enumeration and
2-primary closure for this type. The computed carrier supplies the
`2/2/2/2/0` Hom-space dimensions used by the encoding; Lean does not derive
those dimensions from the Clifford matrices.

`C_inv` is the broader semantic target suggested by the manuscript's prose:
all linear or antilinear structures on the fixed carrier that are equivariant
under the stated split symmetry and use only sector-internal data. The bounded
lift closes the map from the computed C1--C5 Hom-space families to `C_fin`.
It does not close C6 as a classification of every characteristic construction,
nor does it classify non-equivariant, function-space/Fredholm, physical
Lorentzian-bundle, or true-`Y14` source-action structures.

The surviving theorem is therefore exact and non-vacuous:

> Every expression in `C_fin` has the typed conclusion proved by the finite
> core. For `C_inv`, the same conclusion is conditional on a carrier-faithful
> lift into `C_fin`; that lift is computed for C1--C5 and remains open for the
> unrestricted C6/analytic remainder.

No title-level or central-verdict change is triggered.

## Domain definitions

| Domain | Membership rule | Evidence grade | Certified conclusion |
|---|---|---|---|
| `C_fin` | One of the fifteen constructors in the encoded finite generator type, or a finite product/gcd/lcm expression over them | Lean theorem from explicitly supplied finite data | finite torsion stays 2-primary; integer equalities/divisibilities stay integer-typed; no encoded row supplies a mod-3 congruence or selects an odd integer |
| `C_inv` | Any sector-internal linear/antilinear invariant structure on the fixed carrier under the stated split symmetry | computed/conditional | only the portion with a proved or computed lift to `C_fin` inherits the theorem |
| outside both | external backgrounds, added representations or spurions, non-equivariant operators, Fredholm/APS/family-index constructions, or true-`Y14` source action | open or separate controls | no conclusion from Theorem 1 |

The constructor count is `2+2+2+2+0+7=15`. The exact finite theorem must not
silently expand C6 from “the seven named rows” to “all characteristic classes
from the sector's own bundles.”

## Bounded lifting evidence

The primary computation is
`tests/enum-completeness/enum_class_c_generators.py`; its independent signature
and method cross-check is
`tests/enum-completeness/verify/indep_check.py`. The finite deduction is
`Lean/GUFormalization/LocatedNotForcedFiniteCore.lean`, with the paper-facing
smoke certificate in `tests/located-not-forced/H2_FiniteCore.lean`.

The computation establishes a basis census for the explicit carrier and maps
each computed family to the finite constructors. This is enough for a bounded
C1--C5 lift. It is not a symbolic proof that every object satisfying the
semantic prose normalizes to that basis independently of the computed carrier
premises.

## Failed or open lifts

1. **C6 catch-all:** the seven encoded rows are certified; the phrase “all
   characteristic classes from sector data” is not.
2. **Physical-signature transfer:** a compact/complexified or split-carrier
   computation does not itself identify the physical Lorentzian bundle and its
   real/antilinear structures.
3. **Analytic lift:** finite matrices do not classify section-space,
   Fredholm, APS, family-index, or source-action operators.
4. **True-`Y14` lift:** no global integer-by-construction generation
   observable has been built.

These are explicit scope exits, not counterexamples to the `C_fin` theorem.
