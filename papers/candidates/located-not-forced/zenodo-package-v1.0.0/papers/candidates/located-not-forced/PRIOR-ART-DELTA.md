# Located, Not Forced: Prior-Art and Novelty Delta

## H6 verdict

The literature supports a narrow candidate delta, not a broad novelty claim.
Topology-, anomaly-, and algebra-based constraints on the number of fermion
families are established prior art. So are the stable group
\(\pi_3^S\cong\mathbb Z/24\), the extraction of a factor of three from
\(24=8\cdot3\), and—as of Wan–Wang–Yau v2—an explicit separation of the
2-primary and 3-primary anomaly data.

The search-bounded candidate delta of *Located, Not Forced* (LNF) is only the
following assembly:

1. an explicit cross-chirality Clifford–Rarita–Schwinger carrier;
2. a scoped sector-interior obstruction/selector result whose arithmetic
   content is 2-primary; and
3. the inverse conclusion that this sector-interior result cannot determine a
   separate 3-primary torsion component.

That conclusion locates a possible \(\mathbb Z/3\) carrier. It does not produce
an integer-valued generation index, force the value three, or construct the
true-\(Y^{14}\) source action.

## Comparison layers

| Layer | Question | Objects that live here |
|---|---|---|
| L0 — foundational topology | What are the stable group, framing normalization, and characteristic-class normalization? | \(\pi_3^S\), image of \(J\), the Adams \(e\)-invariant, stable framing change, \(\tfrac12p_1\) |
| L1 — algebraic carrier | How are family-like copies represented before dynamics or anomaly constraints? | Clifford/vector-spinor modules, triality modules, finite generation spaces |
| L2 — four-dimensional boundary or spectrum | What restrictions follow from the 4d fermion spectrum and its ordinary, global, or discrete anomalies? | Standard Model/GUT spectra, baryon triality, discrete \(R\)-symmetry, Dai–Freed anomaly data |
| L3 — full spacetime, bulk, or TQFT | What follows after adding higher-dimensional chirality, a bulk phase, compactification, or an anomaly-canceling TQFT? | 6d global anomaly, 5d topological-insulator bulk, dimensional reduction, 4d anomalous TQFT |
| L4 — LNF sector interior | What can a delimited class of operators do on the reconstructed Clifford–Rarita–Schwinger sector with its indefinite pairing? | cross-chirality Krein carrier, class-C enumeration, 2-primary obstruction/selector data |
| L5 — true GU geometry and integer index | Does the actual \(Y^{14}\) geometry, source action, or a relative/equivariant construction provide an integer family count? | the not-yet-built source operator/action and its index home |

The layers are not interchangeable. In particular, an indefinite Krein
pairing at L4 is not a positive-Hilbert/unitarity construction, and a torsion
class at L0 is not an integer index at L5.

## Foundational inputs, not family-number novelty

LNF also depends on primary results that should be treated as inputs rather than
novel contributions:

- J. F. Adams, [“On the groups \(J(X)\). IV”](https://doi.org/10.1016/0040-9383(66)90004-8),
  with [the 1968 correction](https://doi.org/10.1016/0040-9383(68)90010-4),
  for the image-of-\(J\) and \(e\)-invariant normalization.
- R. Kirby and P. Melvin,
  [“Canonical framings for 3-manifolds”](https://arxiv.org/abs/math/9903056),
  for stable framing normalization.
- D. A. McLaughlin,
  [“Orientation and string structures on loop space”](https://msp.org/pjm/1992/155-1/pjm-v155-n1-p08-s.pdf),
  and H. Sati–H.-b. Shim,
  [“String structures associated to indefinite Lie groups”](https://arxiv.org/abs/1504.02088),
  for the \(\tfrac12p_1\) normalization used in the reconstruction.

These inputs sharpen an important type distinction. Under
\[
\mathbb Z/24\longrightarrow \mathbb Z/8\oplus\mathbb Z/3,
\qquad
2\longmapsto(2,2),
\]
the full class \(2\in\mathbb Z/24\) has order \(12\), and its Adams value
\(1/12\in\mathbb Q/\mathbb Z\) also has order \(12\). The projected 3-primary
component is \((0,2)\), equivalently \(8\in\mathbb Z/24\), and has order \(3\).
Thus the program-native candidate is a torsion \(\mathbb Z/3\) carrier, not an
integer generation count.

## What LNF may add

Subject to the scope and risks below, the defensible candidate contribution is:

- the explicit L1 Clifford–Rarita–Schwinger construction, including the
  gamma-traceless triplet and its reconstructed cross-chirality
  \((+96,-96)\) indefinite pairing;
- the application of a classical Krein-space balance fact to that particular
  carrier;
- the delimited L4 class-C enumeration and accompanying 2-primary
  obstruction/selector result, with the unrestricted analytic/function-space
  residual left open;
- the carrier-specific inverse inference: those L4 data cannot determine the
  separate 3-primary torsion projection; and
- a precise statement of the remaining L5 obligation—a relative, equivariant,
  or rank-by-construction integer index on the true GU geometry.

The strongest safe novelty sentence is:

> To the best of the bounded primary-source search recorded in the H6 review,
> no located source combines the explicit cross-chirality
> Clifford–Rarita–Schwinger carrier, a scoped sector-interior 2-primary
> obstruction/selector result, and the inverse conclusion that those data are
> blind to a separate 3-primary torsion component. Primary decomposition and
> positive uses of its 3-primary content are prior art; the candidate delta is
> only this carrier-specific inverse assembly.

This is a search-bounded candidate novelty statement, not proof of publication
novelty.

## What LNF does not add

LNF does **not** add or establish:

- the general idea that topology, anomalies, or algebra can constrain fermion
  family multiplicity;
- \(\pi_3^S\cong\mathbb Z/24\), the factorization \(24=8\cdot3\), or the
  separation of 2-primary from 3-primary data;
- a new proof of \(N_{\mathrm{gen}}\in3\mathbb Z\) or a proof of
  \(N_{\mathrm{gen}}=3\);
- an integerization of the torsion carrier—indeed
  \(\operatorname{Hom}(\mathbb Z/3,\mathbb Z)=0\);
- a universal no-go over all operators, bundles, boundary conditions, or the
  full function-space theory;
- novelty for the elementary Krein graph/balance lemma or the standard
  vectorlike character of real or pseudoreal representations;
- a positive-Hilbert, unitary, or physical-state construction: the reconstructed
  L4 structure is indefinite Krein structure;
- the true-\(Y^{14}\) source action, a genuine GU family-index operator, or its
  relative/equivariant index home; or
- independent external replication of the LNF computations.

## Unresolved novelty risks

1. **Wan–Wang–Yau v2 narrows the arithmetic delta.** The revision dated
   2026-07-21 explicitly separates 2-primary and 3-primary anomaly parts and
   combines extensions using the Chinese remainder theorem. Any LNF wording
   that calls the primary split itself novel is untenable.
2. **The inverse-blindness step may be mathematically immediate.** Once the
   primary decomposition and the L4 scope are granted, blindness across
   distinct primary summands is elementary. Its plausible novelty lies only in
   the specific carrier/no-go assembly, not in a standalone theorem.
3. **The negative search is bounded.** The verification covered the named
   papers, their directly load-bearing neighbors, and focused arXiv searches.
   It did not exhaust non-arXiv literature, all citation graphs, theses, or
   alternative terminology.
4. **The reconstruction chain remains composite.** No single primary source
   proves the whole GU-specific passage from the reconstructed tangential
   object to the projected 3-primary carrier. The underlying normalizations are
   primary-sourced; the GU identification remains reconstruction-grade.
5. **The integer bridge is absent.** Without the true-\(Y^{14}\) source
   action/operator and an integer-by-construction index, the torsion carrier
   cannot be promoted to “three generations.”
6. **The no-go scope is delimited.** The class-C and finite-dimensional
   statements do not close the unrestricted analytic/function-space residual.
7. **Replication remains internal.** The computational carrier and enumeration
   have not been independently reproduced outside the project.
