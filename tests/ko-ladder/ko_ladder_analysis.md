## 0. The claim, in one line

Each obstruction in the generation-sector no-go is assigned a KO / Spin / Pin **type** and
**degree**. Five of seven land in a **finite 2-group / power of 2** *by the structure of that
type* (a cited Bott / Kirby-Taylor / ABP / ABS fact) — so they cannot carry odd torsion. The
remaining two are **integer (free, `Z`-valued)** indices: `Z` carries no torsion at all, so no
odd torsion, but their blindness to an odd *count* is contingent on the torsion-count reading,
not on a 2-group fact — graded **BLOCKED**. In `pi_3^s = Z/24 = Z/8 (+) Z/3`, every rung's
detectable class lies in the 2-Sylow `Z/8`; the order-3 count carrier lies in the CRT-disjoint
`Z/3`, reachable only via the odd part of `Im(J)` that KO/Spin/Pin invariants are constitutionally
blind to.

## 3. Count-disjointness (the exact statement)

> In `pi_3^s = Z/24 = Z/8 (+) Z/3`, the subgroup generated by all obstruction-detectable classes
> is contained in the 2-Sylow `H2 = <3>` (order 8). The generation count carrier is the order-3
> element `c = 8 = (0,2)`, which lies in the CRT-complementary 3-Sylow `H3 = <8> = {0,8,16}`. Since
> `H2 ∩ H3 = {0}` (coprime orders, CRT), **`c` is not in the subgroup generated by any obstruction**;
> equivalently every 2-primary rung, being a hom into a 2-group, annihilates `c`. The order-3
> class is reachable **only** inside `H3`, the odd part of `Im(J)`, which KO/Spin/Pin invariants
> are constitutionally blind to because their torsion is 2-primary (Bott, Kirby-Taylor, ABP).

This is asserted three ways in the script: `c ∉ <obstructions>`; `<obstructions> ∩ Z/3 = {0}`;
and, per rung, `kills_order3_carrier(2^k)` via `gcd(3, 2^k) = 1`.

## 4. The theorem (weakest-defensible form)

**Theorem (structural 2-primary ladder).** Let each obstruction of the GU generation-sector no-go
be assigned its KO/Spin/Pin type and degree as in the table of §2. Then each obstruction is either

- **(i)** a finite-2-group / mod-`2^k` / power-of-2 invariant whose target group has trivial odd
  part — rungs **1, 2, 5, 6, 7** (five of seven), each landing in `Z/2`, `Z/16`, or `2^floor(m/2)`
  *by a cited structural fact* (Bott KO torsion; Kirby-Taylor `Omega^{Pin+}_4 = Z/16`; ABS Clifford
  module rank) — hence provably incapable of carrying odd torsion; or

- **(ii)** an integer (free, `Z`-valued) index/signature — rungs **3, 4** (two of seven): `Z`
  carries no torsion, hence no odd torsion, but its blindness to an odd *count* is contingent on
  the torsion-count reading rather than on a 2-group fact (a literal integer index pins every
  prime). Graded **BLOCKED**.

Consequently, in `pi_3^s = Z/24 = Z/8 (+) Z/3`, every rung's detectable class lies in the 2-Sylow
`Z/8`, while the order-3 count carrier lies in the CRT-disjoint `Z/3`; **no rung — proven or
blocked — can carry the order-3 class**, and the count is reachable only inside the odd part of
`Im(J)`, to which every KO/Spin/Pin invariant is constitutionally blind.

**Scope / what is NOT claimed.** This does not assert the count *is* 3, nor that it is realized;
it is a statement about the *reachability* of the odd-torsion sector by the program's own
obstructions. The `order-3-class -> integer-3` identification (see the CRT-RESULTS canon) remains
the single named open conjecture and a candidate category error; nothing here closes it. The two
BLOCKED rungs are the honest boundary: the mod-2 lemma (every obstruction even / mod-`2^k`) is
GU-independent and stands; the corollary's force on rungs 3–4 depends on the torsion-count reading.

## 6. The BLOCKED rungs (explicit, honest)

**Rung 3 — cross-chirality Krein signature `(+96,-96)`.** The signature of the `so(p,q)`-invariant
Krein form is `Z`-valued (free part of `L^0 ~ KO^0`); the `+96/-96` split gives net signature 0.
`96 = 2^5 * 3` has 2-part 32, and the *even* 96/96 split is a genuine `Z/2` parity — but "net 0" is
an integer statement, and under a literal integer reading it pins every prime (hardening audit).
Free means no torsion at all, so its blindness to `Z/3` is not a 2-primary-torsion fact but a
free-part/interpretive one — **contingent on the torsion-count reading**. *Upgrade path:* reducing
the signature mod 8 lands in a genuine 2-group via `Omega^{Pin-}_2 = Z/8` (VERIFIED), which would
make it 2-primary-by-type — but `(+96,-96)` is a representation Krein form, not a `Pin^-` surface,
so that reduction is **not licensed** here. Left BLOCKED, not fudged.

**Rung 4 — adjoint Dirac index `4k / 12k / 24k`.** Integer index (free `K^0`). The divisibility by
`4 = 2^2` is a real 2-primary divisibility (`2 * T(adjoint)`). In `24 = 8 * 3` the **8** is the
2-part a spin-bordism refinement pins; the **3** is a Dynkin-index / multiplicity **multiplicand**
of an integer, *never a mod-3 congruence* on the count — this is the precise, un-fudged reading of
"`24 = 8 * 3`". A `Z`-valued index kills *all* of `pi_3^s` torsion (both `Z/8` and `Z/3`), so it
detects no torsion class; a literal integer index would pin an integer count. Odd-blindness is
therefore **contingent** (hardening audit), not a 2-group fact. Left BLOCKED.

Both BLOCKED rungs are blocked by the **interpretive contingency of the integer-index reading**,
not by any missing or unverified citation. That is the difference between the GU-independent mod-2
*lemma* (which stands) and the *corollary*'s force on the integer-valued items (which rides the
reconstruction-grade torsion-count premise).
