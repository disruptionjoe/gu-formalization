# Persona 09: Mathematical Physicist / Formal Axiomatist

## (a) Which question

Primary: the central meta-question. What would a *proof* of Geometric Unity (GU) look like, and what rigorous standard separates "construction exists" from "well-defined theory" from "reproduces known physics in limits"? Questions 1 to 4 are evaluated against that standard.

## (b) Rigorous-standard read: what GU must satisfy

A program that claims to unify gravity and gauge theory on a 14-dimensional observerse over a 4-manifold is, mathematically, a claim about either (i) a classical field theory (variational principle on a fiber bundle with well-posed Cauchy problem and a Lorentzian symmetric-hyperbolic system), or (ii) a quantum field theory. Each tier has non-negotiable axioms.

Classical tier: existence of a Lagrangian density of bounded-below energy (or at minimum a hyperbolic system admitting a well-posed initial value problem on a globally hyperbolic 4-slice), diffeomorphism covariance, and a consistent reduction to a 4-dimensional effective theory via a rigorous Kaluza-Klein or dimensional-reduction theorem.

Quantum tier: either Wightman / Osterwalder-Schrader axioms (locality, Lorentz covariance, spectrum condition, unique vacuum, cluster), or Haag-Kastler / locally covariant QFT (Brunetti-Fredenhagen-Verch: a functor from globally hyperbolic spacetimes to C*-algebras satisfying isotony, locality, time-slice, covariance), or Atiyah-Segal TQFT axioms if topological. Add: anomaly cancellation (Wess-Zumino consistency), CPT theorem compatibility, spin-statistics, unitarity, and at least perturbative renormalizability or a non-perturbative construction (constructive QFT in the Glimm-Jaffe sense).

A "proof of GU" would be: a theorem of the form "there exists a quadruple (M, P, S, A) with M a 4-manifold, P the 14-dim observerse bundle, S an action functional, A an algebra of observables, such that the axioms above hold and the effective theory at scale below E_0 is isomorphic to the Standard Model coupled to general relativity."

## (c) What fails or is forced under those standards

Question 1 (4 to 14 makes sense?): At the *classical Lagrangian* level, yes, a fiber bundle with 10-dim fiber over a 4-manifold is a well-defined geometric object. But "mathematical theory" requires more: a well-posed variational problem, gauge-fixing, and a reduction theorem. None of these are automatic. [speculation] The 14-dim total space likely carries a degenerate or signature-mixed metric, which already endangers hyperbolicity and Osterwalder-Schrader positivity.

Question 2 (SM gauge group emerges?): Rigorously, "emergence" means a holonomy reduction theorem plus a symmetry-breaking mechanism whose vacuum manifold has the right isotropy group. At Lagrangian level this is a conjecture, not a theorem, absent an explicit potential.

Question 3 (fermions from geometry?): Spin-statistics (Streater-Wightman) forces half-integer spin = anticommuting fields in any Wightman QFT. Geometry alone produces sections of associated bundles, which are c-number valued. Promoting them to fermions requires either super-geometry from the start or a quantization prescription. There is no theorem that says "geometric sections automatically anticommute." This is a hard obstruction unless GU is super-geometric.

Question 4 (GR + QFT in limits?): Requires a rigorous low-energy effective field theory limit. Even perturbative quantum gravity is non-renormalizable (t'Hooft-Veltman 1974 at one loop with matter); GU inherits this unless it is UV-completed. No known mechanism in pure geometry provides UV completion.

## (d) Obstructions

Renormalizability of any 14-dim gravity-like theory: power-counting non-renormalizable. Anomaly cancellation: requires explicit fermion content and representation theory, not derivable from bundle geometry alone. Existence theorems: constructive QFT exists rigorously only in d <= 3 (phi^4_3, Yang-Mills_2,3 partial); d = 4 Yang-Mills is a Millennium Problem. A 14-dim construction is far beyond current rigorous reach. Coleman-Mandula and Haag-Lopuszanski-Sohnius constrain how internal and spacetime symmetries can combine: only supersymmetry escapes. If GU mixes them non-trivially without SUSY, it violates these no-go theorems.

## (e) Verdict

Not a mathematical theory in any rigorous sense currently. It is, at best, a *proposal for a classical geometric setup*. To become a theory it must: (1) specify a well-posed Lagrangian, (2) prove a dimensional-reduction theorem yielding SM + GR, (3) provide fermion structure compatible with spin-statistics (likely requiring super-geometry), (4) demonstrate anomaly cancellation, (5) address UV completion. Path forward: treat GU as a classical-geometry conjecture and demand the dimensional-reduction theorem as the first falsifiable deliverable; without it, the program is not yet in the category of objects mathematical physics evaluates.
